CHRONICLE

The recorded history of a world that ended. What remains are fragments, transmissions, and the testimony of those who survived.

YEAR 0

The Final Transmission

"This is Dr. Nikolas Theslar, Head of Resonance Studies at the Sibarian Institute. If you're hearing this, the test has failed. The Engine was supposed to eliminate scarcity. Unlimited energy. Post-death consciousness transfer. Immortality for all. Instead, it's... singing. God help us. It's singing, and I can't stop listening."

The last words from the Sibarian Institute before the Sundering. The Resonance Engine activated at 14:22 GMT. By 14:23, the world had ended.

YEAR 3

The First Casket

Recorded testimony from Survivor Camp 7, Northern Wastes:

"We found Marissa three days after she died. Frost took her in the night. But when we went to bury her, she was gone. We heard something walking in the snow. Heavy. Mechanical. It was her, but not her. She'd built herself a body out of scrap metal and frozen corpses. She didn't speak. Just pointed north and started walking."

The first documented case of post-mortem consciousness persistence. The phenomenon was named "Remnant Animation." Later, it would simply be called "The Curse."

YEAR 47

The Church Manifesto

From the Church of Absolution's founding document:

"We did not die because we were unworthy. We died because the world demanded sacrifice. Now, bound to these iron shells, we are given a second chance. Not to live, but to atone. Every enemy felled is a sin purged. Every mission completed is a step toward salvation. We will earn our rest through violence, or we will walk this broken world forever."

The Church's ideology spread rapidly among the Remnants. Within a generation, they controlled three major city-states and declared Absolution their divine mandate.

YEAR 89

The Taint Plague

Medical log from Dr. Helena Vask, Fortuna Settlement:

"Exposure to Abominations is changing the Remnants. It starts with discoloration around the wound sites. Then the hallucinations. They report hearing the Engine's song, even hundreds of miles from Sibaria. Stage 3 Taint victims exhibit reality distortion fields. Stage 5... they become something else. We have no cure. We can only quarantine and pray they choose to self-terminate before transformation."

YEAR 134

The Great Schism

From the archives of Bonelord Thresh:

"The Church split today. Not over theology, but methodology. Cardinal Vross proclaimed that only human Remnants deserve salvation. Archbishop Kael argued that all who suffer can be redeemed. The vote was close. Vross won. By morning, half the Church had seceded, forming the Covenant of Broken Chains. They welcomed the elves, the mutants, the forgotten. The Church Militant declared them heretics. The civil war lasted 80 years."

YEAR 197

The Forge-Titan Awakens

From the Dwarven Assembly records:

"We found it in the Deep Quarry, 900 meters down. A construct from before the Sundering. Pre-Casket war machine, massive beyond comprehension. 40 meters tall, fusion core still active after two centuries. Forgemaster Durr insisted we could control it. He was wrong. It activated, killed 60 miners, then went dormant again. Durr claimed it spoke to him. Said it was 'waiting for the right war.' We sealed the quarry. Durr still goes down there. Alone. He's been going for 240 years."

YEAR 223

The Betrayal at Roothold

From the testimony of Warden Kess at the Greenfire Council:

"The Church came with promises. Protection from the Rot-Screamers. Medicine for the blight. All they asked was passage through Thornveil. We refused. Three days later, they burned half the forest. The trees screamed. I watched them die and felt nothing. That's when I knew: I am as cursed as they are. We all are."

The Church-Elf war began that winter. 214 years later, it has not ended.

YEAR 271

The Merchant Ascendancy

Trade manifest from Guild-Master Vex Sarros:

"The other factions fight over ideology. We fight for profit. Today, the Seven Guilds signed the Concordat of Steel. We will sell to all sides. We will enrich all conflicts. We will ensure the wars never end, because peace is unprofitable. Let them call us vultures. Vultures survive."

Within 50 years, the Merchant Guilds controlled 60% of Soulstone trade and every major forge-city.

YEAR 298

The Screaming Choir

Audio log recovered from the Sibarian Wastes Expedition:

"We're within visual range of the Engine now. It's... singing. There are people inside it. Hundreds of them. Embedded in the walls. Still alive. Oh God. I recognize that voice. That's Nikolas Theslar. He's still in there. He's been in there for THREE HUNDRED YEARS. He's conducting them. He's conducting all of them."

No one from the expedition returned. Their Caskets were found three weeks later, wandering in circles, empty. One had carved a message into its own chassis: "HE REMEMBERS OUR NAMES."

YEAR 314

The Silent Massacre

From the Undead Court archives:

"The Fae do not negotiate. They do not declare war. They simply appear, and settlements die. Greymoor lost 300 Remnants in one night. No bodies. No scrap. Just empty Caskets arranged in a perfect circle. In the center, a single flower, still blooming. Bonelord Thresh investigated personally. He found no evidence of Fae involvement. The flower, however, whispered his true name."

YEAR 352

The Blighted Exodus

Testimony from Alpha Korran, Pack-Leader:

"We didn't ask to be born this way. Chimeric. Cursed. Half-human, half-beast. The Church hunts us. The Elves exile us. The Dwarves dissect us. So we left. All of us. Ten thousand Blighted, walking into the Wastes. Half died in the first month. The survivors adapted. Mutated further. Became something new. We are not human anymore. We are not Abominations. We are the Packs. And we will no longer beg for acceptance."

YEAR 381

The Sibarian Metamorphosis

Final transmission from Research Station Sigma-7:

"The researchers at ground zero... they didn't die. They changed. Fused with the arthropod specimens in the bio-labs. We thought they were Abominations. We were wrong. They're organized. Intelligent. Hive-minded. They call themselves the Chitinous Ascendancy. They say they've achieved perfect evolution. They say we should join them. The signal is so loud now. So loud. I can hear them inside my head. They're singing. Just like the Engine. I... I think I want to join them."

Sigma-7 went dark. Three months later, the first Ascendancy war-forms emerged from the Wastes.

YEAR 407

The Fracture Storms Begin

Scientific observation from Nomad outpost Zeta-9:

"The Engine's instability is increasing. Reality fractures now occur daily. Last storm brought creatures we have no names for. Some were humans who died centuries before the Sundering. Others were things that should not exist. The barriers between what is, what was, and what could have been are collapsing. Recommend immediate evacuation of all settlements within 500km of Sibaria."

The recommendation was ignored. There is nowhere left to evacuate to.

YEAR 437

Present Day

Four centuries after the Sundering, the Resonance Engine still sings. The dead still walk. Nine factions war across a shattered world, each convinced they alone hold the path to salvation. Generations of Remnants have lived and died in these iron shells, and still no absolution has come.

The Church promises redemption through violence. The Elves tend their dying forests. The Dwarves build ever more complex solutions to unsolvable problems. The Undead simply wait. The Fae laugh at mortal suffering. The Nomads survive. The Merchants profit. The Blighted Packs hunt. The Chitinous Ascendancy evolves.

None remember what peace felt like. None remember what it meant to be truly alive. There is only the weight of centuries, the grinding of iron against iron, and the faint hope that maybe, just maybe, the next kill will be the one that finally brings rest.

It won't be. It never is.

Welcome to Penance.

ICONIC PILOTS

Five Remnants who have transcended death to become legends. Each is broken. Each is dangerous. None are heroes.

SISTER VEX
The Hollow Penitent
Church of Absolution

Taint Level 7. Believes suffering purifies. Each wound she takes brings her closer to salvation. Each ally she sacrifices is a prayer answered. The Church calls her a saint. Everyone else calls her a fanatic. She calls herself damned.

Pilots "The Confessional" - a 1,600lb heavy Casket covered in scripture and scars.

ROOTWARDEN KESS
The Last Gardener
Elven Remnants

Died protecting a grove that burned anyway. Now tends a garden of corpse-trees that whisper secrets. Can grow thorned barriers from the dead. Hasn't spoken to another living being in 30 years. Doesn't consider that a loss.

Pilots "The Arbor Mortis" - a medium Casket woven from petrified wood and bone.

FORGEMASTER DURR
The Machine Prophet
Dwarven Clans

Talks to the Forge-Titan. It talks back. Builds Caskets that shouldn't work but do. His own Casket regenerates damage by consuming scrap metal. The Clans tolerate him because his weapons win wars. He tolerates them because the Titan isn't ready yet.

Pilots "The Crucible" - a heavy Casket that burns at 800 degrees internally. Durr doesn't notice.

BONELORD THRESH
The Collector
Undead Court

Death is a science. Thresh has been studying for 80 years. Collects the skulls of worthy opponents. Speaks to them. They answer. Commands an army of reanimated Caskets piloted by consciousnesses he's rebound to service. Polite, methodical, utterly merciless.

Pilots "The Ossuary" - a medium Casket built entirely from the bones of other Remnants.

MOCKINGBIRD
The Laughing Shade
Fae Courts

Died as a human. Woke as something else. The Fae claimed her because she amused them. Now she's the joke and the punchline. Steals faces, voices, memories. You'll never see her coming. You'll always know when she's been there. The laughter echoes for days.

Pilots "The Mummer's Waltz" - a light Casket that changes appearance. What it looked like yesterday is gone.

KEY MECHANICS

Deck-as-HP
30 cards = 30 HP. Discard cards when damaged. Reshuffle adds Damage cards (death spiral).
Component Destruction
3 Component Damage = Destroyed. Lose entire card type. Kingdom Death-style brutality.
SP Economy
Play multiple cards per turn. Safe Zone vs Danger Zone. Heat Strain checks at 5+ Heat.
Faction Asymmetry
Church: Self-harm for burst damage. Dwarves: Armor-piercing + Rune Counters. Completely different playstyles.
Facing Modifiers
Rear arc +2 damage. Positioning matters. Flank for advantage.
Reactive Cards
0 SP interrupts. Play during opponent's turn. Brace for Impact, Unbreakable.
