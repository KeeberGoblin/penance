The Manuscript of Penance

Welcome, seeker of the Bound. Herein lies the complete chronicle of Penance: a grimoire of iron, blood, and redemption. Study well these pages, for knowledge is the only armor against the darkness.

Playtest Ready: Version 2.0

The great work is complete. Four factions stand ready for battle, their Caskets armed and their souls bound. Take up arms and seek absolution.

Contents of this Grimoire:

  • Setup Time: 15 minutes of preparation
  • Battle Duration: 45-60 minutes of bloodshed
  • Four Complete Factions (Church, Dwarves, Ossuarium, Elves)
  • 24 Support Units with Behavior Decks
  • Variable Deck Sizes (26-50 cards, as fate allows)
  • 60+ Equipment Items (weapons, shields, sigils)
  • 16 Weapon Move Sets (89 cards of martial technique)

Genesis of the Project

The journey from concept to playable grimoire spanned two decades:

  • 2005 - Project Arena Mech conceptualized in a binder (mech combat, hex-based tactics)
  • 2016 - Discovered Kingdom Death: Monster - Added brutal progression, event tables, permanent consequences
  • 2018 - Backed GKR: Heavy Hitters on Kickstarter - Inspired by deck-as-HP and SP economy
  • 2018-2024 - Hiatus due to constraints in cohesive boardgame mechanics knowledge
  • October 2024 - With Claude AI assistance, transformed vague vision into complete playable system

Design Philosophy

Penance follows the brutal principles of Kingdom Death: Monster:

  • Brutal progression - Permanent consequences matter (component destruction, pilot death, scars)
  • Asymmetric factions - No two play the same (Church ≠ Dwarves ≠ Ossuarium ≠ Elves)
  • Mystery over answers - The lore raises more questions (What is the Resonance Engine?)
  • Meaningful scarcity - Resources are precious (Scrap economy, equipment slots)
  • No pure heroes - Every faction is morally complex (Church burns heretics, Ossuarium harvests souls)
  • Death spiral - Inevitable decline (Damage cards accumulate, component destruction is permanent)
  • Support Units - GKR-inspired AI companions with behavior decks (unpredictable allies)
