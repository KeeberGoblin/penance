CODEX / CAMPAIGN / MISSIONS / Ironlord Thrain

Mission: The Vault Breaker

Mission Type: Boss Encounter (Difficulty 5-6)
Recommended Pilots: 3-4 pilots with Grit 2+
Time Limit: Available anytime, but scales with campaign progress
Faction: Forge-Guilds (Rogue Forgemaster)
Reward: Unlock Rune Behemoth support unit + 500 Credits + Forge-Guild Reputation +3

"The Guilds waste runecraft on trivial labor. I see its true potential—perfection through iteration, immortality through engineering, domination through superior design. They call me rogue. I call myself the only one awake." -Ironlord Thrain, former Forgemaster, Year 154
✿

Mission Briefing

Contact: High Forgemaster Durak (Forge-Guilds)
Location: The Ironfast Vault, deep within the Northern Peaks

"Thrain was our finest Forgemaster. Mastery of runecraft at age 30. Invented three new anchoring techniques. We promoted him to Master Artificer by Year 140. Then he... changed.

He began hoarding ancient pre-Sundering schematics. He locked himself in the Ironfast Vault for 14 years, researching forbidden designs. When we demanded entry, he activated defenses that killed 12 of our best. He declared the Vault his sovereign territory and barricaded himself inside with 200 loyalists.

We've received reports of impossible constructs—self-repairing walls, autonomous war machines, runic weapons that shouldn't exist. Thrain isn't just hoarding knowledge. He's building an army.

Break the Vault. Kill Thrain. Recover the schematics before he perfects whatever nightmare he's designing."

✿

The Boss: Ironlord Thrain

Background

Forgemaster Thrain was a prodigy—youngest Artificer in Forge-Guild history. But brilliance bred obsession. He discovered pre-Sundering military schematics in a buried vault and became convinced the Guilds were wasting runecraft on construction when they should be perfecting war machines.

He seized the Ironfast Vault—a pre-Sundering weapons depot—and spent 14 years reverse-engineering ancient tech, blending it with dwarven runecraft. His Casket, the Ironclad Colossus, is a walking fortress: 12-foot-tall, covered in interlocking runic plates that self-repair, armed with a plasma hammer that melts armor.

Thrain believes the Forge-Guilds are soft. He dreams of a new dwarven empire built on military supremacy, ruled by Forgemasters like himself—engineers who understand that survival demands absolute strength.

Attribute Value

Defense 5 (runic plate armor, highest in game)
Deck Size 50 cards (25 HP deck threshold, extremely durable)
Special Traits Runic Regeneration, Siege Mode, Master Artificer

Boss Abilities

Passive: Runic Regeneration

At the start of Thrain's turn, heal 5 HP. This regeneration can be disabled for 2 turns by destroying one of the 3 Rune Pylons scattered around the battlefield (15 HP each).

Strategic Note: Destroying pylons is critical—otherwise he heals faster than you can damage him.

Passive: Reactive Plating

The first time each turn Thrain would take 10+ damage, reduce that damage by 5 (his armor absorbs the impact).

Action: Plasma Hammer Strike (2 SP)

Range: Melee
Effect: Deal 8 damage. Ignore 3 Defense. Target's Defense reduced by 1 permanently (armor melted).

Action: Siege Mode (3 SP, toggle)

Effect: Thrain roots in place (cannot move). Gain +2 Defense (now Defense 7) and ranged attack: Artillery Cannon (range 8, 6 damage, 2 SP). Can toggle off to resume movement.

Tactical Note: Forces long-range engagement. Use mobility to flank him while he's stationary.

Action: Deploy War Construct (4 SP, twice per battle)

Effect: Summon 1 Runic Golem (24 HP, Defense 3, 5 damage melee) adjacent to Thrain. Golem acts immediately and follows basic commands. Golems persist until destroyed.

Phase Transition: Overcharge Protocol (triggers at 40 HP)

Thrain overloads his Casket's Soulstone core. He gains:

  • Empowered Strikes: All attacks deal +3 damage
  • Unstable Core: Takes 3 automatic damage per turn (overheating)
  • Emergency Repairs: Can spend 5 SP to heal 15 HP (once only)
  • Siege Cannon Upgraded: Now deals 8 damage in 2-hex radius (splash damage)

Ultimate: Fortress Protocol (5 SP, below 25 HP only)

Thrain activates full lockdown. All Rune Pylons reactivate (even destroyed ones rebuild). Thrain gains +3 Defense (now Defense 8) and heals 10 HP per turn for 3 turns. He cannot move or attack during this time (pure defense).

Counter: Destroy all 3 pylons during Fortress Protocol to disable healing and drop Defense to 5.

Battlefield: The Ironfast Vault

Hex Map Layout (18x18 hexes)

Underground weapons vault converted into a fortress. Metal walls etched with glowing runes. Forges burning at perimeter. Arsenal of ancient weapons on racks.

Terrain Features

  • Central Forge (Center, 4x4 hexes): Thrain starts here. Elevated platform (+1 Defense). Contains 3 repair drones (heal Thrain 3 HP/turn each if not destroyed, 8 HP per drone).
  • Rune Pylons (3 locations, triangular formation): 15 HP each. Provide Thrain's regeneration. Glow bright blue. Destroying one disables regeneration for 2 turns; destroying all 3 permanently disables it.
  • Arsenal Racks (6 scattered): Can be looted (2 SP action) for temporary weapon: Runic Blade (one-use, 10 damage, ignores Defense).
  • Molten Channels (4 trenches): 1-hex-wide channels of molten metal. Deal 4 fire damage if crossed. Can be jumped (costs +1 SP).
  • Vault Doors (4 sealed exits): Heavy blast doors. Can be destroyed (20 HP each) to create escape routes or reinforcement entries.

Hazards

  • Automated Turrets (4): Wall-mounted, 12 HP each. Attack random pilot each turn (4 damage, range 6). Prioritize destroying these early.
  • Forge Vents: Every 4 turns, the vault vents superheated air. All units take 3 fire damage unless in cover.
  • Lockdown Alarm: If Thrain reaches 10 HP, vault seals all doors. No retreat possible—fight to the death.

Enemy Composition

Support Units

  • Movement: 3 hexes
  • Defense: 4 (heavily armored)
  • Attack: Runic Halberd (5 damage, 1 SP, melee)
  • Special: Shield Wall (2 SP) - Adjacent allies gain +2 Defense until Sentinel's next turn
  • Loyalty: Always position between Thrain and attackers

Artificer Engineers (x2) - 12 HP each

  • Defense: 2
  • Support Action: Emergency Repairs (2 SP) - Heal allied Casket or construct 8 HP
  • Attack: Plasma Torch (3 damage, 1 SP, range 2) - Deals +2 damage to constructs/machines

Runic Golems (x2, summoned by Thrain) - 24 HP each

  • Movement: 3 hexes
  • Defense: 3
  • Attack: Crushing Fists (5 damage, 1 SP, melee)
  • Passive: Runic Shell - First time taking 8+ damage, reduce it to 5
  • Berserk: Below 10 HP, gain +2 damage and +1 movement

Reinforcements

Backup Protocol (Turn 5): If Thrain is below 50 HP, 1 Vault Door opens and 3 Ironguard Sentinels arrive. They bring 1 Repair Drone (8 HP) that immediately starts healing Thrain.

Failsafe (Turn 8): All destroyed Automated Turrets reboot and reactivate.

Victory Conditions

Primary Objective

Kill Ironlord Thrain. Reduce him to 0 HP.

Secondary Objectives (Optional)

  • Disable Regeneration: Destroy all 3 Rune Pylons before killing Thrain (+100 Credits, Forge-Guild Reputation +1)
  • Golem Hunter: Destroy both summoned Runic Golems (+50 Credits)
  • Steal Schematics: Loot Thrain's workbench before he reaches 20 HP (unlocks bonus equipment blueprints, +150 Credits)

Failure Conditions

  • All pilots reduced to 0 HP (standard)
  • Vault self-destructs (if Thrain uses Fortress Protocol and you fail to disable pylons, he triggers self-destruct at 5 HP—everyone dies)

GM Guidance

Tactical Tips for Players

  • Destroy Pylons First: 5 HP/turn regeneration means he's unkillable without disabling it. Split the party—2 attack Thrain, 2 destroy pylons.
  • Kill Engineers Fast: They repair Thrain and reactivate drones. Prioritize them to stop healing chain.
  • Use Terrain: Lure Thrain into molten channels for free fire damage. Destroy turrets from behind cover.
  • Burst Damage Windows: When he enters Siege Mode, he's stationary—perfect for ranged bombardment.
  • Counter Fortress Protocol: Save AoE attacks to destroy all 3 pylons during his ultimate.

Narrative Moments

  • Opening: Thrain stands atop Central Forge as pilots breach the vault. "So the Guilds send assassins. Expected. Inefficient." He activates turrets and golems.
  • Phase 2 (40 HP): His Casket glows red-hot. "Core at 180% capacity. Acceptable risk." Plasma vents hiss steam.
  • Fortress Protocol (25 HP): "Activating total defense. You will exhaust yourselves against perfection." Runes blaze across his armor.
  • Near Death (10 HP): "Calculations... failed? Impossible. My designs were flawless..." Armor cracks, sparks fly.
  • Death: "The equation... was wrong..." His Casket collapses with a thunderous crash. Vault begins to destabilize.

Adjusting Difficulty

Easier (for 2 pilots or low Grit):

  • Reduce Thrain HP to 70, Defense to 4
  • Only 2 Rune Pylons (instead of 3)
  • Remove 2 Ironguard Sentinels
  • No Backup Protocol reinforcements
  • Regeneration heals 3 HP/turn (instead of 5)

Harder (for 4+ pilots or high Grit):

  • Increase Thrain HP to 90, Defense to 6
  • 4 Rune Pylons (triangular + center)
  • Add 2 more Automated Turrets (6 total)
  • Golems have 30 HP each
  • Fortress Protocol heals 15 HP/turn and grants Defense 10
  • Lockdown Alarm triggers at 20 HP (instead of 10 HP)

Story Integration

This mission works best when:

  • Forge-Guild Reputation is High: Durak trusts you with sensitive internal matter
  • Late Campaign: Thrain has had decades to perfect his designs (Year 20+)
  • Moral Complexity: Thrain isn't evil—he's a pragmatist who believes might makes right. Some pilots might agree.
  • Aftermath: Thrain's schematics are revolutionary but dangerous. Forge-Guilds debate whether to adopt or destroy them.
  • Loot Temptation: The vault contains pre-Sundering tech. Do pilots take what doesn't belong to them?
