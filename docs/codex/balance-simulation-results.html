Balance Simulation Results

Data-Driven Balance Guidelines

"Through 30,600 battles, the truth emerges: small changes echo loudly, and patience yields balance."

Executive Summary

Between October 15-19, 2025, we conducted extensive combat simulations testing faction balance across 17 rounds of iterative testing, comprising 30,600 total battles. The results provide empirical evidence for proven balance values and design principles.

Key Finding: Small incremental changes (±10%) produce stable balance. Large simultaneous changes (±25%+ or multiple factions) create catastrophic meta collapses.
✿

Damage Values Win Rate Status Tests
8/10/12 73-82% ❌ BROKEN Rounds 10, 15, 17

Implementation: Biomass cards must not exceed 7/9/11 damage progression. This value has been proven stable across multiple rounds. Any increase to 8/10/12 consistently produces 70%+ win rates.

Dwarven Forge-Guilds - Rune System

Mechanic Win Rate Status
Offensive (+1 damage per rune) 93.1% ❌ CATASTROPHIC
Defensive (-1 damage taken, max 3) 50-55% (est.) ✅ BALANCED

Design Principle: Runes are defensive stacking mechanics, NOT offensive. Offensive rune scaling creates exponential damage growth (Turn 5: +5 damage = 8 total damage per turn = unbeatable).

SP Cost Damage Win Rate Status
0 SP 4 81.1% ❌ BROKEN

Opportunity Cost: Lifesteal abilities must cost significant SP (3+) to create tactical decisions. Free or cheap lifesteal enables infinite grinding (70-110 turn battles observed).

Critical Design Lessons

Lesson 1: Never Buff Multiple Factions Simultaneously

Round 17 Disaster: Buffed 4 factions at once (Church, Dwarves, Ossuarium, Bloodlines). Result: Meta collapsed from 4 balanced factions → 1 balanced faction.

When multiple factions receive buffs simultaneously, an arms race occurs. Buffed factions crush unbuffed ones, creating cascading imbalance. Previously balanced factions (Nomads, Elves, Crucible) dropped from 45-50% WR to 27-41% WR.

Faction Round 16 WR Round 17 WR Change
Vestige Bloodlines 40.8% 73.6% +32.8%
The Ossuarium 39.2% 71.9% +32.7%
The Exchange 61.1% 27.5% -33.6%
Wyrd Conclave 61.4% 13.6% -47.8%

Correct Approach: Change ONE faction per patch. Test ripple effects across all matchups. Iterate incrementally.

Lesson 2: Small Incremental Changes

  • Large swings (±25% damage): Catastrophic imbalance
  • Small adjustments (±10% damage): Manageable tuning
  • Micro-buffs (adding utility): Safest approach

Example: Ossuarium damage increase 4 → 5 (+25%) resulted in 71.9% WR. Better approach: Keep 4 damage, add additional utility effect instead.

Lesson 3: Testing Protocol

When making balance changes:

  1. Change only ONE faction per patch
  2. Test minimum 20 runs per matchup (200+ battles total for 10 factions)
  3. Check for 100% or 0% matchups (indicates broken mechanics)
  4. Target 45-55% WR for all factions (50% ± 5%)
  5. Monitor average combat length (15-30 turns optimal, not 100+ turns)
  6. If catastrophic (60%+ or 40%- WR), REVERT immediately
