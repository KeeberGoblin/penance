Overview: Speciation Through Isolation

Before the Theslar Event, humanity was not one species—it was six distinct subspecies of Homo sapiens, each adapted to extreme environments over 50,000+ years of geographic isolation. What fantasy calls "dwarves" and "elves" are actually evolutionary divergences shaped by volcanic caverns, ancient forests, and isolated ecosystems.

The Sundering forced these subspecies into contact for the first time in millennia. They don't recognize each other as kin. They see aliens.

The Six Subspecies of Homo Sapiens

[IMAGE: Comparative height chart showing all six subspecies side-by-side]

Homo sapiens sapiens (5'4"-6'2") | Homo Subterra (4'2"-4'10") | Homo Sylvanus (5'8"-6'6") | Homo Infernus (3'10"-4'8") | Homo Aquilus (5'6"-6'2") | Homo Polaris (5'0"-5'8")

1. Homo sapiens sapiens (Baseline Humans)

Common Names: Humans, Baseline, "The Unchanged"

Population: 8.2 million (68.3% of survivors)

Key Traits: Full melanin range, medium build, generalist physiology. No extreme adaptations—retained ancestral Homo sapiens phenotype.

Lifespan: 60-80 years (pre-Sundering)

Primary Factions: Church, Ossuarium, Exchange, Nomads

2. Homo Subterra (Dwarves)

Common Names: Dwarves, Forge-Kin, The Deep Folk, Stone-Born

Evolutionary Origin: ~40,000 BCE - Descended into volcanic cavern systems in the Alps and Caucasus Mountains. 45,000+ years underground near geothermal heat, low oxygen, darkness, and volcanic gases.

Physical Characteristics:

  • Skin: Ash-gray to charcoal-black (melanin loss + volcanic ash exposure). Thick, heat-resistant dermis.
  • Hair: Completely hairless (head, face, body). Hair follicles atrophied over 40,000 years.
  • Eyes: Reflective tapetum lucidum (like cats). Amber, copper, or deep red coloration. Sensitive to bright light.
  • Height: 4'2" - 4'10" (127-147 cm). Stocky build with barrel chest.
  • Weight: 180-250 lbs (bone density 40% higher than baseline)

Cultural Identity:

Facial tattoos using crushed minerals indicate clan, rank, and craft specialization. Runesmith tattoos are most intricate (master craftsmen). Smooth skin considered "unmarked" (childlike/unproven). Beardlessness is NOT a flaw—it's ancestral.

[IMAGE: Tattoo pattern chart - clan markings, craft symbols, rank indicators]

Population: Pre-Sundering: ~1.5 million | Year 437: 1.2 million (10% of survivors)

Lifespan: 90-120 years

Adaptations: Sulfur toxin immunity, seismic sensitivity, enhanced grip strength, slow metabolism (800 cal/day)

Primary Factions: Forge-Guilds, Crucible Pacts

3. Homo Sylvanus (Elves)

Common Names: Elves, Verdant, The Rooted, Green-Kin

Evolutionary Origin: ~50,000 BCE - Isolated in ancient Amazon Basin and Congo rainforests. Exposed to concentrated ley line energy for 50,000+ years. Developed symbiotic relationship with fungal networks (mycelial integration).

Physical Characteristics:

  • Skin: Chlorophyll-infused epidermis (pale green to olive-green tint). Semi-photosynthetic (absorb ~15% energy from sunlight). Bark-like texture on elbows, knees, spine.
  • Hair: Living plant fibers (cellulose-based, not keratin). Colors: moss green, bark brown, autumn red. Can photosynthesize.
  • Eyes: Compound iris structure (thousands of micro-lenses). See into infrared spectrum. Vertical slit pupils.
  • Height: 5'8" - 6'6" (173-198 cm). Tall and willowy.
  • Weight: 110-150 lbs (hollow bones like birds)

Casket Integration:

Elves undergo "Root-Grafting" - legs replaced with living vine prosthetics that plug directly into Casket hydraulics (plant-machine hybrid interface). Can photosynthesize energy for Casket systems (backup power).

[IMAGE: Root-Grafting diagram - vine prosthetics replacing legs, connecting to Casket hydraulics]

Population: 480,000 (4% of survivors)

Lifespan: 300-400 years (ley line exposure + photosynthetic supplementation)

Adaptations: Mycorrhizal nervous system (fungal tendrils connect to forest networks), regeneration (regrow fingers/toes), poison immunity, seasonal dormancy

Primary Factions: Verdant Covenant

4. Homo Infernus (Crucible Goblinoids)

Common Names: Goblinoids, Fire-Kin, Ashborn, Scrap-Folk

Evolutionary Origin: ~35,000 BCE - Descended into Icelandic volcanic rifts and Mediterranean sulfur caves. 33,000+ years exposed to extreme geothermal radiation and toxic gases.

Physical Characteristics:

  • Skin: Ash-gray to soot-black. Ritual burn scars cover 30-60% of body (cultural rite of passage—badges of honor).
  • Hair: White-gray to silver (thermal bleaching). Coarse texture. Often braided with obsidian beads.
  • Eyes: Yellow to orange (sulfur-pigmented retinas). Reflective in firelight.
  • Height: 3'10" - 4'8" (117-142 cm). Small but wiry.
  • Weight: 85-120 lbs. Fast-twitch muscle fibers.
  • Teeth: Filed teeth (cultural practice - sharpened canines)

[IMAGE: Goblinoid portrait showing ash-gray skin, ritual burn scars, yellow eyes, white-gray braided hair with obsidian beads]

Population: 220,000 (1.8% of survivors)

Lifespan: 35-50 years (fast metabolism = early burnout)

Adaptations: Pyrokinetic thermoregulation (body temp 102-105°F), sulfur toxin immunity, accelerated healing, pain suppression

Cultural Identity: Burn scars are badges of honor. More scars = higher status. Fire-worship pre-dates written history.

Primary Factions: Crucible Pacts

5. Homo Aquilus (Desert-Adapted Nomads)

Common Names: Desert-Kin, Sandborn, Wind-Walkers

Evolutionary Origin: ~30,000 BCE - Isolated in Sahara, Arabian, and Australian deserts. 29,000 years of extreme heat and water scarcity adaptation.

Physical Characteristics:

  • Skin: Deep bronze to copper-brown (extreme melanin concentration). No sweat glands (conserve water). Reflective sheen (silicon-based outer layer deflects UV).
  • Hair: Black to dark brown. Coarse texture. Dreadlocks common (natural formation).
  • Eyes: Double eyelids (nictitating membrane for sandstorms). Brown to amber.
  • Height: 5'6" - 6'2" (168-188 cm)
  • Weight: 130-170 lbs (lean, hollow bones)

[IMAGE: Nomad portrait showing bronze skin, sand-glass tattoos (melted silica), double eyelids, natural dreadlocks]

Population: 1.8 million (15% of survivors)

Lifespan: 50-70 years

Adaptations: Water conservation (kidneys extract 99% moisture), temperature extremes (120°F days, 40°F nights), endurance running (50+ miles/day)

Primary Factions: Nomad Collective

6. Homo Polaris (Northern Ice-Adapted)

Common Names: Frostborn, Pale Folk, Ice-Kin

Evolutionary Origin: ~25,000 BCE - Isolated in Arctic Circle (Siberia, Greenland, Northern Canada). 25,000 years of extreme cold adaptation.

Physical Characteristics:

  • Skin: Alabaster white (complete melanin loss). Thick subcutaneous fat layer (4x baseline). Semi-translucent (veins visible).
  • Hair: White, platinum blonde, or ice-blue. Dense (50% more follicles).
  • Eyes: Pale blue, gray, or white (minimal pigmentation). Reflective tapetum lucidum.
  • Height: 5'0" - 5'8" (152-173 cm). Compact build.
  • Weight: 160-220 lbs (dense fat reserves)

[IMAGE: Frostborn portrait showing alabaster skin, ice-blue hair, pale eyes, translucent complexion with blue undertone]

Population: ~50,000 (0.4% of survivors - CRITICALLY ENDANGERED)

Pre-Sundering Population: ~2.5 million (98% killed in Theslar Event - epicenter was Arctic)

Lifespan: 70-100 years

Adaptations: Extreme cold resistance (-60°F indefinitely), brown fat thermogenesis, hibernation capability

Status: Near-extinct. Most integrated into other factions or bound to Iron Doctrine trains.

Prognosis: Extinction within 200 years without intervention (genetic bottleneck, inbreeding depression)

Post-Sundering Mutations (Non-Evolutionary)

Vestige Bloodlines (Void-Mutated Humans)

NOT a subspecies - recent mutations (Year 0 - Year 437). Baseline humans exposed to Void radiation. Rapid mutation over 1-5 generations.

Population: ~150,000 (1.2% of survivors)

Examples: Serrak (reptilian), Vexis (insectoid), Fenmar (lupine), Urtok (ursine), Corvath (avian)

Emergent Syndicate (Collective Consciousness)

NOT a subspecies - metamorphosis into hive-mind. Voluntary transformation via Chrysalis process. Chitinous exoskeletons replace human biology. Individual consciousness merges into collective.

Population: ~100,000 drones + unknown hive-minds

Iron Doctrine (Train-Bound Immortals)

NOT a subspecies - accidental soul-fusion with Eternal Trains (Year 0). Immortal but bound to Pulse Rails. Cannot age, cannot die (while on rails), cannot leave trains.

Population: ~200,000 (1.7% of population)

Composition: Mixed subspecies (mostly baseline humans + Frostborn survivors)

Biological Compatibility & Interbreeding

Cross Fertility Offspring Viability Notes
Human × Human 100% 100% Baseline
Human × Dwarf 60% 80% Hybrid offspring common (medium height, partial heat resistance)
Human × Elf 40% 60% Rare (photosynthesis trait unstable, often stillborn)
Human × Goblinoid 30% 50% Very rare (metabolic incompatibility)
Human × Desert-Adapted 95% 95% Nearly identical genetics
Dwarf × Elf <5% <20% Biologically incompatible (volcanic vs forest adaptation)
Elf × Goblinoid <10% <30% Symbiotic fungi reject goblinoid thermoregulation

Cultural Taboos: Most factions consider interbreeding "diluting bloodlines." The Exchange allows it (pragmatic eugenics programs). Nomads practice it (survival > purity).

Evolutionary Timeline

~200,000 BCE: Homo sapiens emerges in Africa

~50,000 BCE: Elven Divergence
  └─ Homo Sylvanus (ley line exposure in ancient forests)

~40,000 BCE: Dwarven Descent
  └─ Homo Subterra (volcanic cavern adaptation)

~35,000 BCE: Geographic isolation continues
  ├─ Homo Infernus (geothermal rifts)
  ├─ Homo Aquilus (desert extremes)
  └─ Homo Polaris (Arctic conditions)

~10,000 BCE: Agriculture develops (baseline humans only)

~5,000 BCE: First contact between subspecies
  - Trade routes established
  - Subspecies recognized as "different peoples"
  - Myth of "separate creation" begins

~1,200 Years Ago: The Veil Accords
  - Mages and non-human species sign agreement to remain hidden
  - Memory-wiping, misinformation campaigns
  - "Revealing magic would mean war"

Year -100 to Year 0: Industrial age
  - Subspecies integrated into global economy
  - Dwarven engineering, Elven bio-sciences flourish
  - Pre-Sundering world: multicultural, subspecies-diverse

Year 0: Theslar Event (The Sundering)
  - 99.3% of all subspecies killed
  - Survivors form factions along subspecies lines
  - Cultural identity supersedes biological identity

Year 437: Present day
  - Subspecies intermarriage rare but increasing
  - Void mutations create NEW post-human variants
  - Humanity's future uncertain

Extinction Threats

Critically Endangered: Homo Polaris (Frostborn)

Pre-Sundering Population: ~2.5 million
Year 437 Population: ~50,000 (98% decline)

Causes:

  • Theslar Event epicenter was North Pole (their homeland)
  • Arctic survivors fled south (heat intolerance)
  • Genetic bottleneck (inbreeding depression)
  • Low birth rates in temperate climates

Prognosis: Extinction within 200 years without intervention

Vulnerable: Homo Sylvanus (Elves)

Threat: Deforestation + Void corruption destroying sacred groves

Year 437 Status:

  • 40% of ancestral forests destroyed
  • Mycorrhizal networks collapsing
  • Elves who lose grove connection suffer "The Withering" (slow death)

Prognosis: Stable if remaining forests protected

GM Notes: Using Subspecies in Play

Physical Traits Are Visible

  • Dwarven ash-skin and tattoos are instantly recognizable
  • Elven green tint and bark patches mark them as "other"
  • Desert-Adapted bronze skin distinguishes Nomads
  • Goblinoid burn scars and small stature are unmistakable

Mechanical Implications

  • Dwarves: +1 Heat Resistance, -1 Bright Light Penalty
  • Elves: +1 Regeneration (regrow 1 card/mission in sunlight), -1 Cold Resistance
  • Goblinoids: +1 Speed, -1 Endurance
  • Desert-Adapted: +1 Endurance, -1 Cold Resistance

Roleplay Hooks

  • "You're a Dwarf. The Church doesn't trust Dwarves. They think your volcanic heritage taints you."
  • "As an Elf, you feel the forest screaming. Every tree felled is pain."
  • "Goblinoids live fast, die young. You're 38—ancient by your people's standards."
  • "You're Frostborn. One of maybe 50,000 left alive. People stare at your albino skin like you're a ghost."

Cross-Subspecies Tensions

  • Dwarves vs Elves: "You worship dirt. We worship fire."
  • Humans vs Non-Humans: "We survived without mutations. You needed crutches."
  • All vs Vestige: "You're not evolution. You're corruption."
  • Nomads vs Settled Factions: "You build walls. We build survival."
