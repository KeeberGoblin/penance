The Factions: Humanity's Fractured Survival

"We used to be one species. Now we're ten tribes fighting over the corpse of civilization. Maybe that's progress. Maybe it's just decay." —Nomad Elder testimony

When the Sundering shattered civilization, it also shattered humanity's unity. From the ruins emerged ten major factions—each with unique philosophies, resources, and survival strategies. Some seek redemption through faith. Others embrace mutation and evolution. All compete for territory, resources, and the right to define what humanity will become in the centuries ahead.

Faction Power Structure (Year 437)

Tier Factions Characteristics
Tier 1: Superpowers Church of Absolution, The Ossuarium Control vast territories, populations 10-20 million, standing armies, industrial capacity
Tier 2: Regional Powers Dwarven Forge-Guilds, The Exchange, Elven Verdant Covenant Control key resources/geography, populations 2-5 million, specialized capabilities
Tier 3: Minor Factions Nomad Collective, Vestige Bloodlines, Emergent Syndicate, Wyrd Conclave Dispersed/isolated, populations 500k-2 million, guerrilla tactics or niche dominance
Tier 4: Outliers Crucible Packs, Draconid Remembrance Specialized/neutral, populations <100k, non-expansionist

The Ten Major Factions

1. Church of Absolution

Philosophy: "The Sundering was divine punishment for humanity's hubris. Salvation comes through penance and service."

Aspect Details
Territory Temperate zones (former Europe/North America), fortified cathedral-cities
Population ~15 million (largest faction)
Government Theocracy (Archbishops, Cardinals, Tech-Priests)
Economy Tithes, tribute, Casket leasing (to non-Church forces)
Military Penitent Legions (Casket pilots serving penance), Crusader infantry
Key Strength Morale (fanatical faith), numbers, established infrastructure
Key Weakness Rigid dogma, slow to adapt, internal schisms

2. The Ossuarium

Philosophy: "Souls are labor. Death is a debt. We collect both."

Aspect Details
Territory The Boneyards (industrial salvage complex), scattered outposts
Population ~12 million
Government Corporate meritocracy (Council of Ledgers, Bonelord Thresh as founder)
Economy Soul-binding contracts, Casket manufacturing/sales, Soulstone trade
Military Contracted Caskets (largest fleet), mercenary infantry
Key Strength Soul-binding monopoly, industrial capacity, wealth
Key Weakness Moral bankruptcy, widely hated, worker unrest

3. Dwarven Forge-Guilds

Philosophy: "Quality endures. Craft perfects. We build what lasts."

Aspect Details
Territory Underground cities (former mountain ranges), geothermal forges
Population ~3 million
Government Guild confederation (Council of Forgemasters)
Economy Custom Casket construction, weapons/armor smithing, engineering contracts
Military Elite Caskets (quality over quantity), runic-enhanced defenses
Key Strength Superior craftsmanship, defensible terrain, self-sufficient
Key Weakness Small population, slow production, isolationist tendencies

4. Elven Verdant Covenant

Philosophy: "Merge with nature. Heal the world. Reject the machines that broke it."

Aspect Details
Territory Preserved forests, bio-domes, controlled groves
Population ~2 million
Government Council of Rootwardens (democratic, consensus-based)
Economy Agriculture (best in world), bio-organic materials, symbiotic contracts
Military Bio-organic Caskets (living wood, vine hydraulics), guerrilla tactics
Key Strength Food security, environmental expertise, moral high ground
Key Weakness Fragile Caskets, limited territory, vulnerable to Void corruption

5. The Exchange

Philosophy: "Everything has a price. Death, life, loyalty—all negotiable."

Aspect Details
Territory Vault-cities (fortified trading hubs), caravan routes
Population ~4 million
Government Plutocracy (Council of Vaults, wealth = voting power)
Economy Banking, Casket leasing, Soulstone trading, debt collection
Military Repossessed Caskets, mercenary contracts, private security
Key Strength Wealth, trade networks, neutral ground (everyone needs them)
Key Weakness No loyalty, hated by debtors, vulnerable to economic collapse

6. Nomad Collective

Philosophy: "Stay mobile. Stay alive. Trust the convoy."

Aspect Details
Territory None (migratory caravans, temporary camps)
Population ~2 million (dispersed globally)
Government Decentralized (convoy Elders, consensus democracy)
Economy Scavenging, trade caravans, information brokering
Military Jury-rigged Caskets (patchwork but functional), hit-and-run tactics
Key Strength Mobility, oral history preservation, impossible to conquer (no fixed target)
Key Weakness No territory, vulnerable in winter, limited manufacturing

7. Vestige Bloodlines

Philosophy: "We didn't survive extinction by staying human. Evolution is survival."

Aspect Details
Territory Isolated enclaves (jungles, swamps, Void-adjacent zones)
Population ~1 million (mostly mutated)
Government Tribal (Bloodline Patriarchs, hereditary rule)
Economy Mutation services (controlled Void exposure), adaptive biotech
Military Bio-mechanical Caskets (grafted mutations), shock troops
Key Strength Adaptive evolution, Void resistance, unpredictable abilities
Key Weakness Widely feared, unstable mutations, internal power struggles

8. Emergent Syndicate

Philosophy: "The hive thinks. The hive endures. Individual thought is obsolete."

Aspect Details
Territory Bio-organic hive structures (jungles, underground)
Population ~500,000 (collective consciousness, hard to count)
Government Hive-mind (no leaders, collective decision-making)
Economy Resource pooling, bio-organic manufacturing, pheromone coordination
Military Swarm Caskets (hive-linked, perfect coordination), overwhelming numbers
Key Strength Perfect teamwork, no morale loss, rapid reproduction
Key Weakness Creepy (universally mistrusted), hive vulnerable to disruption, no diplomacy

9. Wyrd Conclave (The Fae)

Philosophy: "Reality is negotiable. Rules are suggestions. We dance between the cracks."

Aspect Details
Territory Void rifts, reality-warped zones (mobile, unpredictable)
Population ~100,000 (Stage 2-3 Void corruption, embraced)
Government Anarchic (Courts of Whimsy, no fixed hierarchy)
Economy Void artifacts, reality manipulation services, stolen goods
Military Reality-warped Caskets (phase-shifting, impossible geometry), trickery
Key Strength Reality manipulation, Void mastery, unpredictable
Key Weakness Insane (by human standards), unreliable, self-destructive tendencies

10. Crucible Packs

Philosophy: "Bombs purify. Explosions cleanse. We worship the blast."

Aspect Details
Territory Ashveil Caldera (volcanic wasteland)
Population ~15,000 (goblinoid mutations, bomb-worshipers)
Government Cult (Blast-Prophets, ritualized hierarchy)
Economy Explosive manufacturing, demolition contracts, scrap salvage
Military Explosive Caskets (mobile bombs, suicide tactics)
Key Strength Fearless, explosives expertise, chaotic
Key Weakness Self-destructive, tiny population, universally avoided

11. Draconid Remembrance

Philosophy: "We've seen this before. We'll see it again. Witness. Record. Endure."

Aspect Details
Territory Petrified groves (crystallizing giants, neutral ground)
Population ~50 (ancient beings, near-immortal)
Government None (individual Draconids, no collective authority)
Economy Knowledge trading, quest-giving, historical consultation
Military None (neutral NPCs, individually powerful but non-combatant)
Key Strength Ancient knowledge, neutrality, memory crystals (historical records)
Key Weakness Passive, crystallizing (literal petrification over centuries), pessimistic

Major Conflicts & Alliances

Active Wars (Year 437)

  • Church vs. Ossuarium (Cold War): Ideological conflict (redemption vs. exploitation), proxy battles, no open war yet
  • Elves vs. Wyrd (Border Skirmishes): Void corruption vs. environmental purification, contested forests
  • Exchange vs. Everyone (Economic Conflict): Debt collection, repossession raids, universal resentment

Unlikely Alliances

  • Church + Dwarves: Mutual defense pact (Dwarves supply weapons, Church provides manpower)
  • Ossuarium + Exchange: Economic partnership (Ossuarium manufactures, Exchange distributes)
  • Nomads + Everyone: Information trading (Nomads neutral, sell intelligence to highest bidder)

Permanent Enmities

  • Everyone vs. Emergent Syndicate: Hive-mind terrifies factions, universally distrusted
  • Everyone vs. Crucible Packs: Too dangerous to ally with (bombs solve nothing)
  • Elves vs. Ossuarium: Soul-binding is abomination (Elves' core belief vs. Ossuarium's foundation)

The Future of Faction Politics

Key Questions (Year 437)

  • Will Church and Ossuarium go to war? Cold war heats up yearly, open conflict seems inevitable
  • Can Elves purify Void corruption? If yes, shifts power balance dramatically
  • What happens when Exchange debt bubble bursts? Economic collapse could destabilize all factions
  • Will Emergent Syndicate expand? Hive-mind reproduction accelerates, territory grows
  • Are Draconids right about cycles? Do civilizations always self-destruct? Is Year 437 just delaying Year 0 Part 2?
