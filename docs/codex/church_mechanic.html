Blood Offering: Weaponizing Suffering

Church of Absolution Core Mechanic

"Pain purifies. Blood absolves. Sacrifice ensures the strike." -Church Combat Doctrine, Chapter VII
✿

The Core Mechanic: Blood Offering

Card Effect

Type: Gambit | SP Cost: 0 | Range: Self

Effect: Discard 1 card from top of your deck (self-harm). Your next attack this turn: +3 damage, ignores 1 Defense, and -1 to target number (easier to hit).

LIMIT: You can only play 1 Blood Offering per turn.

⚖️ Balance Note: Reduced from "discard 2 cards" to "discard 1 card" to improve campaign viability.

How It Works

Blood Offering is the Church's signature high-risk, high-reward mechanic. You sacrifice your own deck (HP) to guarantee a devastating attack. Here's the step-by-step:

  1. Play Blood Offering (0 SP) - No SP cost means you can combo it with other gambits
  2. Discard 1 card from deck - This is self-harm. Your deck IS your HP in Penance
  3. Your NEXT attack this turn - Gains all three bonuses:
    • +3 damage (huge burst)
    • Ignores 1 Defense (bypasses armor)
    • -1 to target number (4+ becomes 3+, easier to hit)
  4. Attack resolves - Apply all bonuses to your chosen attack card

Example Turn

Turn 1: You have 5 SP. You play:

  1. Blood Offering (0 SP) - Discard 1 card from deck
  2. Longsword Strike (2 SP) - Base: 5 damage, 4+ to hit
    • With Blood Offering: 8 damage, 3+ to hit, ignores 1 Defense
  3. Remaining SP: 3 (saved for defense or positioning)

You've turned a standard 5-damage attack into an 8-damage armor-piercing guaranteed hit—at the cost of 1 card from your deck.

Companion Mechanic: Righteous Fury

Righteous Fury (Passive): Each time an allied Casket is destroyed, gain +1 damage to all attacks permanently for the rest of the mission (max +3 damage).

⚖️ Balance Update Oct 19: Capped at +3 to prevent infinite scaling. Church also starts with 32 HP (base 30 +2) to compensate for self-harm.

Righteous Fury rewards you for fighting alongside allies and surviving their deaths. Combined with Blood Offering, a late-game Church pilot with +3 Fury can deal absurd burst damage:

Blood Offering + Longsword Strike (5 base) + Righteous Fury (+3) = 11 damage, armor-piercing, 3+ to hit

Strategy Guide: When to Use Blood Offering

✓ Good Times to Blood Offering

  • Turn 1 Alpha Strike - Establish early pressure. Most factions can't respond to 8-damage opening
  • Execute Targets - Combine with Divine Judgment to guarantee kills on 10 HP or less enemies
  • Breaking Armor - Ignoring 1 Defense + high damage punches through Tower Shields and Reinforced Plating
  • Securing Key Kills - When you MUST kill a specific target (healer, artillery, etc.), Blood Offering ensures the hit lands
  • Combo Setup - 0 SP cost means you can Blood Offering + expensive attacks in the same turn

✗ Bad Times to Blood Offering

  • Low HP Without Healing - Don't Blood Offering at 10 HP unless you have Consecrated Ground ready
  • Against Weak Targets - Wasting Blood Offering on 5 HP Thralls is inefficient. Save it for threats
  • When Already Ahead - If you're dominating, preserve your deck. Blood Offering is for desperation or alpha strikes
  • Multiple Turns in a Row - 1 per turn limit prevents spam, but even if you could, chaining self-harm spirals into death
  • Without Follow-Up - Blood Offering buffs your NEXT attack. If you have no attack cards, it's wasted
✿

Advanced Tactics & Combos

The Alpha Strike Opener

Turn 1 Combo:

Blood Offering (0 SP) → Longsword Cleave (3 SP) → Hit multiple enemies for 8 damage each

Why it works: You've spent 1 card and 3 SP to potentially deal 16-24 damage total (hitting 2-3 targets). Most factions can't recover from losing half their HP turn 1.

The Martyr's Gambit

Mid-Game Combo:

Martyrdom Protocol (redirect damage to self) → Blood Offering → Divine Judgment (execute)

Why it works: You tank damage for allies (building Heat but protecting them), then execute the attacker with buffed Divine Judgment. You're low HP but the threat is dead.

The Flagellant's Fury

Desperation Combo:

Flagellant's Zeal (discard 2 cards, gain 5 SP) → Blood Offering (discard 1 card) → Greatsword Overhead Strike (5 SP, 9 damage)

Total: Discard 3 cards, deal 12 damage + Righteous Fury, ignores 1 Defense, 3+ to hit. You're probably dying next turn, but you're taking someone with you.

The Healer's Paradox

Recovery Combo:

Blood Offering (discard 1) → Buffed Attack → Consecrated Ground (heal 3 cards)

Net result: -1 card, +3 cards = +2 cards gained while also dealing massive damage. Blood Offering isn't always a net loss if you can heal afterward.

Common Mistakes

❌ Mistake #1: Spamming Blood Offering Every Turn

Why it fails: 1 per turn limit exists for a reason. Even without the limit, discarding 1 card per turn with no recovery = death by turn 15-20.

Fix: Use Blood Offering strategically (turns 1, 4, 7, 10) with Consecrated Ground recovery between uses.

❌ Mistake #2: Using Blood Offering Without an Attack

Why it fails: Blood Offering buffs your NEXT attack. If you don't have an attack card in hand or enough SP to play it, you've discarded a card for nothing.

Fix: Always check your hand before playing Blood Offering. Make sure you have an attack ready to benefit.

❌ Mistake #3: Ignoring HP Management

Why it fails: Church has 32 HP (only +2 over standard). Blood Offering + Flagellant's Zeal + Martyrdom Protocol can spiral you to 0 HP in 3-4 turns without recovery.

Fix: Always include Repair Sigil or Consecrated Ground in your deck. Budget 1-2 turns mid-game for healing.

❌ Mistake #4: Wasting Blood Offering on Overkill

Why it fails: Using Blood Offering to turn a 5-damage attack into 8 damage against a 3 HP target wastes the buff.

Fix: Target high-HP enemies (15+ HP) where the +3 damage and armor-piercing matter. Use regular attacks for cleanup.

Strategy: Martyrdom Protocol + Martyr's Brand = ultimate ally protector. Consecrated Ground creates healing zones. Blood Offering for burst when needed. Slow but unkillable.

Matchup-Specific Tips

vs. Dwarven Forge-Guilds (High Armor)

Blood Offering ignoring 1 Defense is CRITICAL against Tower Shield + Reinforced Plating Dwarves. Without it, you're bouncing off 4-5 Defense. With it, you punch through. Always Blood Offering before attacking tanky Dwarven builds.

vs. Elven Verdant Covenant (High Mobility)

Blood Offering's -1 to target number (4+ becomes 3+) helps land hits on evasive Elves. They rely on dodging—make your attacks guaranteed. Combine with Crusader's Vow (movement + attack) to chase them down.

vs. The Ossuarium (Resurrection)

Ossuarium revives constantly. Blood Offering + Divine Judgment ensures kills STICK. Execute low-HP targets before they can resurrect. Don't waste regular attacks on Bone Thralls—Blood Offering ensures one-shot kills.

vs. Vestige Bloodlines (Mutation Spam)

Bloodlines stack mutations rapidly. Blood Offering lets you alpha-strike them before they spiral out of control. Turn 1 Blood Offering + Greatsword Cleave can cripple their early game.

vs. The Wyrd Conclave (Reality Manipulation)

Wyrd mechanics are unpredictable. Blood Offering's guaranteed +3 damage and armor-pierce can't be stolen or manipulated. It's one of the few "safe" mechanics against Fae trickery.
