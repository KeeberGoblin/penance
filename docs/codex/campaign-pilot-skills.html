Pilot Skills System

Optional Campaign Rule • Pilot Progression and Specialization

"Every scar tells a story. Every skill, a lesson written in iron and fire."
✿

Overview

Pilot Skills represent the accumulated combat experience, natural talent, and hard-won expertise of a Casket pilot. As pilots survive missions and grow in skill, they unlock powerful abilities that can shift the tide of battle.

Optional Rule

This system adds progression depth but increases complexity and bookkeeping. Skip this system for simpler campaigns or one-shot missions. Best used for campaigns of 10+ missions where pilot development matters.

✿

Gaining Experience

Pilots earn Experience Points (XP) from completing missions:

Mission Outcome Base XP
Victory 100 XP
Partial Victory (objectives incomplete) 50 XP
Survival (fled or tactical withdrawal) 25 XP
Defeat 0 XP

Bonus XP Modifiers

  • +25 XP: Killed 3+ enemy Caskets
  • +25 XP: No allied Caskets destroyed
  • +25 XP: Completed bonus objective
  • +50 XP: Boss enemy defeated
✿

Unlocking Skills

200 XP Roll 1d6 on Tier 1 table
500 XP Roll 1d20 on Tier 2 table
1000 XP Gain your faction's signature ability (Tier 3)

Maximum: Each pilot can have up to 3 skills (one from each tier).

Randomization: Skills are rolled randomly - no cherry-picking! Reroll duplicates.

Player Choice: Rolling 17-20 on Tier 2 grants your choice from that tier's table.

✿

Tier 1: Universal Basics (200 XP)

Roll 1d6 on This Table

Small but reliable bonuses. Early career foundation skills.

Roll Skill Name Effect
1 Steady Hands Your first attack each turn generates -1 Heat. (Good for sustained fire builds)
2 Scrap Scrounger Salvage action recovers 3 cards instead of 2 (3 SCRAP → 3 cards). (Good for component damage recovery)
3 Combat Awareness +1 to all Defense Dice results (not additional dice). Example: [2][3][4] becomes [3][4][5]. (Good for survivability)
4 Efficient Movement Moving 1-2 hexes costs only 1 SP instead of 2 SP. (Good for mobile strikers)
5 Heat Sink Upgrade Your Heat Strain threshold increases by 1 (roll at 6+ Heat instead of 5+). (Good for energy weapons)
6 Field Triage During Refresh Phase, if you took no damage last turn, remove 1 SCRAP card. (Good for long missions)

Reroll Rule: If you roll a skill you already have, reroll immediately.

Tier 2: Universal Specialization (500 XP)

Roll 1d20 on This Table

Moderate bonuses and powerful conditional effects. Define your combat style.

Roll Skill Name Effect
1 Headhunter (Passive) - Attacks targeting Head component deal +2 damage. (Precision builds)
2 Leg Breaker (Passive) - Attacks targeting Legs apply -1 Move until end of next turn (stacks). (Mobility denial)
3 Death Defiance (Once per Mission) - When reduced to 0 HP, survive with 1 card instead. (High-risk aggression)
4 Component Reinforcement (Passive) - Choose 1 component at mission start: +4 HP permanently this mission. (Adaptive defense)
5 SP Efficiency (Passive) - Start each turn with 4 SP instead of 3. (Action economy)
6 Battlemaster (Passive) - During Draw Phase, draw +1 additional card (up to hand limit). (Deck cycling)
7 Opportunist (Passive) - Gain +1 SP immediately when you destroy an enemy component. (Snowball potential)
8 Terrain Expert (Passive) - Ignore movement penalties from difficult terrain (rubble, water, etc.). (Mobility builds)
9 Ace Pilot (Once per Turn) - After moving, make one free 1 SP attack (does not cost SP). (Hit-and-run tactics)
10 Component Savant (Passive) - Choose 1 component at mission start: +4 HP and cannot be targeted by Called Shots. (Countering snipers)
11 Soulstone Overcharge (Once per Battle) - Gain +3 SP this turn, but take 3 Heat immediately. (Finishing blows)
12 Final Stand (Passive) - When you have only 1 component with HP: +3 damage and +2 Move. (Death spiral acceptance)
13 Cornered Beast (Passive) - When your deck has ≤10 cards: all attacks deal +2 damage. (Desperation builds)
14 Guardian Angel (Once per Mission) - When an allied Casket within 3 hexes would be reduced to 0 HP, they survive with 1 card instead. (Cooperative play)
15 Master Technician (Once per Battle) - During Refresh Phase, remove up to 3 SCRAP cards from your deck. (Component recovery)
16 Killing Blow (Passive) - When you destroy an enemy Casket (reduce to 0 HP), immediately draw 3 cards. (Aggressive offense)
17-20 PLAYER'S CHOICE Choose any skill from this Tier 2 table (1-16). This represents natural talent or focused training in a specific area. Congratulations - you got lucky!

Reroll Rule: If you roll a skill you already have, reroll immediately.

Player Choice (17-20): Lucky roll! Choose any skill from this table that you don't already have.

Settlement Phase Training

During the Settlement Phase, you may spend resources to train pilots:

Training Action

Cost: 75 Credits per session

Effect: Choose one pilot. They gain +100 XP.

Trade-Off: You can only perform one Settlement action per phase. Training means you cannot Repair, Recruit, or Trade this phase.

Pacing Note: With new thresholds (200/500/1000 XP), training accelerates progression. 2 training sessions = Tier 1 skill, 5 sessions = Tier 2 skill, 10 sessions = Tier 3 faction signature. This is expensive but allows veteran pilots to catch up.

✿

Flesh Bargain Integration

The Flesh Bargain can grant skills as alternative rewards:

Example Bargain: "The Veteran's Edge"

Cost: -4 Max HP (permanent)

Reward: Gain 200 XP immediately, then roll for a Tier 1 skill (or 500 XP for Tier 2 if bargain is more costly)

GM Note: Flesh Bargains grant XP, not direct skill picks - pilots still roll! This maintains the randomization philosophy while rewarding risk-takers.

✿

Pilot Death and Skill Loss

When a pilot dies permanently (Casket destroyed, failed death save, etc.):

  • All skills are lost
  • No XP carries over to new recruits
  • Veteran pilot death should be meaningful and painful

Optional Rule: Mentorship

If a veteran pilot (2+ skills) dies, their highest-tier skill can be passed to one other pilot in the roster as a "legacy."

✿

GM Guidelines

Balancing Skilled Pilots

When players have skilled pilots, adjust difficulty:

  • Increase enemy numbers by 10-20%
  • Give enemies 1-2 skills of their own
  • Add environmental hazards
  • Use more boss-tier enemies

When to Use This System

✓ Use When:

  • Campaigns run 10+ missions
  • Players want RPG progression
  • Permadeath is accepted
  • Faction mechanics are mastered

✗ Skip When:

  • Campaigns are 1-5 missions
  • Players prefer tactical purity
  • Minimizing bookkeeping
  • Learning core rules
✿

Designer's Notes

Pilot Skills add progression depth to campaigns, making veteran pilots feel distinct and powerful. The roll-based system prevents meta-optimization and creates emergent builds - every pilot's journey is unique.

Why Randomization? Penance is built on roll tables (mission generation, loot, events, pilot generation). Cherry-picking skills broke this philosophy. Random skills create:

  • Build Variety: No two pilots play the same way - rolls force adaptation
  • Emergent Identity: "The Immortal" didn't plan three resurrection skills - the dice told that story
  • Reduced Decision Paralysis: No agonizing over "optimal" picks - roll and adapt
  • Thematic Consistency: Everything from enemies to loot is procedural - skills should be too

The hybrid system (universal Tier 1/2, faction Tier 3) ensures every pilot gets their faction's signature while keeping early progression unpredictable.

Pilot skills work best when integrated with Flesh Bargain (HP-for-XP trades) and Settlement Phase Training (resource management). They should feel earned through blood, not handed out freely.

"A pilot is forged in fire, tempered in loss, and sharpened by survival. Skills are not learned—they are carved into your soul."
