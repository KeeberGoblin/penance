Elven Verdant Covenant

CODEX / FACTIONS / Elven Verdant Covenant
"The forest remembers. The thorns remember. And so shall your enemies remember the taste of their own blood."
❈

Playstyle: Hit-and-Run Assassin

Core Mechanic: Bleed Stacking (infinite scaling, mobility, regeneration)

Philosophy: Natural supremacy. The forest was here before civilization. It will remain after.

Population: 2-4 million

Territory: The Thornwood

Governance: Circle of Elders (consensus-based)

The Stalker (Scout): Assassin scout. 550 lbs, 6 SP, 26-32 cards.

The Archer (Medium): Ranged striker. 850 lbs, 7 SP, 28-36 cards.

The Thorn-Weaver (Heavy): Bleed master. 1300 lbs, 8 SP, 32-40 cards.

The Ancient Treant (Ultra-Heavy): Living wood. 1900+ lbs, 9 SP, 35-48 cards.

  • Thorn Stalkers: Mobility assassins, hit-and-fade tactics
  • Rootweavers: Plant-based control specialists
  • Archers: Ranged bleed application
  • Briarwood Colossus: Heavy defensive tree-construct
  • Assassin: Elite single-target damage
  • Ancient Treant: Oldest and mightiest protector

Apply Bleed tokens to enemies. Each Bleed token deals 1 damage at end of enemy turn. Stack as many bleeds as you can—infinite scaling potential.

Bleed Application: Most Elf attacks apply 1-2 Bleed tokens. Combine multiple attacks to stack rapidly.

Regeneration: Elves heal 1 HP per turn in Thornwood territory. Mobile and self-sustaining.

Advantage: Enemies suffer cumulative damage they can't avoid. Control the pace—attack, retreat, let bleeds do the work.

The Thornwood was a nature preserve before the Sundering. When civilization collapsed, the forest reclaimed the land. Elves emerged from the wild—not survivors of the old world, but products of the new one.

They use Caskets sparingly, binding their spirits into wood-grown constructs rather than industrial machines. Every casket is part-plant, part-machine. Every pilot is one with the forest.

They do not conquer territory. They reclaim it. Land that falls to Elven control returns to nature within years. They are neither conquerors nor defenders—they are inevitability.

Nomad Collective: Cooperative (travel through Thornwood)

Wyrd Conclave: Friendly (both respect wild magic)

Church of Absolution: Hostile (expansion into Thornwood)

The Exchange: Suspicious (resource exploitation)

Emergent Syndicate: Competitive (biomass and territory)

Early Game: Apply bleeds early. Mobility is key. Attack multiple targets to stack bleeds widely.

Mid Game: Maintain bleed pressure. Retreat and regenerate. Let bleeds wear enemies down.

Late Game: Stack bleeds to overwhelming levels. Enemies die to cumulative damage they couldn't prevent.

Key Strength: Infinite scaling. No upper limit to bleed stacking. Enemies who survive become weaker, not stronger.
