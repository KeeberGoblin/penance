The Ossuarium

CODEX / FACTIONS / The Ossuarium
"Death is not the end. It is evolution. Your flesh sustains us. Your bones strengthen us. Through you, we become eternal."
❈

Playstyle: Lifesteal Vampire

Core Mechanic: Soul Harvest (lifesteal triggers, resurrections, Decay cards)

Philosophy: Death as evolution. Undeath as continuation.

Population: 10-15 million (includes undead and living thralls)

Territory: The Boneyards

Governance: Immortal aristocracy of Lichs and Grave Lords

The Thrall (Scout): Swift undead servant. 650 lbs, 5 SP, 26-30 cards.

The Revenant (Medium): Risen warrior. 1050 lbs, 6 SP, 28-35 cards.

The Lich-Knight (Heavy): Undead nobility. 1500 lbs, 8 SP, 33-42 cards.

The Bone Colossus (Ultra-Heavy): Ancient horror. 2300+ lbs, 9 SP, 38-50 cards.

  • Bone Thralls: Expendable undead fodder
  • Grave Knight: Elite mounted undead warrior
  • Fleshcrafter: Bio-necromancer, damage conversion support
  • Bone Colossus: Massive armored construct
  • Lich: Spell-casting undead mage
  • Death's Herald: Resurrection and debuff specialist

Every time your Casket deals damage, gain HP (lifesteal). Dead cards stay in graveyard longer. Decay triggers resurrection of destroyed allies.

Lifesteal Trigger: Heal 1 HP per 2 damage dealt. Stacks with multiple attacks.

Resurrection: Spend 3 Decay tokens to resurrect one destroyed Support Unit at 50% HP.

Advantage: Sustain through battle. The longer you fight, the more you heal. Undeath never ends.

The Ossuarium began as a death cult seeking immortality. When Casket technology emerged, they discovered a path: trap souls in Soulstones, bind them to preserved corpses, achieve undeath.

Now they are an industrial empire built on grave-robbing and necromancy. The Boneyards stretch for hundreds of miles—endless ossuary cities where the dead work alongside the living.

They do not raid territories. They harvest them. Every battlefield becomes a recruitment ground. Every death strengthens them.

Church of Absolution: Cordial (shared understanding of death)

The Exchange: Hostile (refuse to conduct soul trade)

Dwarven Forge-Guilds: Suspicious (undead philosophy conflicts)

Nomad Collective: Predatory (harvest nomad dead)

Emergent Syndicate: Competitive (both seek biomass dominance)

Early Game: Maximize damage output. Every point of damage heals you. Trade aggressively.

Mid Game: Activate Decay triggers. Resurrect fallen allies. Build graveyard advantage.

Late Game: Sustain becomes your superpower. Enemies fade; you endure.

Key Strength: The longer battle lasts, the more you heal. Unlike Church, you don't sacrifice resources—you harvest them. Immortal attrition.
