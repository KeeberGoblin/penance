The Core Mechanic: Credit Token Economy

Monetary Power System

Generation: Start each mission with 10 Credits. Gain +2 Credits per turn passively. Complete contracts for bonus Credits (+5 per contract).

Maximum Storage: 50 Credits (excess Credits are lost)

Spending Options:

  • Hire Mercenary (15 Credits): Summon allied unit (12 HP, 4 damage, lasts 3 turns)
  • Purchase Equipment (10 Credits): Draw 3 cards, keep 1
  • Bribe Enemy (12 Credits): Force 1 enemy to skip their next turn
  • Bounty Contract (5 Credits): Mark 1 enemy. Deal +3 damage to marked target. If killed, gain 8 Credits back

How It Works

Credit Tokens are pure economic warfare—you buy advantages, hire reinforcements, and bribe enemies. The more Credits you have, the more options you command. Here's the cycle:

  1. Generate Credits - Passive +2/turn, complete contracts for +5 bonus
  2. Bank Early Game - Save 15-20 Credits by turn 4-5
  3. Strategic Spending - Use Credits to:
    • Hire Mercenaries (extra bodies, tank damage)
    • Purchase Equipment (cycle deck for perfect cards)
    • Bribe Enemies (disable key threats)
    • Bounty Contracts (high-value target elimination)
  4. Credit Loop - Bounty Contracts refund Credits on kill. Spend 5, gain 8 back. Profit through efficiency.

Example Credit Management

Turn 1: Start with 10 Credits, +2 passive = 12 Credits

Turn 2: +2 passive = 14 Credits. Bank it.

Turn 3: +2 passive = 16 Credits. Spend 15 to Hire Mercenary (now 1 Credit, have ally)

Turn 4: +2 passive = 3 Credits. Complete contract (+5 bonus) = 8 Credits total

Turn 5: +2 passive = 10 Credits. Spend 5 on Bounty Contract (mark boss enemy)

Turn 6: Kill marked enemy → Gain 8 Credits refund → Now at 13 Credits. Net gain: +3

You profited from killing. Welcome to Exchange economics.

Companion Mechanic: Bounty Hunter

Bounty Hunter (Passive): When you kill an enemy with an active Bounty Contract, gain +3 Credits instead of +2. Bounties become even more profitable.

High-Value Target (Active): Once per mission, mark 1 enemy as High-Value Target (costs 0 Credits). If killed, gain 15 Credits and draw 2 cards.

⚖️ Exchange gets paid to win. Kill marked targets for Credit refunds. Efficiency is profit.

Strategy Guide: When to Spend Credits

✓ Good Times to Spend Credits

  • Turn 3-4 (Hire Mercenary) - 15 Credits for an ally that tanks 12 HP of damage is efficient
  • Before Boss Fights (Purchase Equipment) - 10 Credits to draw 3, keep 1. Find your win condition
  • Against Key Threats (Bribe Enemy) - 12 Credits to disable enemy healer/artillery for 1 turn. Disrupts their strategy
  • High HP Targets (Bounty Contract) - 5 Credits for +3 damage and 8 Credit refund on kill. Always profitable
  • High-Value Target on Bosses - Free mark, 15 Credit payout + 2 cards on kill. Massive value

✗ Bad Times to Spend Credits

  • Early Game (Turn 1-2) - Don't spend before accumulating 15+ Credits. Be patient
  • On Weak Enemies - Don't Bounty Contract a 10 HP Thrall. Save for real threats
  • When Winning Easily - If already dominating, bank Credits for next mission
  • Bribing Low-Impact Enemies - 12 Credits to disable a weak melee unit is wasteful. Bribe healers/artillery
  • At Credit Cap (50) - You're wasting generation. Spend to make room

Advanced Tactics & Combos

The Mercenary Wall

Defensive Combo:

Turn 3: Hire Mercenary (15 Credits) → Turn 5: Hire second Mercenary (15 Credits) → Position both between you and enemies

Result: Two mercenaries absorb 24 HP of damage over 3 turns. You take zero hits. Pure economic defense.

The Bounty Loop

Profit Combo:

Bounty Contract enemy A (5 Credits) → Kill A (refund 8 Credits, net +3) → Bounty Contract enemy B (5 Credits) → Kill B (refund 8 Credits, net +3) → Repeat

You profit +3 Credits per kill. Infinite economic loop as long as you're killing. Getting paid to win.

The High-Value Spike

Boss Killer Combo:

Mark boss as High-Value Target (0 Credits) → Bounty Contract same boss (5 Credits, +3 damage) → Kill boss (gain 15 Credits + 8 Credits refund + 2 cards = 23 Credits and 2 cards)

You spent 5 Credits, gained 23 Credits and 2 cards. 18 Credit profit plus deck cycling. Absurd value.

The Bribe Chain

Control Combo:

Turn 5: Bribe enemy healer (12 Credits, skip their turn) → Turn 6: Kill all wounded enemies before healer can act → Turn 7: Bribe enemy artillery (12 Credits)

You disabled two key enemies for 24 Credits total. Won the fight by removing their support. Money buys control.

Common Mistakes

❌ Mistake #1: Spending Too Early

Why it fails: Spending 10 Credits on turn 1 leaves you with 2 Credits. You can't afford Mercenaries or Bribes for 3-4 turns.

Fix: Bank until turn 3-4. Accumulate 15-20 Credits, then spend strategically. Patience builds wealth.

❌ Mistake #2: Not Using Bounty Contracts

Why it fails: Bounty Contracts are profit-positive. Spending 5 Credits to gain 8 back (net +3) means you're getting paid to kill.

Fix: Always Bounty Contract high-value targets. It's free money and +3 damage. Use it every mission.

❌ Mistake #3: Hoarding Credits Without Spending

Why it fails: Sitting at 45 Credits means you're wasting +2/turn generation. Credits at cap are worthless.

Fix: Spend down to 25-30 Credits periodically. Hire Mercenaries, Purchase Equipment, Bribe enemies. Use your wealth.

❌ Mistake #4: Wasting High-Value Target

Why it fails: Using High-Value Target on a 15 HP minion is inefficient. It's once-per-mission—save for bosses.

Fix: Mark the toughest enemy (30+ HP boss). 15 Credit payout + 2 cards on kill is massive. Be strategic.

Matchup-Specific Tips

vs. Church of Absolution (Burst Damage)

Church alpha strikes early. Hire Mercenary on turn 3 to tank their Blood Offering burst. Your ally absorbs 12 HP, you take zero. Economic defense wins.

vs. Dwarven Forge-Guilds (High Armor)

Dwarves have 3-4 Defense. Bounty Contract them for +3 damage (bypasses some armor). Hire Mercenaries to swarm—multiple attacks overwhelm their Defense.

vs. The Ossuarium (Resurrection)

Ossuarium resurrects constantly. Bounty Contract key targets, kill them multiple times, profit each time. Their resurrections fund your economy. Grind them into bankruptcy.

vs. Elven Verdant Covenant (Bleed Kiting)

Elves kite and Bleed. Hire Mercenaries to body-block their escapes. Bribe their fastest unit to disrupt kiting. Bounty Contract their damage dealer. Control beats mobility.

vs. The Wyrd Conclave (Reality Manipulation)

Wyrd steal and manipulate. Your Credits are YOUR resource—they can't steal tokens. Bank Credits, Hire Mercenaries (they can't steal allies), Bounty Contract for guaranteed +3 damage. Economy beats trickery.
