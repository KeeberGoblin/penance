CODEX / BALANCE / Balance Analysis

Balance Analysis Report

Simulation Results & Recommendations

"Through simulation, we divine the flaws. Through iteration, we achieve balance."

Executive Summary

Ran 3 combat scenarios and 1 campaign simulation to analyze faction balance, casket class viability, and progression systems. Key findings:

  • Church self-harm is too aggressive (deals 2 damage per turn for +3 damage bonus)
  • Bleed stacking has no cap (reached 9 stacks in testing)
  • Dwarven rune stacking is well-balanced (slow ramp, capped at +5)
  • Fortress vs Scout matchup is asymmetric but balanced (tank wins through attrition)
  • Campaign difficulty may be too high (0/1 campaigns completed)

Scenario 1: Church vs Dwarves

Result: DRAW (Holy Avenger: 20 HP | Runeforge Titan: 26 HP)

Combat Flow

  • Turn 1-5: Church uses Blood Offering every turn, dealing 7 damage/turn (but costing 2 HP/turn)
  • Turn 6-10: Dwarves ramp up to +5 damage bonus (capped), matching Church output
  • Turn 11-16: Both factions trade evenly, fight goes to time

⚠️ Balance Issue: Church Self-Harm

Problem: Church dealt 14 damage to themselves over 16 turns (7 Blood Offerings × 2 HP each). This is nearly half their starting HP (30). They're killing themselves almost as fast as their opponent.

Impact: Church has no longevity in extended fights. Great for burst, terrible for campaigns.

✅ Recommendation: Limit Self-Harm Frequency

Option 1: "Blood Offering can only be used once per turn" (prevents spam)

Option 2: "Costs 1 HP instead of 2" (reduces self-damage by 50%)

Option 3: "Gain +2 damage instead of +3, but free cost" (trade power for sustainability)

Scenario 2: Ossuarium vs Elves

Result: DRAW (Bone Lord: 22 HP | Thornblade: 19 HP)

Mechanic Analysis

Faction Damage Dealt HP Healed/Lost Net HP Change
Ossuarium 24 damage (6 attacks × 4) +12 HP (6 lifesteals × 2) +4 HP (30 → 34 effective)
Elves 30 direct damage + 45 Bleed (10 attacks × 3 + accumulated Bleed) - -7 HP (26 → 19)

⚠️ Balance Issue: Bleed Stacking

Problem: Bleed counters stacked to 9 with no cap. If fight continued, Ossuarium would take 10+ Bleed damage per turn (more than any attack in the game).

Impact: Elves have infinite scaling in long fights. Becomes oppressive in campaigns.

✅ Recommendation: Cap Bleed Counters

Proposed Cap: 10 Bleed counters maximum

Rationale: 10 damage/turn is already devastating (equal to 1/3 of Scout HP). Prevents runaway scaling while keeping Elves viable.

Alternative: Bleed decays by 2 per turn instead of 1 (faster cleanup, rewards burst windows)

Scenario 3: Scout vs Fortress

Result: VICTORY - Iron Wall (Swift Striker: 0 HP | Iron Wall: 34 HP)

Casket Class Balance

Scout (6 SP, 26 HP): Fast actions, low HP pool

Effectiveness: 52%

Fortress (3 SP, 50 HP): Slow actions, massive HP pool

Effectiveness: 68%

✅ Analysis: Asymmetric But Balanced

Scout dealt 48 damage total (16 attacks × 3) + Bleed damage

Fortress dealt 92 damage total (slower ramp, but hit harder late game)

Conclusion: Fortress wins through attrition. Scout needs mobility/kiting to win (which simulation didn't test). This is working as intended...asymmetric matchup where tactical positioning matters.

Campaign Simulation

Result: FAILED (Mission 1 - Draw vs Dwarves Enemy)

Campaign Analysis

  • Starting HP: 30
  • Final HP: 20 (after draw)
  • Damage Taken: 10 HP (33% of starting HP)
  • Scrap Gained: 0 (no victory)
  • Issue: Draw = Defeat in campaigns (no progression)

⚠️ Balance Issue: Campaign Difficulty

Problem: Even a "draw" ends the campaign. Church's self-harm (14 damage over 16 turns) + enemy damage (30 damage) = 44 total damage taken on a 30 HP pool.

Impact: Church cannot sustain 3 missions without dying. Too punishing for campaign play.

✅ Recommendation: Campaign Tuning

Option 1: Grant partial rewards for "close draws" (within 5 HP difference)

Option 2: Reduce enemy HP by 25% in campaign mode

Option 3: Add "Field Repair" option (spend 3 Scrap to recover 5 HP between missions)

Option 4: Start campaigns with 10 Scrap instead of 5

Faction Power Rankings

Rank Faction 1v1 Power Campaign Viability Notes
1 Ossuarium High High Lifesteal provides sustain for both modes
2 Dwarves High Medium-High Rune stacking balanced, no self-harm
3 Elves Medium Medium Strong late game, needs Bleed cap
4 Church Medium Low High burst, terrible sustainability

Recommended Changes

Priority 1: Church Balance

Change: Blood Offering costs 1 HP instead of 2

Impact: Reduces self-harm by 50%, improves campaign viability without removing risk

Rationale: Church should be high-risk, not suicide. 1 HP/turn is still meaningful sacrifice.

Priority 2: Bleed Cap

Change: Maximum 10 Bleed counters per target

Impact: Prevents infinite scaling, keeps Elves competitive without becoming oppressive

Rationale: 10 damage/turn is already massive. Cap prevents degenerate gameplay.

Priority 3: Campaign Difficulty

Change: Add "Field Repair Kit" (3 Scrap → Recover 5 HP) in Settlement Phase

Impact: Gives players agency to heal between missions

Rationale: Rewards good economy management, doesn't auto-heal (maintains tension)

Priority 4: Lifesteal Tuning (Monitor)

Change: No immediate change, but monitor for future testing

Concern: Ossuarium won both matchups tested (Draw vs Elves, superior HP in campaign)

Potential Nerf: Lifesteal heals 1 HP instead of 2, or has diminishing returns after 10 HP healed

Conclusion

Overall, Penance's combat system shows promising asymmetric balance. The core mechanics work well:

  • ✅ Deck-as-HP creates meaningful attrition
  • ✅ Faction mechanics feel distinct
  • ✅ Casket classes have clear identities
  • ⚠️ Church needs slight tuning (reduce self-harm)
  • ⚠️ Bleed needs cap (prevent runaway scaling)
  • ⚠️ Campaign difficulty needs adjustment (too punishing currently)

Recommended Next Steps:

  1. Implement Priority 1-3 changes
  2. Run 10+ simulations with new values
  3. Playtest with human players (AI can't test positioning/kiting)
  4. Monitor Ossuarium winrate in extended testing
