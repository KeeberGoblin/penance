Chapter 2: Combat System

"Your deck is your life. Every card you lose brings you closer to death."

Core Concept: Dual-Layer Damage

Penance uses a dual-layer damage system combining deck-as-HP mechanics with brutal permanent consequences.

Two Separate Systems:

  1. Casket HP Deck (26-50 cards) - Your mech's structural integrity
  2. Pilot Wound Deck (10 cards) - Your pilot's physical/mental state

When Casket HP Deck runs out ‚Üí Casket is destroyed (pilot may survive)
When Pilot Wound Deck runs out ‚Üí Pilot dies (Casket becomes inert)

‚öî

The 3-Pile System

Your Casket uses THREE separate card piles during battle:

  1. HP Draw Deck (26-50 cards) - Your active deck, represents structural integrity
  2. Discard Pile - Cards you've played/used during your turn (attacks, movement, abilities)
  3. Damage Graveyard - Cards destroyed by taking damage (permanent loss, gone forever)

Key Distinction:

  • Play a card (attack, defend, move) ‚Üí Goes to Discard Pile ‚Üí Will reshuffle when draw deck empty
  • Take damage (enemy hits you) ‚Üí Cards go to Damage Graveyard ‚Üí NEVER comes back

Pilot Wound Deck (10 Cards)

Separate from Casket HP! Tracks pilot's physical/mental state.

When Pilot Takes Wounds:

  • Pilot Exposure Zone - Component damage in exposure range
  • Thread Snap - Neural thread severed (1d6 check)
  • Chassis Breach - Casket destroyed at 10+ Chassis damage (+3 Wounds!)
  • Campaign Events - Ambushes, anomalous entities

Pilot Wound Effects:

Wounds Effect
1-3Minor injuries, no penalties
4-6-1 to all rolls (pain, shock)

Pilot Death vs Casket Destruction:

  • Casket destroyed, pilot alive: Pilot ejects, can pilot new Casket
  • Pilot dead, Casket intact: Casket becomes inert scrap
  • Both destroyed: Total loss (campaign character death)

Heat System

Heat represents overloading systems, thermal buildup.

Gain Heat From:

  • Overextend card (+1 Heat)
  • Certain faction abilities (Church Blood Offering, etc.)
  • Defense Dice [HEAT] symbols
  • Weapon jam (Catastrophic Failure, +2 Heat)

Heat Penalties:

HeatPenalty
0-2No penalty
3-4-1 damage to all attacks
5-6-2 damage, -1 SP max
7+OVERHEAT: Cannot attack, -2 SP max

Remove Heat:

  • Emergency Vent card (2 SP, remove 3 Heat)
  • Breathe the Core card (1 SP, remove 1 Heat + draw 1)
  • Standing in water (auto-remove 2 Heat per turn)
‚Äî 12 ‚Äî

Turn Structure (4 Phases)

Phase 1: Upkeep

  1. Draw cards to hand size (5 cards default)
  2. Reset SP to maximum (varies by Casket Class)
  3. Apply start-of-turn effects (Heat penalties, ongoing effects)
  4. Check win conditions (enemy eliminated, objective secured)

Phase 2: Action Phase

Spend SP to play cards:

  • Play cards from hand (costs SP shown on card)
  • Move (costs SP based on Casket Class)
  • Attack (resolve to-hit, damage, defense)
  • Use abilities (faction-specific powers)

Free Actions (no SP cost):

  • 1 rotation per turn (change facing 1 hex-side)
  • Declare reactive cards (played in response to attacks)
  • Discard cards voluntarily

Phase 3: End Phase

  1. Discard down to hand size if over 5 cards
  2. Apply end-of-turn effects (Heat damage, ongoing effects)
  3. Mark movement this turn (for next turn's to-hit modifiers)

Phase 4: Enemy Turn

Opponent takes their turn (repeat phases 1-3)

‚öî

Victory Conditions

Skirmish (Deathmatch):

  • Eliminate all enemy Caskets
  • Or force enemy surrender (negotiated)

Objective Control:

  • Hold objective for X turns
  • Capture multiple objectives
  • First to Y victory points

Campaign Scenarios:

  • Escort duty (protect NPC to extraction)
  • Sabotage (destroy enemy structure)
  • Survival (last X turns vs waves)
  • Assassination (eliminate specific target)

When a component is destroyed, equipment becomes "SCRAP".

Chapter 5: Range & Line of Sight

"Distance is measured in hexes. Death is measured in mistakes. Know your range. Know your angles."

Hex Grid Basics

Range is counted in hexes from your position.

  • Range 0: The hex you're in
  • Range 1: 6 adjacent hexes
  • Range 2: 12 hexes one ring out
  • Range 3+: Continue counting outward

Do NOT count your own hex.

‚öî

Range Bands (For To-Hit Modifiers)

Range Distance To-Hit Mod Weapons
Short 0-3 hexes +0 Melee, pistols, shotguns
Medium 4-6 hexes +1 Rifles, bows, crossbows
Long 7-10 hexes +2 Sniper rifles, long bows
Extreme 11+ hexes +3 Artillery, siege weapons

Example:

Rifle Shot (Range 8):

  • Shooting at 3 hexes ‚Üí Short range ‚Üí +0 modifier
  • Shooting at 5 hexes ‚Üí Medium range ‚Üí +1 modifier (need 6+ instead of 5+)
  • Shooting at 8 hexes ‚Üí Long range ‚Üí +2 modifier (need 7+ instead of 5+)

Line of Sight (LOS)

To attack, you must have clear LOS to target.

You have LOS if:

  • You can draw a straight line from center of your hex to target hex
  • That line does NOT pass through LOS-blocking terrain

LOS is BLOCKED by:

  • Walls (solid obstacles)
  • Large terrain (buildings, cliffs)
  • Dense forests (marked "blocks LOS")

LOS is NOT blocked by:

  • Other Caskets (you can shoot past allies/enemies)
  • Rubble (provides cover, doesn't block)
  • Light terrain (water, ice, clear ground)

Cover System

Cover provides defensive bonuses without blocking LOS.

Cover Types:

  • Light Cover (forest, rubble): +1 to target number
  • Heavy Cover (fortress walls): +2 to target number
  • Other Caskets: +1 if enemy between you and attacker

Cover does NOT stack - use highest modifier only.

Rotation & Facing Changes

  • 1 free rotation per turn (1 hex-side clockwise or counter-clockwise)
  • Additional rotations cost 1 SP each
  • Example: Rotate 3 hex-sides = 1 free + 2 SP

Shield Side vs Weapon Side

Declare shield side during Casket construction.

Left-Shield (Default):

  • Shield protects hex-sides 5, 6, 1 (left + front)
  • Weapons on hex-sides 2, 3 (right side)

Right-Shield:

  • Shield protects hex-sides 1, 2, 3 (right + front)
  • Weapons on hex-sides 5, 6 (left side)

Shield Side Effects:

  • Attacks from shield-side hexes can be blocked with shield cards
  • Attacks from weapon-side hexes CANNOT be blocked with shields
  • Rear attacks (hex-sides 3, 4, 5) cannot use reactive defense cards

Elevation & Height

Higher Ground (+1 level or more):

  • -1 to target number (easier to hit)
  • +1 damage to attacks
  • Ignore cover (shoot over it)

Shooting Uphill (-1 level or more):

  • +1 to target number (harder to hit)
  • -1 damage to attacks

Climbing:

  • Moving UP 1 level: +1 SP (2 SP total)
  • Moving DOWN 1 level: Normal cost (1 SP)
