The Core Mechanic: Improvisation

Flexible Multi-Use Cards

Concept: Most Nomad cards have 2-3 different modes. You choose which mode to activate when playing the card.

Example - "Scrap Weapon":

  • Mode 1 (Attack): 4 damage, 2 SP
  • Mode 2 (Defense): +2 Defense this turn, 1 SP
  • Mode 3 (Utility): Draw 1 card, 1 SP

Scavenge System: When you kill an enemy or complete objectives, gain Scrap tokens. Spend 3 Scrap to draw 1 card or reduce SP cost of next card by 1.

How It Works

Improvisation means Nomads are never caught without options. Every card is versatile, and Scrap economy lets you adapt on the fly. Here's how:

  1. Multi-Mode Cards - Each Nomad card has 2-3 activation modes. Read the situation, pick the mode that helps most
  2. Scrap Generation - Kill enemies (+2 Scrap), complete objectives (+3 Scrap), survive desperate situations (+1 Scrap)
  3. Scrap Spending - Use Scrap to:
    • Draw 1 card (3 Scrap)
    • Reduce next card cost by 1 SP (3 Scrap)
    • Repair 1 card (2 Scrap)
  4. Adapt and Overcome - Never locked into one strategy. Switch modes mid-fight based on what you need

Example Turn - Improvising Victory

Turn 1: Play Scrap Weapon (Mode 1: Attack, 4 damage) → Kill enemy → Gain 2 Scrap

Turn 2: Enemy counterattacks hard → Play Scrap Weapon (Mode 2: Defense, +2 Defense)

Turn 3: Hand is weak → Spend 3 Scrap to Draw 1 card → Play drawn card

Turn 4: Need big play but low SP → Spend 3 Scrap to reduce next card cost → Play expensive attack cheaply

Same card solved 2 problems. Scrap economy filled the gaps. Pure versatility.

Companion Mechanic: Survivor's Luck

Survivor's Luck (Passive): When reduced to 10 HP or below, gain +1 Scrap per turn and all multi-mode cards cost -1 SP (min 0).

Desperate Measures (Active): Once per mission, when below 10 HP, spend all Scrap to fully heal (recover cards equal to Scrap spent, max 10).

⚖️ Nomads are strongest when cornered. Low HP activates survival mode—more Scrap, cheaper cards, clutch healing.

✿

Strategy Guide: When to Use Each Mode

✓ Good Times to Switch Modes

  • Aggro to Defense - Start aggressive (attack mode), switch to defense mode when HP drops below 15
  • Defense to Utility - If safe and hand is weak, switch to utility mode (draw cards) to refresh options
  • Utility to Aggro - Drew good cards? Switch back to attack mode and finish enemies
  • Scrap Spending for Burst - Have 6 Scrap? Spend 3 to reduce SP cost, play expensive card, then spend 3 to draw refill
  • Desperate Measures Timing - At 8 HP with 8 Scrap? Activate Desperate Measures (heal 8 cards = back to 16 HP). Clutch comeback

✗ Bad Times to Improvise

  • Over-Switching - Don't use utility mode every turn. Focus on winning, not cycling cards aimlessly
  • Hoarding Scrap - Sitting at 12 Scrap is wasted value. Spend 3-6 Scrap per mission to stay flexible
  • Desperate Measures Too Early - Using heal at 12 HP wastes potential. Wait until 8 HP or below for maximum value
  • Wrong Mode for Situation - Playing attack mode when you need defense is still a mistake, even with options. Read the board

Advanced Tactics & Combos

The Scrap Engine

Resource Loop:

Kill enemy (+2 Scrap) → Spend 3 Scrap to draw card → Play drawn card (kill another enemy, +2 Scrap) → Repeat

Result: As long as you're killing, you're generating Scrap faster than spending it. Economy perpetuates itself.

The Survivor's Gambit

Intentional Low HP:

Let HP drop to 9 (activate Survivor's Luck) → All cards cost -1 SP, gain +1 Scrap/turn → Play 3-4 cards per turn cheaply → Build 8+ Scrap → Desperate Measures heal back to 17 HP

You weaponize being low HP, then fully heal when ready. High-risk power spike.

The Mode Mixer

Versatility Chain:

Turn 1: Attack mode (kill enemy) → Turn 2: Defense mode (survive burst) → Turn 3: Utility mode (draw cards) → Turn 4: Attack mode (finish fight)

You solved 3 different problems with the same cards. No dead draws, no bad hands. Always have answers.

Common Mistakes

❌ Mistake #1: Always Using the Same Mode

Why it fails: If you always pick attack mode, you're just a worse damage faction. Improvisation's strength is FLEXIBILITY.

Fix: Read the situation each turn. Need defense? Use defense mode. Need cards? Use utility mode. Adapt constantly.

❌ Mistake #2: Not Spending Scrap

Why it fails: Scrap cap is 15. Sitting at cap means wasted generation. Every kill beyond 15 is lost value.

Fix: Spend 3 Scrap whenever you have 9+. Use it to draw cards, reduce costs, or repair. Keep Scrap flowing.

❌ Mistake #3: Wasting Desperate Measures

Why it fails: Using Desperate Measures at 12 HP with 4 Scrap only heals 4 cards. That's inefficient for a once-per-mission ability.

Fix: Wait until you're at 6-8 HP with 8+ Scrap. Heal 8-10 cards for massive comeback. Timing is everything.

Build Archetypes

Pure Improviser

Focus: Maximum versatility, all multi-mode cards

Equipment: Scrap Weapon (6 cards), Makeshift Tool (5 cards), Salvage (2 cards), Light Armor (1 card)

Strategy: Switch modes every turn based on situation. Never committed, always adapting. Jack-of-all-trades mastery.

Scrap Hoarder

Focus: Generate massive Scrap, explosive late-game

Equipment: Salvage (4 cards), Scrap Weapon (4 cards), Makeshift Tool (3 cards), Medium Armor (2 cards)

Strategy: Bank 12+ Scrap by turn 6. Spam reduced-cost cards. Desperate Measures for 10+ card heal. Snowball through economy.

Survivor's Edge

Focus: Intentional low HP, Survivor's Luck abuse

Equipment: Scrap Weapon (5 cards), Makeshift Tool (4 cards), Desperate Measures (1 card), Light Armor (1 card), Salvage (2 cards)

Strategy: Drop to 9 HP intentionally (all cards -1 SP). Play 4-5 cards per turn cheaply. Build 8+ Scrap. Desperate Measures heal to full. High-risk power spike.

Matchup-Specific Tips

vs. Church of Absolution (Burst Damage)

Church alpha strikes turn 1. Use defense mode to survive their Blood Offering. Generate Scrap from kills, then outlast them through Scrap economy. Flexibility beats one-dimensional aggression.

vs. Dwarven Forge-Guilds (Resource Banking)

Dwarves bank Rune Counters slowly. You generate Scrap faster (2 per kill). Out-economy them. Spend Scrap to draw cards and reduce costs—play more cards per turn than they can.

vs. The Ossuarium (Attrition)

Ossuarium wants long grindy games. You want flexibility. Use utility mode to cycle cards faster. Kill their Thralls for Scrap. Desperate Measures negates their attrition advantage. Speed beats grinding.

vs. Vestige Bloodlines (Mutation Burst)

Bloodlines snowball mutations. Use attack mode early to kill them before they scale. If they mutate, switch to defense mode. If desperate, Desperate Measures heal negates their burst. Adapt faster than they mutate.

vs. The Wyrd Conclave (Trickery)

Wyrd steal and manipulate. Your multi-mode cards are flexible—even if they steal one mode, you have 2 others. Generate Scrap (they can't steal resources). Consistency beats tricks.
