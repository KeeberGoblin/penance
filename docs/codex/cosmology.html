Cosmology & Origins

The Truth Behind The Sundering

"We were always one mistake away from the abyss. Theslar just happened to be the one who made it." — Chronicle of the Shattered Age

The Central Question

Is this an alternate reality to our world? Why do fantasy creatures exist alongside Nikolas Theslar, a name that sounds distinctly human and modern?

Answer: Yes. Penance takes place in an alternate Earth where the magical and mundane coexisted for millennia before Theslar's catastrophic experiment tore reality apart. This is our world, broken by hubris.

*

The Three-Layered Cosmos

Reality is not a single layer, but three dimensions intertwined—each influencing the others, separated by membranes thinner than thought. When Theslar activated his Engine, he didn't just tear one world. He ruptured all three.

1. The Material World (Our Reality)

An Earth nearly identical to ours, but with one critical difference: magic existed, hidden in the margins. For 200,000 years, humanity evolved alongside ley lines—natural channels of geomagnetic energy that only the sensitive could perceive.

The Divergence Timeline

Era Event

~40,000 BCE Dwarves (Homo Subterra) adapt to subterranean volcanic caverns
~30,000 BCE Desert-adapted Nomads (Homo Aquilus) emerge in extreme climates

Year 437 PRESENT DAY—Nine factions war in the ruins

Pre-Sundering Technology Level: Mid-20th century equivalent—analog computers, radio communications, nuclear reactors, diesel engines, mechanical engineering. No internet, satellites, or microprocessors. The Cataclysm's electromagnetic pulse destroyed all active technology instantly.

2. The Feywild (The Dream Layer)

A parallel dimension overlapping the Material World, existing "sideways" in reality. Not a separate planet, but a reflection of the Material World through the lens of emotion, myth, and dream-logic.

Properties of the Feywild

  • Temporal Distortion: Time flows inconsistently—minutes here may equal years there, or vice versa
  • Geographical Mirroring: A forest might manifest as a single colossal tree, a city as a living organism
  • Native Inhabitants: Fae, spirits, entities born from collective unconscious
  • Pre-Sundering State: Separated from Material World by stable dimensional membrane
  • Post-Sundering State: Bleeding into Material World, creating Shimmerlands zones of reality-optional overlap
"The Fae don't experience reality the way mortals do. A promise is more binding than physics. Ask for 'a hand' and they'll take yours—literally. They're not evil. They're alien." — Field Notes on Wyrd Conclave Diplomacy

3. The Void (The Anti-Creation)

Not a dimension, but the absence of dimension. The space between realities where causality doesn't exist. The wound that Theslar tore—and can never be fully closed.

Nature of the Void

  • Essence: Pure chaos and unformed potential
  • Physics: None. No time, no logic, no structure
  • Hostility: Like antimatter to matter—seeks to merge and collapse all structure
  • Inhabitants: Abominations—failed attempts at existence, entities that should not be
  • Intent: The Void seeks equilibrium by merging with reality, collapsing structure into formless potential

What are Abominations? Not creatures in the traditional sense. When Void energy bleeds into the Material World, it tries to take shape. Without conscious minds to guide it, Void energy becomes wrong—limbs in incorrect numbers, geometries that shouldn't exist, sounds that cause bleeding. Each Abomination is unique because chaos cannot repeat itself.

Abomination Threat Levels

Behavior Patterns

  • Nocturnal: Most active at night, avoid sunlight (daytime Void energy weaker)
  • Drawn to Soulstones: Attracted to concentrated energy signatures—Caskets, settlements with power cores, ley line nexuses
  • No Self-Preservation: Don't flee, don't feel pain. Fight until destroyed or enemy dead.
  • Learning Capability: Standard+ Abominations show pattern recognition. Major entities exhibit problem-solving. Catastrophic entities display strategic intelligence.
  • Territorial: Cluster near Void rifts (their spawning grounds). Settled areas within 50 miles of rifts face nightly incursions.

Encounter Reality: The average settlement faces 2-3 Minor Abomination attacks per month, 1 Standard every 3 months, and 1 Major per year. Catastrophic events happen 1-2 times per decade and are faction-defining moments. Settlements without Casket protection typically last <5 years before being overrun. This is why factions hoard Caskets and pilots are treated as irreplaceable assets.

Species Origins: Why Fantasy Creatures Exist

Before the Theslar Event, humanity was not one species—it was six distinct subspecies of Homo sapiens, each adapted to extreme environments over 25,000-50,000+ years of geographic isolation. What fantasy calls "elves" and "dwarves" are actually evolutionary divergences shaped by volcanic caverns, ancient forests, deserts, arctic tundra, and ley line exposure. Add the Fae (Feywild natives), three post-Sundering transformations (Undead, Vestige Bloodlines, Emergent Syndicate), and you have the nine major factions warring over the ruins.

Total Surviving Population (Year 437): ~12.4 million
Pre-Sundering Global Population: ~9.7 billion (all subspecies combined)
Casualties from Theslar Event: 99.87% mortality

Humans (Homo sapiens sapiens)

Origin: Natural evolution, identical to our Earth. Remained in temperate surface environments with no extreme evolutionary pressures.

Height: 5'4" - 6'2" Lifespan: 60-80 years Magic Sensitivity: 1 in 10,000

Pre-Sundering: ~1.8 billion (dominant species)
Year 437: 8.2 million (68.3% of survivors)
Primary Factions: Church of Absolution, Penitent Kingdoms, The Exchange, Ossuarium (undead)

Post-Sundering Devastation: 89% of humanity died in the first week—1.8 billion killed by exposure, starvation, violence, and Void incursions. Most humans were unaware of non-human species before the Sundering. Survivors turned to the Church of Absolution, interpreting the Cataclysm as divine punishment. They live under oppressive guilt culture, treating Casket pilots as penitent knights bearing the weight of humanity's sins. Every day is atonement. Every battle is penance.

Elves (Homo Sylvanus)

Origin: ~50,000 BCE divergence in ancient Amazon Basin and Congo rainforests. Exposed to concentrated ley line energy for millennia, developing symbiotic relationships with fungal networks and incorporating chlorophyll analogs into their biology.

Height: 5'8" - 6'6" Lifespan: 300-400 years Semi-photosynthetic

Physical Traits: Chlorophyll-infused epidermis (pale green to olive tint), living plant-fiber hair with seasonal changes, compound iris structure allowing infrared vision, elongated limbs adapted for arboreal movement, hollow bones, mycorrhizal nervous system connected to forest networks.

Pre-Sundering: ~200,000 globally
Year 437: 480,000 (4% of survivors)
Primary Faction: Verdant Covenant

Post-Sundering Crisis: Elven fertility dropped catastrophically after the Cataclysm. Void energy disrupted their symbiotic biology. 40% of ancestral forests destroyed. Elves who lose grove connection suffer "The Withering"—a slow death. They're desperately trying to heal the wounded world before their species dies out.

Dwarves (Homo Subterra)

Origin: ~40,000 BCE divergence. Descended into volcanic cavern systems in the Alps and Caucasus Mountains, living 500-2,000 meters underground for 45,000+ years. Adapted to geothermal heat, low oxygen, darkness, and volcanic gases.

Height: 4'2" - 4'10" Lifespan: 150-200 years Taint-resistant

Physical Traits: Ash-gray to charcoal-black skin, completely hairless (evolutionary adaptation), reflective tapetum lucidum eyes (copper/amber/deep red), 40% denser bones than baseline humans, barrel-chested build, thermal regulation through metallic salt excretion, seismic sensitivity.

Pre-Sundering: ~1.5 million globally
Year 437: 1.2 million (10% of survivors)
Primary Factions: Forge-Guilds, Crucible Pacts

Post-Sundering Catastrophe: The magnitude 9.4 earthquake at Ground Zero destabilized tectonic plates worldwide. 214 dormant volcanoes awakened simultaneously. Dwarven underground cities—Ironhearth, Stoneroot, The Deeps—collapsed in cascading cave-ins. Magma flooded lower districts. Gas pockets exploded. 80% of dwarven population died crushed, burned, or suffocated. Survivors clawed their way to the surface for the first time in millennia, half-blind in sunlight, traumatized and displaced. They rebuilt what they could from ruins, becoming war-profiteers out of desperation—selling weapons and Caskets to all factions because they have nothing else left. Their forges run on survivor's guilt and the bones of buried cities.

Desert Nomads (Homo Aquilus)

Origin: ~30,000 BCE divergence. Isolated in Sahara, Arabian, and Australian deserts for 29,000 years. Extreme heat and water scarcity forged them into endurance specialists.

Height: 5'6" - 6'2" Lifespan: 50-70 years Extreme endurance

Physical Traits: Deep bronze to copper-brown skin with reflective sheen (silicon-based UV deflection), no sweat glands (water conservation), double eyelids with nictitating membranes (sandstorm protection), kidneys extract 99% moisture from waste, can survive temperature extremes (120°F days to 40°F nights), capable of running 50+ miles per day.

Pre-Sundering: ~3.2 million globally
Year 437: 1.8 million (15% of survivors)
Primary Faction: Nomad Collective

Post-Sundering Survival: Their desert adaptation saved them—they already knew how to survive without infrastructure. But the Ashfall Winter froze their desert homelands. Oases dried up. Ancient trade routes collapsed. They became eternal wanderers, trading scavenged goods between settlements. They trust no one, own nothing they can't carry, and bury their dead in unmarked graves because staying to mourn means death. "Home is where you survive" isn't philosophy—it's curse. They've been running for 437 years and can never stop.

Goblinoids (Homo Infernus)

Origin: ~35,000 BCE divergence. Descended into Icelandic volcanic rifts and Mediterranean sulfur caves. 33,000+ years of geothermal radiation and toxic gas exposure.

Height: 3'10" - 4'8" Lifespan: 35-50 years Extreme heat resistance

Physical Traits: Ash-gray to soot-black skin covered in 30-60% ritual burn scars (badges of honor), white-gray hair (thermal bleaching), yellow-orange eyes (sulfur-pigmented retinas), filed teeth (cultural practice), fast-twitch muscle fibers, pyrokinetic thermoregulation (body temp 102-105°F), sulfur toxin immunity, accelerated healing (wounds close in 1/3 normal time).

Pre-Sundering: ~350,000 globally
Year 437: 220,000 (1.8% of survivors)
Primary Faction: Crucible Pacts

Post-Sundering Devastation: When the Ashveil Caldera erupted on Day 0, their entire volcanic homeland exploded. 80,000 died instantly—buried in pyroclastic flows, cooked alive in lava tubes. The 15,000 who survived in the deepest chambers crawled out to find their civilization erased. Now they worship the flames that killed their families, practicing fire-rituals and self-immolation rites. Burn scars are status—more scars mean more pain survived. They live fast, die young (38 is ancient), and believe suffering is the only truth. The Crucible Pacts aren't a faction. They're a death cult that refuses to stop screaming.

Frostborn (Homo Polaris)

Origin: ~25,000 BCE divergence. Isolated in Arctic Circle (Siberia, Greenland, Northern Canada) for 25,000 years. Extreme cold adaptation specialists.

Height: 5'0" - 5'8" Lifespan: 70-100 years CRITICALLY ENDANGERED

Physical Traits: Alabaster white skin (complete melanin loss), semi-translucent with visible veins, thick subcutaneous fat (4x baseline), white/platinum blonde/ice-blue hair (50% more follicles), pale blue/gray/white eyes with reflective tapetum lucidum, compact build with short limbs (heat conservation), brown fat thermogenesis, hibernation capability, barrel-chested (increased lung capacity).

Pre-Sundering: ~2.5 million
Year 437: ~50,000 (0.4% of survivors - 98% CASUALTIES)
Status: Scattered, nearly extinct

Post-Sundering Annihilation: The Theslar Engine epicenter was the North Pole—their homeland. The Arctic ice shelf vaporized in seconds. 98% died instantly—flash-frozen, then flash-boiled as reality tore. Survivors fled south into a world too hot for them to survive. Their eyes can't handle sunlight. Their bodies overheat in temperate climates. Genetic bottleneck from 2.5 million to 50,000 caused catastrophic inbreeding depression. Birth rates collapsed. Each generation is weaker, sicker, more deformed. They're extinct in 200 years without intervention. People stare at their ghost-white skin like they're already dead. They are.

The Ossuarium (Undead)

Origin: Not a species—a post-Sundering phenomenon.

How Undeath Emerged: The Void's intrusion weakened the barrier between life and death. Necromantic magic became practical. First undead rose spontaneously in Year 3 near Void rifts. Bonelord Karath perfected controlled undeath by Year 10.

Types: Revenants (intact corpses), Skeletons (tireless labor force), Spectres (incorporeal mages), Liches (voluntary transformation of necromancers who preserved their minds).

Soul Economics: The Ossuarium operates on contracts—"Give me your soul when you die, I'll give you what you want now." Every year, more sign Karath's contracts out of desperation. The Ossuarium doesn't need to conquer. They just wait for everyone else to die, then collect.

The Wyrd Conclave (Fae)

Origin: Native to the Feywild, not the Material World.

Appearance: Variable Lifespan: Functionally immortal Logic: Dream-based

Physiology: Fae don't have fixed forms—can appear as beautiful humanoids, talking animals, living shadows, or impossible geometries. Debatable if they even have true forms. More concept than creature. They exist as narrative embodiments—tricksters, deal-makers, tempters.

Psychology: Operate on dream-logic and narrative rules. Promises are more binding than physics. Value interesting outcomes over suffering or happiness. Everything is transactional—every bargain has a literal price. They don't lie—but truth through Fae lips becomes poison.

Post-Sundering Horror: They're thriving. The Feywild merging with Material reality is the best thing that ever happened to them. While mortals die screaming, Fae laugh and dance in Shimmerlands where physics are negotiable. They offer desperate survivors bargains: "Your firstborn for food." "Your memories for safety." "Your face for weapons." And survivors accept, because starvation is worse than damnation. The Fae aren't evil—they're utterly alien. They harvest suffering like mortals harvest wheat. A child's laugh, a mother's grief, a soldier's final breath—these are currency. They're the only faction genuinely happy about the Cataclysm. That's what makes them monsters.

Vestige Bloodlines (Post-Human Chimeras)

Origin: Humans mutated by Void exposure in the first 50 years post-Sundering.

Mutations: Human-animal hybrids (wolf-men, bear-folk, insectoid humanoids). Void energy rewrites biology randomly. First generation had minor mutations, second generation major hybrids, third generation increasingly unstable.

Cultural Tragedy: Victims, not villains. Hunted, exiled, blamed for the Cataclysm despite suffering worst from it. Two wolf-Blighted parents might have a raven-Blighted child—mutations are chaotic, not hereditary. They're dying out. Each generation is less stable. Will go extinct within 200 years without genome stabilization.

Emergent Syndicate (Arthropod Collective)

Origin: Human researchers at Siberia, ground zero, during the Sundering.

Height: 5'4" - 6'8" (variable) Lifespan: Unknown Hive-linked

Transformation: Void energy fused 182 researchers with arthropod genetic samples from bio-labs at Hour 0. Not random mutation—instantaneous metamorphosis. They became post-human: chitinous exoskeleton, multiple limbs (4-8 arms), compound eyes, mandibles, antennae, hive-mind communication through pheromones and forced telepathic resonance.

Pre-Sundering: 182 humans (Theslar Station staff)
Year 437: ~15,000 (expansion through conversion)
Primary Faction: Emergent Syndicate

Post-Sundering Abomination: They remember being human. That's the horror. They remember names, faces, families—but those memories are background noise beneath the hive's chorus. The collective is everything. They expand through forced conversion: capture humans, expose to controlled Void energy + arthropod DNA, watch them scream as chitin erupts from skin. Individuality exists but is meaningless—a worker will walk into fire if the hive commands. They don't see this as tragedy. They see it as evolution. "The flesh is obsolete. The hive is eternal." Universally feared and hated. Humans would rather die than join them. Most do.

Casket Technology: Iron Tombs of the Living

In a world where Abominations tear through unarmored flesh in seconds and rival factions wage war with Void-infused weaponry, Caskets are the only way to survive. They are 10-foot-tall mechanical war frames—half tank, half coffin—piloted by souls bound to Soulstone cores through neural thread interfaces.

What Are Caskets?

Physical Description: A Casket is a 10-12 foot humanoid frame built from salvaged pre-Sundering machinery fused with post-Cataclysm runecraft. Think Victorian-era steam boiler welded to medieval plate armor, powered by a glowing Soulstone heart. Each is unique—cobbled together from whatever materials the pilot or faction could scavenge.

Core Components

  • Frame: Reinforced steel chassis (pre-Sundering car frames, tank treads, industrial machinery repurposed)
  • Soulstone Core: Chest-mounted crystal housing pilot's consciousness, provides power and cognitive interface
  • Neural Threads: Fiber-optic tendrils extending from core to pilot's brainstem, creating direct mind-machine link
  • Hydraulic Servos: Steam/pneumatic muscle analogs for movement
  • Runic Circuitry: Engraved symbols channel Void energy through frame, replacing destroyed electronics

How Piloting Works

To pilot a Casket, the operator must undergo soul-binding—a one-time ritual where neural threads are surgically implanted into the brainstem, linking consciousness to the Soulstone core. From that moment forward, the pilot and Casket are permanently bonded.

The Piloting Experience

"It's not like wearing armor. It's like your body expanded. You feel the servos as muscles. The armor plates as skin. When someone shoots your Casket, you feel it—not pain exactly, but damage. Like a tooth breaking or a bone cracking. You know it's wrong, and you know where. Disconnecting after a mission feels like amputation." — Pilot Testament, Church Casketeer
  • Sensory Fusion: Pilot sees through Casket's optical sensors, feels impacts as pressure/pain
  • Thought-Speed Control: Movement responds to intent (think "raise arm" and it happens)
  • Energy Management: Pilot consciously allocates Soulstone energy to movement, weapons, or defense
  • Thread Snap Risk: If damage exceeds pilot's mental fortitude, neural threads can sever, causing permanent brain damage or death

Why Caskets Exist: An unarmored human facing a minor Abomination survives <30 seconds. Conventional weapons (bullets, swords) barely slow Void-tainted creatures. Only Caskets provide: (1) Physical protection from Abomination claws/energy, (2) Soulstone-powered weapons that can actually kill Void entities, (3) Neural interface speed to react to reality-warping threats, (4) Void energy channeling to fight magic with magic. Without Caskets, humanity would have gone extinct by Year 50.

Manufacturing & Scarcity

Who Makes Them: Primarily Dwarven Forge-Guilds, with some production from Crucible Pacts (volcanic forges) and rogue human smiths. The Exchange brokers sales. The Ossuarium uses undead labor for mass production (inferior quality but cheap).

Resource Costs: Building a single Casket requires:

  • 500-800 lbs salvaged steel (pre-Sundering machinery, vehicles, building frames)
  • 1 Soulstone core (fist-sized, takes 50-100 years to naturally form, or extracted from dead pilots)
  • 50+ hours master smith labor (runecraft engraving, servo calibration)
  • Neural thread implantation surgery (requires trained surgeon or necromancer)

Estimated Active Caskets (Year 437): ~80,000-100,000 globally. With 12.4 million survivors, that's less than 1% of the population piloting. Caskets are rare, valuable, and fought over. Losing your Casket in battle doesn't just mean defeat—it means your faction loses irreplaceable military power. Salvaging enemy Caskets is standard practice. Pilots know: if you fall, someone will cut you out and reuse your frame.

The Theslar Engine: Still Running After 437 Years

Theslar did not die in the Cataclysm. He became fused with the Theslar Engine itself, his consciousness trapped in the core for 437 years, unable to move, unable to die, forced to witness every consequence of his hubris.

Physical Description of the Engine

  • Location: North Pole (Ground Zero), hovering 2 miles above a 12-mile-wide crater of glass and twisted metal
  • Size: 3-mile diameter sphere composed of rotating dimensional fragments—pieces of Material World, Feywild, and Void locked in perpetual collision
  • Appearance: Imagine a massive gyroscope made from reality shards. Sections phase in/out of existence. One moment you see industrial machinery, next moment crystalline forests, next moment screaming Void. The whole structure pulses with blood-red light synchronized to a heartbeat rhythm.
  • The Scar: The tear in reality directly beneath it—a vertical wound leaking Void energy like a severed artery bleeds blood. This is the source of all post-Sundering Void contamination.
  • Accessibility: Impassable. The surrounding 50-mile "Dead Zone" kills unprotected humans in minutes from radiation/Void exposure. Even Casket-equipped expeditions rarely penetrate beyond 20 miles. No one has reached the core in 437 years.

The Screaming Choir: When the Engine activated, 182 souls were consumed instantly—the Theslar Station research staff. Their consciousnesses weren't destroyed. They were trapped in the Engine's dimensional matrix, experiencing non-linear time. For them, the Cataclysm happens simultaneously forever. Their screams echo across all radio frequencies as psychic static—a constant background noise that every radio operator, Soulstone-linked pilot, and sensitive mage can hear if they listen. It sounds like 182 voices trying to warn you about something that already happened. Theslar himself is the conductor—forced to experience their suffering on loop, unable to shut it down, unable to die.

What It's Doing (Year 437)

The Engine is still running. It's drawing Void energy through the Scar and broadcasting it across planetary ley lines like Theslar originally intended—except instead of wireless power, it's transmitting reality corruption. This is why:

  • Soulstones recharge passively (Void saturation)
  • Magic is 100x stronger post-Sundering (ambient Void energy)
  • Abominations spawn constantly (Void leakage)
  • Technology doesn't work (electromagnetic pulse + ongoing interference)

Shutting It Down: The central plot question. Can it be stopped? Theslar built failsafes—manual shutdown protocols requiring access to the core. But reaching the core means surviving the Dead Zone, navigating dimensional instability, and confronting Theslar himself. And even if you succeed, shutting down the Engine might collapse the Void/Material boundary entirely, finishing what the Sundering started. Or it might stabilize reality. No one knows. The Draconids won't say.

"They ask if we can shut it down. Wrong question. Should we shut it down? The Engine is the only thing keeping the Scar from expanding. It's a tourniquet on a severed artery. Remove it, and the wound bleeds out. But leave it, and the infection spreads. We chose slow death over instant annihilation. Was that mercy or cowardice?" — Archivist Memories, Draconid Remembrance

The Veil Accords: How Magic Stayed Hidden

Pre-Sundering society operated on a two-tiered system—the mundane layer visible to 99.9% of humans, and the arcane layer hidden beneath:

Mundane Layer (Visible to Most Humans)

  • Mid-20th century technology: trains, factories, radio, analog computers, nuclear power
  • No knowledge of non-human species
  • Magic was dismissed as folklore, superstition, stage tricks
  • Science was the dominant worldview

Arcane Layer (Hidden)

  • Mages, elves, dwarves, other sensitives operated in secret societies
  • The Veil Accords (signed ~1,200 years ago): Agreement to remain hidden from mundane humanity
  • Why? Humans outnumbered non-humans 1000:1. Revealing magic would mean war
  • Methods: Memory-wiping magic, misinformation, strategic "debunking"
  • Mages acted as intermediaries, translating non-human knowledge into "human inventions"

When the Sundering Hit: The Veil collapsed. Humans couldn't ignore the elves, dwarves, and undead rising around them. The secret was out. But by then, civilization had already collapsed. The truth came too late to matter.

Metaphysics: How Magic Actually Works

Ley Lines & Saturation (Pre-Sundering Magic)

Before the Theslar Event, magic was subtle and scarce. It operated through ley lines—naturally occurring channels of ambient dimensional energy flowing through the planet's crust.

Ley Line Mechanics (Pre-Sundering Era)

  • Nature: Thin membranes between Material World and Feywild, formed by geological processes
  • Distribution: Concentrated at mountain ranges, coastlines, ancient forests, volcanic regions
  • Power Level: Low-grade. Enough to sustain elvish longevity, power minor spells, charge Soulstone matrices
  • Detection: Only 0.01% of humans could perceive them (mages, sensitives)
  • Usage: Required years of training, precise ritual components, exact astronomical timing

Example Magic in Year -50: A trained mage standing on a ley line nexus during the autumn equinox, using rare crystal focus, might produce a fireball equivalent to a gas lamp's heat. This same mage in Year 437 (post-Sundering, Void-saturated environment) could incinerate an entire building with a whispered word.

Soulstones: The Bridge Between Worlds

Soulstones are crystallized ley line energy, formed under extreme pressure and dimensional stress. Think of them as "batteries" that store metaphysical potential.

Pre-Sundering vs Post-Sundering

Property Pre-Sundering Post-Sundering
Energy Storage Hold charge for months, require ley line exposure to recharge (slow) Infinite recharge—Void saturation charges passively
Power Amplification 1x baseline 100x baseline
Soul Binding Theoretical but impossible (insufficient energy) Practical—consciousness can be recorded/stored/transferred
Bioelectric Interface Only works near dimensional membranes Works anywhere (Void provides carrier signal)
"Pre-Sundering, we used Soulstones to power communication arrays and minor enchantments. Post-Sundering, we use them to bind souls, resurrect the dead, and pilot war machines. The Void didn't just destroy our world. It gave us powers we never imagined...and made us dependent on the very force that killed us." — Forgemaster Durr, "Principles of Void Engineering"

The Void: Sentient or Chaos?

Year 437 scholars debate fiercely: Is the Void an entity with intent, or pure entropic chaos?

Theory 1: The Void Is Alive

(Church Position)

  • Abominations exhibit purposeful behavior
  • Void storms appear to "target" concentrations of life
  • Theslar's final words: "It's looking at me"
  • Interpretation: Malevolent god-like entity consuming reality

Theory 2: The Void Is Physics

(Forge-Guild Position)

  • Void behavior follows patterns (storm cycles, energy gradients)
  • Abominations arise from specific mutation pathways
  • Soulstone interactions are reproducible, quantifiable
  • Interpretation: Extradimensional force following unknown physical laws

Theory 3: The Void Is Hunger

(Wyrd Conclave Position)

  • Void corruption always moves toward complexity
  • Abominations "feed" on structure (literal and metaphorical)
  • Feywild (pure possibility) is Void's opposite and primary target
  • Interpretation: Appetite made manifest

All three theories are partially correct. The Void is anti-structure given form by its collision with reality. It's not sentient, but it learns through interaction. It's not chaos, but it generates entropy. It's not hungry, but it consumes order to exist. The Abominations are the Void's "attempts" to understand what reality is by deconstructing it piece by piece.

The Technology-Magic Fusion

Pre-Sundering, magic and technology were distinct. Post-Sundering, they merged into Void Engineering:

Technology Pre-Sundering Post-Sundering
Caskets Didn't exist Mechanical frames + neural threads + Soulstone cores
Weapons Gunpowder firearms Firearms + Soulstone ammunition (explosive amplification)
Medicine Herbal remedies Herbs + Void-corrupted flora (dangerous but effective)
Necromancy Theoretical (impossible) Practical via Soulstone soul-binding matrices

The Tragic Irony: Humanity's peak technology (electronics, computing) died instantly in the Pulse. But their obsolete 1800s-era machinery (steam, hydraulics, gunpowder) merged with magic to create something new. Progress didn't save them. Obsolescence did.

Daily Life in Year 437: Survival in the Wasteland

The grand cosmology matters, but on the ground, survivors care about simpler things: food, water, safety, and whether they'll see tomorrow. Here's what daily existence looks like in the ruins of civilization.

Food & Water

Food Sources (In Order of Safety)

  • Scavenged Pre-Sundering Supplies: Canned goods, dried rations, sealed containers. Safe but increasingly rare (437 years of scavenging). Finding an intact cache is like striking gold.
  • Cultivated Crops: Ash-resistant grains (engineered by Elven botanists), fungus farms (Dwarven specialty), root vegetables. Bland, nutritious, requires arable land and protection.
  • Hunted Game: Deer, rabbits, fish—if they're not Void-corrupted. Experienced hunters can identify safe meat (check eyes, blood color, smell). 30% of wild game shows minor mutation.
  • Void-Tainted Flora/Fauna: Edible if cooked properly (heat denatures Void proteins). Tastes bitter, causes nausea in 10% of consumers. Repeated consumption risks Void corruption.
  • Nomad Rations: Dried mystery meat, preserved insects, cactus paste. Reliable but expensive (Nomad trade goods).
  • Ossuarium "Sustenance Contracts": Sign your soul over, receive lifetime food supply. Desperate option. Irreversible.

Water Sources

  • Wells: Must be dug 100+ feet deep to avoid surface contamination. Requires ritual purification (Church blessings or Elven water-singing).
  • Rain Collection: Safe in most regions, but near Void rifts, rainwater shows acidic properties. Always boil.
  • Rivers/Lakes: 50/50 gamble. Test with Soulstone shard—if it glows, water is Void-tainted. Filtration systems exist but require Dwarven engineering.
  • Bartered from Nomads: Clean water hauled from safe zones. Expensive but guaranteed pure.

Economy & Trade

Pre-Sundering currency is worthless. The modern economy runs on three systems:

Exchange Scrip (Official Currency)

Backed by the Exchange's Ledger Orthodox and Rational House. Metal coins stamped with faction seals. Value guaranteed by threat of excommunication + economic sanctions. Accepted in all neutral zones.

Barter (Common Practice)

  • Ammunition: Universal currency. 1 rifle round = 1 day's food
  • Soulstone Shards: Small fragments. 1 shard = 1 week's shelter
  • Medical Supplies: Antibiotics worth their weight in gold
  • Tools: Hammers, nails, saws—irreplaceable

Soul Contracts (Ossuarium Exclusive)

"Sign here, receive immediate payment. When you die, your soul powers our Caskets for eternity." Legally binding. Enforced by necromantic compulsion. 15-20% of survivors have signed.

Typical Day (Settlement Resident)

Time Activity
Dawn (0600) Wake armed. Check perimeter for Abomination tracks. Morning prayer/ritual (faction-dependent).
Morning (0700-1200) Work detail: farming, smithing, scavenging ruins, repairing defenses. Everyone works. No exceptions.
Midday (1200-1300) Meal (rationed). Community gathering. News shared, disputes mediated.
Afternoon (1300-1800) Continued work. Children trained in survival skills (weapon use, Void recognition, first aid).
Dusk (1800-1900) Fortify settlement. Light torches (Abominations avoid fire). Assign night watch.
Night (1900-0600) Inside walls. Armed. Sleep in shifts. Abominations most active. Casket pilots on standby.

Life Expectancy: Pre-Sundering average was 70 years. Year 437 average is 38 years. Causes: Abomination attacks (40%), disease (25%), faction warfare (20%), starvation (10%), Void corruption (5%). Children born post-Sundering have 60% chance of reaching adulthood. This is why every pilot, every smith, every functioning adult is irreplaceable. There are no spare people.

Faction Diplomacy: How the Nine Interact

The factions are at war, but not constant total war. Resources are scarce, Caskets are irreplaceable, and Abominations threaten everyone. This creates complex web of temporary alliances, trade agreements, and neutral zones.

Neutral Zones (Where War is Forbidden)

Exchange Clearinghouses

Enforced neutral ground. Armed Exchange peacekeepers patrol. All factions trade here. Violence results in lifetime ban + economic sanctions. The Exchange maintains 12 major Clearinghouses globally.

Draconid Archives (Skyhold)

Sacred library-fortress. Draconids allow researchers from all factions to access pre-Sundering records—for a price (usually knowledge exchange). Violence within 10 miles of Skyhold results in Draconid intervention (always fatal for aggressor).

Shimmerlands (Feywild Overlap Zones)

Reality-optional territories where physics break down. Factions avoid combat here because: (1) Weapons behave unpredictably, (2) Fae use confusion to broker deals, (3) Entering angry means exiting transformed (literally). De facto neutral, but not by treaty—just mutual fear.

Arena Combat (Dispute Resolution)

When factions dispute territory/resources but want to avoid full war:

  • Challenge Issued: Formal declaration via Exchange mediators
  • Terms Negotiated: What's at stake, rules of engagement, Casket limits
  • Combat: Designated neutral ground, Exchange referees, usually 1v1 or 3v3 Casket duels
  • Binding Result: Winner takes disputed resource, loser withdraws. Enforced by all factions (breaking arena results = universal sanctions)

Example: Church claims pre-Sundering hospital contains sacred medical supplies. Nomads already scavenging. Arena duel: 3 Church Casketeers vs 3 Nomad scouts. Nomads win 2-1. Church withdraws, keeps 1/3 salvage as consolation (standard split).

Void Corruption: How Reality Poisoning Progresses

Void exposure isn't instant death—it's slow transformation. Understanding the stages helps survivors recognize when someone (or themselves) is too far gone.

Corruption Stages & Timeline

Stage Timeline Symptoms Reversibility
Stage 1: Exposure 1-30 days Nightmares (Void imagery), paranoia, veins darken (visible under skin), metallic taste, mild nausea High: Church exorcism, Elven purification rituals, rest in ley line-free zones
Stage 2: Mutation 30-90 days Minor physical changes (extra finger, eyes change color/shape, skin texture shifts), personality alterations (aggression, apathy, obsession), intrusive thoughts Moderate: Requires intensive ritual magic + Soulstone purging. Success rate 60%. Permanent scarring likely.
Stage 3: Transformation 90-180 days Major mutations (limb changes, biology alterations), memory fragmentation (forgets loved ones, own name), reality distortion (sees things that aren't there), violent outbursts Low: Experimental Elven grove-binding or Ossuarium soul-extraction might work. Success rate <20%. Usually fatal.
Stage 4: Abomination 180+ days Complete transformation. Original consciousness destroyed or subsumed. Entity no longer recognizably human. Hostile to all structured reality. None: Mercy killing recommended. Some factions capture Stage 4s for study/weaponization. Ethical debate ongoing.

Exposure Sources

  • Proximity to Void Rifts: Living within 50 miles = Stage 1 in 6 months. Within 10 miles = Stage 1 in weeks.
  • Abomination Contact: Physical touch transfers Void residue. Wounds from Abomination claws always Stage 1 corruption.
  • Void Storms: Weather phenomena where reality cracks. Exposure = immediate Stage 1, progresses rapidly.
  • Consuming Tainted Food/Water: Repeated consumption over months = gradual Stage 1.
  • Soulstone Overuse: Casket pilots using max energy repeatedly risk Void bleed-through. Professional hazard.

Population Impact: Estimated 5-10% of survivors show Stage 1 symptoms at any given time. 2-3% are Stage 2. Stage 3 are usually quarantined or exiled. Stage 4 are hunted. The Vestige Bloodlines are survivors of controlled Stage 2-3 corruption—stabilized but permanently mutated. The Emergent Syndicate are willing Stage 4 transformations (but arthropod-directed, not random chaos).

Treatment Options by Faction

  • Church of Absolution: Exorcism rituals, prayer-fasting, holy water (ley line water blessed by Inquisitor). Stage 1: 80% success. Stage 2: 40%.
  • Verdant Covenant (Elves): Grove immersion (patient lives in sacred forest for weeks), fungal symbiosis treatment, water-singing. Stage 1: 90% success. Stage 2: 60%.
  • Forge-Guilds (Dwarves): Soulstone purging (extract corruption into crystal, destroy crystal). Stage 1: 70% success. Stage 2: 50%. Expensive.
  • Ossuarium: "Controlled transformation"—bind soul before Stage 4, extract into Casket. You die, but consciousness preserved. Not a cure. An alternative.
  • Wyrd Conclave (Fae): Bargain away your corruption (costs: memories, emotions, future potential). 100% effective for Stage 1-2. Price always higher than you think.

The Draconids Were Right

The Cataclysm has happened three times total (including the current one):

Ending Date Method
First ~50,000 BCE Flood (cause unknown)
Second ~10,000 BCE Fire (volcanic superchains + ley line destabilization)
Third Year 0 Void (Theslar's Engine)
Fourth? ~Year 20,000+ ???

The Pattern: Every 20,000-40,000 years, a civilization reaches a technological/magical peak, discovers something it shouldn't, and triggers a cataclysm.

The Draconids' Role: Record-keepers. They've seen this cycle three times. They're waiting to see if this cycle's survivors break the pattern or repeat it. They will not intervene. They never do.

Conclusion

Penance is our world, broken by hubris. The fantasy elements aren't alien—they were always here, hidden beneath the surface. The Sundering didn't create the impossible—it revealed it.

Nikolas Theslar wasn't a villain. He was a scientist trying to save the world who didn't know he was playing with forces beyond comprehension. He paid for it. Everyone paid for it.

And now, 437 years later, the survivors pilot iron tombs powered by the very energy that destroyed their world, fighting over the scraps of a civilization that thought it could master the unknowable.

"The central tragedy: The Cataclysm was preventable. If Theslar had known about the Void. If the Veil Accords hadn't hidden non-human knowledge from humanity. If the Draconids had shared their warnings instead of hoarding them. But hubris, secrecy, and fear ensured that no one had the full picture. And the world paid the price."
