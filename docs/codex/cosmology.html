Cosmology & Origins

The Truth Behind The Sundering

"We were always one mistake away from the abyss. Theslar just happened to be the one who made it." — Chronicle of the Shattered Age

The Central Question

Is this an alternate reality to our world? Why do fantasy creatures exist alongside Nikolas Theslar, a name that sounds distinctly human and modern?

Answer: Yes. Penance takes place in an alternate Earth where the magical and mundane coexisted for millennia before Theslar's catastrophic experiment tore reality apart. This is our world, broken by hubris.

*

The Three-Layered Cosmos

Reality is not a single layer, but three dimensions intertwined—each influencing the others, separated by membranes thinner than thought. When Theslar activated his Engine, he didn't just tear one world. He ruptured all three.

1. The Material World (Our Reality)

An Earth nearly identical to ours, but with one critical difference: magic existed, hidden in the margins. For 200,000 years, humanity evolved alongside ley lines—natural channels of geomagnetic energy that only the sensitive could perceive.

The Divergence Timeline

Era Event
~200,000 BCE Timeline divergence begins—early humans discover ley lines
~50,000 BCE Elves (Homo Sylvanus) diverge from baseline humans through ley line exposure
~40,000 BCE Dwarves (Homo Subterra) adapt to subterranean volcanic caverns
~30,000 BCE Desert-adapted Nomads (Homo Aquilus) emerge in extreme climates
~1,200 years ago The Veil Accords signed—magic and non-humans enter hiding
Year -50 to Year 0 Pre-Sundering civilization reaches 1950s-1970s technology level
Year -43 Nikolas Theslar born (human scientist and engineer)
Year 0 THE SUNDERING—Theslar Engine tears reality apart
Year 437 PRESENT DAY—Nine factions war in the ruins

Pre-Sundering Technology Level: Mid-20th century equivalent—analog computers, radio communications, nuclear reactors, diesel engines, mechanical engineering. No internet, satellites, or microprocessors. The Cataclysm's electromagnetic pulse destroyed all active technology instantly.

2. The Feywild (The Dream Layer)

A parallel dimension overlapping the Material World, existing "sideways" in reality. Not a separate planet, but a reflection of the Material World through the lens of emotion, myth, and dream-logic.

Properties of the Feywild

  • Temporal Distortion: Time flows inconsistently—minutes here may equal years there, or vice versa
  • Geographical Mirroring: A forest might manifest as a single colossal tree, a city as a living organism
  • Native Inhabitants: Fae, spirits, entities born from collective unconscious
  • Pre-Sundering State: Separated from Material World by stable dimensional membrane
  • Post-Sundering State: Bleeding into Material World, creating Shimmerlands zones of reality-optional overlap
"The Fae don't experience reality the way mortals do. A promise is more binding than physics. Ask for 'a hand' and they'll take yours—literally. They're not evil. They're alien." — Field Notes on Wyrd Conclave Diplomacy

3. The Void (The Anti-Creation)

Not a dimension, but the absence of dimension. The space between realities where causality doesn't exist. The wound that Theslar tore—and can never be fully closed.

Nature of the Void

  • Essence: Pure chaos and unformed potential
  • Physics: None. No time, no logic, no structure
  • Hostility: Like antimatter to matter—seeks to merge and collapse all structure
  • Inhabitants: Abominations—failed attempts at existence, entities that should not be
  • Intent: The Void seeks equilibrium by merging with reality, collapsing structure into formless potential

What are Abominations? Not creatures in the traditional sense. When Void energy bleeds into the Material World, it tries to take shape. Without conscious minds to guide it, Void energy becomes wrong—limbs in incorrect numbers, geometries that shouldn't exist, sounds that cause bleeding. Each Abomination is unique because chaos cannot repeat itself.

Species Origins: Why Fantasy Creatures Exist

Before the Theslar Event, humanity was not one species—it was six distinct subspecies of Homo sapiens, each adapted to extreme environments over 50,000+ years of geographic isolation. What fantasy calls "elves" and "dwarves" are actually evolutionary divergences shaped by volcanic caverns, ancient forests, and ley line exposure.

Total Surviving Population (Year 437): ~12.4 million
Pre-Sundering Global Population: ~1.8 billion
Casualties from Theslar Event: 99.3% mortality

Humans (Homo sapiens sapiens)

Origin: Natural evolution, identical to our Earth. Remained in temperate surface environments with no extreme evolutionary pressures.

Height: 5'4" - 6'2" Lifespan: 60-80 years Magic Sensitivity: 1 in 10,000

Pre-Sundering: ~1.8 billion (dominant species)
Year 437: 8.2 million (68.3% of survivors)
Primary Factions: Church of Absolution, Penitent Kingdoms, The Exchange, Ossuarium (undead)

Post-Sundering Devastation: 89% of humanity died in the first week—1.8 billion killed by exposure, starvation, violence, and Void incursions. Most humans were unaware of non-human species before the Sundering. Survivors turned to the Church of Absolution, interpreting the Cataclysm as divine punishment. They live under oppressive guilt culture, treating Casket pilots as penitent knights bearing the weight of humanity's sins. Every day is atonement. Every battle is penance.

Elves (Homo Sylvanus)

Origin: ~50,000 BCE divergence in ancient Amazon Basin and Congo rainforests. Exposed to concentrated ley line energy for millennia, developing symbiotic relationships with fungal networks and incorporating chlorophyll analogs into their biology.

Height: 5'8" - 6'6" Lifespan: 300-400 years Semi-photosynthetic

Physical Traits: Chlorophyll-infused epidermis (pale green to olive tint), living plant-fiber hair with seasonal changes, compound iris structure allowing infrared vision, elongated limbs adapted for arboreal movement, hollow bones, mycorrhizal nervous system connected to forest networks.

Pre-Sundering: ~200,000 globally
Year 437: 480,000 (4% of survivors)
Primary Faction: Verdant Covenant

Post-Sundering Crisis: Elven fertility dropped catastrophically after the Cataclysm. Void energy disrupted their symbiotic biology. 40% of ancestral forests destroyed. Elves who lose grove connection suffer "The Withering"—a slow death. They're desperately trying to heal the wounded world before their species dies out.

Dwarves (Homo Subterra)

Origin: ~40,000 BCE divergence. Descended into volcanic cavern systems in the Alps and Caucasus Mountains, living 500-2,000 meters underground for 45,000+ years. Adapted to geothermal heat, low oxygen, darkness, and volcanic gases.

Height: 4'2" - 4'10" Lifespan: 150-200 years Taint-resistant

Physical Traits: Ash-gray to charcoal-black skin, completely hairless (evolutionary adaptation), reflective tapetum lucidum eyes (copper/amber/deep red), 40% denser bones than baseline humans, barrel-chested build, thermal regulation through metallic salt excretion, seismic sensitivity.

Pre-Sundering: ~1.5 million globally
Year 437: 1.2 million (10% of survivors)
Primary Factions: Forge-Guilds, Crucible Pacts

Post-Sundering Catastrophe: The magnitude 9.4 earthquake at Ground Zero destabilized tectonic plates worldwide. 214 dormant volcanoes awakened simultaneously. Dwarven underground cities—Ironhearth, Stoneroot, The Deeps—collapsed in cascading cave-ins. Magma flooded lower districts. Gas pockets exploded. 80% of dwarven population died crushed, burned, or suffocated. Survivors clawed their way to the surface for the first time in millennia, half-blind in sunlight, traumatized and displaced. They rebuilt what they could from ruins, becoming war-profiteers out of desperation—selling weapons and Caskets to all factions because they have nothing else left. Their forges run on survivor's guilt and the bones of buried cities.

The Ossuarium (Undead)

Origin: Not a species—a post-Sundering phenomenon.

How Undeath Emerged: The Void's intrusion weakened the barrier between life and death. Necromantic magic became practical. First undead rose spontaneously in Year 3 near Void rifts. Bonelord Karath perfected controlled undeath by Year 10.

Types: Revenants (intact corpses), Skeletons (tireless labor force), Spectres (incorporeal mages), Liches (voluntary transformation of necromancers who preserved their minds).

Soul Economics: The Ossuarium operates on contracts—"Give me your soul when you die, I'll give you what you want now." Every year, more sign Karath's contracts out of desperation. The Ossuarium doesn't need to conquer. They just wait for everyone else to die, then collect.

The Wyrd Conclave (Fae)

Origin: Native to the Feywild, not the Material World.

Appearance: Variable Lifespan: Functionally immortal Logic: Dream-based

Physiology: Fae don't have fixed forms—can appear as beautiful humanoids, talking animals, living shadows, or impossible geometries. Debatable if they even have true forms. More concept than creature.

Psychology: Operate on dream-logic and narrative rules. Promises are more binding than physics. Value interesting outcomes over suffering or happiness. Everything is transactional—every bargain has a literal price.

Post-Sundering: Thriving. The Feywild merging with the Material World is the best thing that ever happened to them. They're the only faction genuinely happy about the Cataclysm.

Vestige Bloodlines (Post-Human Chimeras)

Origin: Humans mutated by Void exposure in the first 50 years post-Sundering.

Mutations: Human-animal hybrids (wolf-men, bear-folk, insectoid humanoids). Void energy rewrites biology randomly. First generation had minor mutations, second generation major hybrids, third generation increasingly unstable.

Cultural Tragedy: Victims, not villains. Hunted, exiled, blamed for the Cataclysm despite suffering worst from it. Two wolf-Blighted parents might have a raven-Blighted child—mutations are chaotic, not hereditary. They're dying out. Each generation is less stable. Will go extinct within 200 years without genome stabilization.

Emergent Syndicate (Arthropod Collective)

Origin: Human researchers at Siberia, ground zero, during the Sundering.

Transformation: Void energy fused researchers with arthropod genetic samples from bio-labs. Not random mutation—metamorphosis. They became post-human: exoskeleton, multiple limbs (4-8 arms), compound eyes, hive-mind communication through pheromones and telepathy.

Psychology: Still have human memories, but distant and irrelevant. Individuality exists but is secondary to hive efficiency. A single member will sacrifice itself without hesitation if it benefits the collective.

Post-Sundering: Expanding through conversion—capture humans, expose to controlled Void + arthropod DNA. Universally feared and hated. They view their transformation as inevitable evolution—the only path to survival in a Void-tainted world.

The Veil Accords: How Magic Stayed Hidden

Pre-Sundering society operated on a two-tiered system—the mundane layer visible to 99.9% of humans, and the arcane layer hidden beneath:

Mundane Layer (Visible to Most Humans)

  • Mid-20th century technology: trains, factories, radio, analog computers, nuclear power
  • No knowledge of non-human species
  • Magic was dismissed as folklore, superstition, stage tricks
  • Science was the dominant worldview

Arcane Layer (Hidden)

  • Mages, elves, dwarves, other sensitives operated in secret societies
  • The Veil Accords (signed ~1,200 years ago): Agreement to remain hidden from mundane humanity
  • Why? Humans outnumbered non-humans 1000:1. Revealing magic would mean war
  • Methods: Memory-wiping magic, misinformation, strategic "debunking"
  • Mages acted as intermediaries, translating non-human knowledge into "human inventions"

When the Sundering Hit: The Veil collapsed. Humans couldn't ignore the elves, dwarves, and undead rising around them. The secret was out. But by then, civilization had already collapsed. The truth came too late to matter.

Metaphysics: How Magic Actually Works

Ley Lines & Saturation (Pre-Sundering Magic)

Before the Theslar Event, magic was subtle and scarce. It operated through ley lines—naturally occurring channels of ambient dimensional energy flowing through the planet's crust.

Ley Line Mechanics (Pre-Sundering Era)

  • Nature: Thin membranes between Material World and Feywild, formed by geological processes
  • Distribution: Concentrated at mountain ranges, coastlines, ancient forests, volcanic regions
  • Power Level: Low-grade. Enough to sustain elvish longevity, power minor spells, charge Soulstone matrices
  • Detection: Only 0.01% of humans could perceive them (mages, sensitives)
  • Usage: Required years of training, precise ritual components, exact astronomical timing

Example Magic in Year -50: A trained mage standing on a ley line nexus during the autumn equinox, using rare crystal focus, might produce a fireball equivalent to a gas lamp's heat. This same mage in Year 437 (post-Sundering, Void-saturated environment) could incinerate an entire building with a whispered word.

Soulstones: The Bridge Between Worlds

Soulstones are crystallized ley line energy, formed under extreme pressure and dimensional stress. Think of them as "batteries" that store metaphysical potential.

Pre-Sundering vs Post-Sundering

Property Pre-Sundering Post-Sundering
Energy Storage Hold charge for months, require ley line exposure to recharge (slow) Infinite recharge—Void saturation charges passively
Power Amplification 1x baseline 100x baseline
Soul Binding Theoretical but impossible (insufficient energy) Practical—consciousness can be recorded/stored/transferred
Bioelectric Interface Only works near dimensional membranes Works anywhere (Void provides carrier signal)
"Pre-Sundering, we used Soulstones to power communication arrays and minor enchantments. Post-Sundering, we use them to bind souls, resurrect the dead, and pilot war machines. The Void didn't just destroy our world. It gave us powers we never imagined...and made us dependent on the very force that killed us." — Forgemaster Durr, "Principles of Void Engineering"

The Void: Sentient or Chaos?

Year 437 scholars debate fiercely: Is the Void an entity with intent, or pure entropic chaos?

Theory 1: The Void Is Alive

(Church Position)

  • Abominations exhibit purposeful behavior
  • Void storms appear to "target" concentrations of life
  • Theslar's final words: "It's looking at me"
  • Interpretation: Malevolent god-like entity consuming reality

Theory 2: The Void Is Physics

(Forge-Guild Position)

  • Void behavior follows patterns (storm cycles, energy gradients)
  • Abominations arise from specific mutation pathways
  • Soulstone interactions are reproducible, quantifiable
  • Interpretation: Extradimensional force following unknown physical laws

Theory 3: The Void Is Hunger

(Wyrd Conclave Position)

  • Void corruption always moves toward complexity
  • Abominations "feed" on structure (literal and metaphorical)
  • Feywild (pure possibility) is Void's opposite and primary target
  • Interpretation: Appetite made manifest

All three theories are partially correct. The Void is anti-structure given form by its collision with reality. It's not sentient, but it learns through interaction. It's not chaos, but it generates entropy. It's not hungry, but it consumes order to exist. The Abominations are the Void's "attempts" to understand what reality is by deconstructing it piece by piece.

The Technology-Magic Fusion

Pre-Sundering, magic and technology were distinct. Post-Sundering, they merged into Void Engineering:

Technology Pre-Sundering Post-Sundering
Caskets Didn't exist Mechanical frames + neural threads + Soulstone cores
Weapons Gunpowder firearms Firearms + Soulstone ammunition (explosive amplification)
Medicine Herbal remedies Herbs + Void-corrupted flora (dangerous but effective)
Necromancy Theoretical (impossible) Practical via Soulstone soul-binding matrices

The Tragic Irony: Humanity's peak technology (electronics, computing) died instantly in the Pulse. But their obsolete 1800s-era machinery (steam, hydraulics, gunpowder) merged with magic to create something new. Progress didn't save them. Obsolescence did.

The Draconids Were Right

The Cataclysm has happened three times total (including the current one):

Ending Date Method
First ~50,000 BCE Flood (cause unknown)
Second ~10,000 BCE Fire (volcanic superchains + ley line destabilization)
Third Year 0 Void (Theslar's Engine)
Fourth? ~Year 20,000+ ???

The Pattern: Every 20,000-40,000 years, a civilization reaches a technological/magical peak, discovers something it shouldn't, and triggers a cataclysm.

The Draconids' Role: Record-keepers. They've seen this cycle three times. They're waiting to see if this cycle's survivors break the pattern or repeat it. They will not intervene. They never do.

Conclusion

Penance is our world, broken by hubris. The fantasy elements aren't alien—they were always here, hidden beneath the surface. The Sundering didn't create the impossible—it revealed it.

Nikolas Theslar wasn't a villain. He was a scientist trying to save the world who didn't know he was playing with forces beyond comprehension. He paid for it. Everyone paid for it.

And now, 437 years later, the survivors pilot iron tombs powered by the very energy that destroyed their world, fighting over the scraps of a civilization that thought it could master the unknowable.

"The central tragedy: The Cataclysm was preventable. If Theslar had known about the Void. If the Veil Accords hadn't hidden non-human knowledge from humanity. If the Draconids had shared their warnings instead of hoarding them. But hubris, secrecy, and fear ensured that no one had the full picture. And the world paid the price."
