The Core Mechanic: Bargain Tokens

Reality-Warping Resource System

Bargain Struck (Passive): Gain +2 Bargain tokens per turn passively. Making deals with reality itself.

Maximum Storage: 10 Bargain tokens (tokens persist across missions)

Bargain Token Abilities:

  • Fae Bargain (0 tokens): Offer enemy a choice: Accept your deal (they skip turn) for +3 Bargain tokens. OR refuse and take 3 damage immediately. Lose-lose for them
  • Reality Bending (2 tokens): Deal 3d6 damage (avg 10.5), ignore all Defense. Pure reality manipulation
  • Stolen Face (3 tokens): Copy 1 ability from enemy casket. Use it as if it were yours. When using stolen ability, gain +2 Defense
  • Mirror Self (5 tokens): Create perfect decoy at your location. If attacked, decoy becomes real and attacks back for 2d6 damage
  • Between Moments (3 tokens): Store 3 Bargain tokens for use in future phases. Action economy advantage

⚖️ Balance Update Oct 19: Increased Bargain Struck from 1/turn to 2/turn. Reality Bending damage increased from 2d6 to 3d6. Stolen Face Defense bonus increased from +1 to +2. Added Between Moments and Illusory Double abilities.

How It Works

Bargain Tokens represent deals made with the Wyrd—every token is a fragment of reality bent to your will. Force enemies into lose-lose choices, steal their abilities, manipulate outcomes. Here's the trickery cycle:

  1. Generate Bargain Tokens - +2/turn passively, +3 if enemy accepts Fae Bargain, +1 per kill with Phase Cut
  2. Fae Bargain (Lose-Lose Choice) - Offer enemy: skip turn (you gain 3 tokens) OR take 3 damage immediately. Either way, you win
  3. Reality Bending (Direct Damage) - 2 tokens for 3d6 damage, ignore all Defense. No counterplay, just numbers
  4. Stolen Face (Ability Theft) - 3 tokens to copy enemy ability. Use their strength against them. Gain +2 Defense when using stolen powers
  5. Mirror Self (Defensive Trick) - 5 tokens for decoy. If attacked, deal 2d6 damage back. Mind games

Example Bargain Sequence

Turn 1: Start with 0 tokens. Gain +2 passive (now 2 total)

Turn 2: Gain +2 passive (now 4 total). Offer Fae Bargain to boss → Boss accepts (skips turn) → You gain 3 more tokens (now 7 total)

Turn 3: Gain +2 passive (now 9 total). Enemy has powerful attack card → Spend 3 tokens on Stolen Face (copy their attack, gain +2 Defense)

Turn 4: Use stolen attack (deals 8 damage + you have +2 Defense this turn) → Kill enemy → Gain 1 token from Phase Cut (now 7 total)

Turn 5: Spend 5 tokens on Mirror Self (create decoy) → Enemy attacks decoy → Decoy counterattacks for 2d6 damage

Turn 6: Gain +2 passive (now 4 total). Spend 2 tokens on Reality Bending (3d6 damage, ignore Defense) → Kill second enemy

You didn't play fair. You rewrote the rules. Welcome to the Wyrd.

Companion Mechanic: Phase Cut & Illusory Double

Phase Cut (Active): Attack that phases through reality. Teleport adjacent to target, attack for normal damage, teleport back. If kill, gain +1 Bargain token.

Illusory Double (Active, 1 token): Create decoy at your location. If attacked, decoy becomes real and attacks back for 3d6 damage. Cheaper than Mirror Self but one-time use.

⚖️ Phase Cut is mobility + Bargain generation. Illusory Double is cheap defensive trick. Wyrd manipulates perception and position.

Strategy Guide: When to Use Bargains

✓ Good Times to Spend Bargain Tokens

  • Fae Bargain on Key Turns - Force enemy to skip critical turn (setup phase, heal turn). They either lose their turn or take 3 damage
  • Reality Bending on Tanks - 3d6 damage ignoring Defense punches through Dwarven armor. Pure damage output
  • Stolen Face on Powerful Enemies - Copy boss's 10-damage attack. Use it against them. Gain +2 Defense while doing so
  • Mirror Self Before Burst - Enemy telegraphing big attack? Place decoy. They hit decoy, take 2d6 back
  • Between Moments (Banking Tokens) - Store 3 tokens for later. Use in desperate situations or end-game burst

✗ Bad Times to Spend Bargains

  • Reality Bending on Low HP - 2 tokens for 3d6 damage on a 5 HP target is overkill. Save for threats
  • Stolen Face on Weak Abilities - Don't spend 3 tokens to copy a 2-damage attack. Steal powerful abilities only
  • Mirror Self When Not Threatened - 5 tokens is expensive. Don't place decoy if no danger. Waste of resources
  • Fae Bargain on Already-Controlled Enemy - If enemy is stunned/disabled, don't waste Fae Bargain. Use on active threats
  • At Token Cap (10) - You're wasting +2/turn generation. Spend down to 6-8 periodically

Result: You forced 2 skipped turns and dealt 3 damage for 0 token cost. Gained 6 tokens total. Pure manipulation.

Common Mistakes

❌ Mistake #1: Not Using Fae Bargain

Why it fails: Fae Bargain costs 0 tokens. Enemy skips turn (you gain 3) OR takes 3 damage. It's free value every turn.

Fix: Use Fae Bargain every mission, multiple times. Force choices, generate tokens. It's your economy engine.

❌ Mistake #2: Hoarding Tokens Without Spending

Why it fails: Sitting at 10 tokens cap wastes +2/turn generation. Bargains are meant to be spent.

Fix: Spend down to 6-8 tokens periodically. Use Reality Bending (cheap at 2), Stolen Face (3), or bank with Between Moments (3).

❌ Mistake #3: Stealing Weak Abilities

Why it fails: Stolen Face costs 3 tokens. Copying a 2-damage attack is wasteful.

Fix: Only steal powerful abilities (6+ damage attacks, game-changing effects). Wait for the right target.

❌ Mistake #4: Not Using Defensive Tricks

Why it fails: Wyrd is fragile (standard 30 HP, light armor). Mirror Self and Illusory Double are your survival tools.

Fix: Place decoys before enemy burst. Use Phase Cut to teleport out of danger. Survival through trickery.

Matchup-Specific Tips

vs. Church of Absolution (Burst Damage)

Church alpha strikes early. Use Fae Bargain to force them to skip their Blood Offering turn OR take 3 damage. Place Illusory Double before their burst. Counterattack when they hit decoy. Control beats aggression.

vs. Dwarven Forge-Guilds (High Armor)

Dwarves have 3-4 Defense. Reality Bending ignores ALL Defense (3d6 pure damage). Spam it. Their armor is irrelevant. Reality manipulation beats stone.

vs. The Ossuarium (Resurrection)

Ossuarium resurrects constantly. Steal their Tainted attacks with Stolen Face (use them with +2 Defense). Reality Bending ignores their Bone Thralls' defenses. Fae Bargain their Bone-Lord to control tempo.

vs. Elven Verdant Covenant (Bleed Kiting)

Elves kite and Bleed. Use Phase Cut to teleport adjacent, attack, teleport back. Fae Bargain to force them to skip their kiting turn. Stolen Face their Poisoned Strike (use it against them with +2 Defense). Mobility + tricks beat kiting.

vs. Vestige Bloodlines (Mutation Spam)

Bloodlines snowball mutations. Use Reality Bending early (turn 3-4) for burst before they scale. Steal their Beast Form with Stolen Face (use it with +2 Defense). Kill them before mutations spiral. Speed beats evolution.
