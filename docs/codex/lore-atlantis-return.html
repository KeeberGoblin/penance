The Atlantean Emergence: When the Lost Returned

"They said we were myth. Legends spoken by firelight. But we were never gone. We were simply... elsewhere. And now, the doors have reopened. We have come home to a world that forgot us. A world that no longer wants us." — High Architect Thalassar, First Contact Address (Year 412)
✿

What Happened in Year 412

Twenty-five years ago, in the Mauritanian desert, something impossible occurred. The Eye of the Sahara—a massive concentric geological formation 40 kilometers wide—began to glow. The light was visible for hundreds of miles—Nomad scouts reported seeing "a teal star rising from the desert." Draconid seismographs recorded vibrations that didn't match any known earthquake pattern. Electromagnetic detectors across the Mediterranean went haywire—needle gauges pegging maximum, Tesla coils sparking spontaneously, telegraph lines crackling with phantom signals. Mages across three continents felt leylines screaming as dormant pathways reactivated. And then, over the course of three days, a city materialized.

Not ruins. Not archaeological remnants. A living, functioning metropolis of impossible architecture—crystalline spires, flowing water gardens, bio-luminescent domes pulsing with leyline energy. The city occupied the exact center of the Eye of the Sahara, a location that had been barren desert for millennia.

The Atlanteans had returned.

What Is Atlantis? The Historical Truth

Atlantis was not a myth. It was Earth's first great civilization, founded approximately 62,000 years ago at the site known today as the Eye of the Sahara (Richat Structure, Mauritania). What made Atlantis unique—and ultimately doomed—was that it existed in two realities simultaneously.

The Dual-Reality Civilization

Atlantis was built atop the most powerful leyline nexus on Earth—a convergence point where twelve major leylines intersected, creating a natural gateway between the Material Plane and the Feywild. The Atlanteans were humanity's first successful experiment in dimensional existence:

  • Material Anchor: Physical bodies, agriculture, industry, technology existed on Earth
  • Feywild Resonance: Consciousness, magic, art, dreams extended into the Feywild
  • Leyline Bridge: The Eye of the Sahara's twelve-fold leyline nexus maintained the connection

Imagine a civilization where every citizen lived in two worlds at once. Their bodies walked Material streets, but their minds touched the infinite possibility of the Feywild. They weren't fully human, and they weren't fully Fae. They were Homo Atlanticus—the Between People.

The Leyline Nexus Architecture

The Eye of the Sahara is a geological formation consisting of concentric rings. The Atlanteans didn't build on this formation—they built it into a machine:

  • Outer Ring (Diameter: 40km): Leyline capacitors—massive stone circles storing dimensional energy
  • Middle Ring (Diameter: 25km): Distribution network—channels routing power to the city core
  • Inner Ring (Diameter: 10km): The City of Atlantis—spires, gardens, archives, temples
  • Central Nexus (Diameter: 2km): The Dimensional Anchor—a crystalline obelisk maintaining the Feywild bridge

This wasn't a city. It was a dimensional engine, using Earth's natural leylines to keep Atlantis anchored in both realities.

The First Cataclysm: How Atlantis Was Lost

Year -50,000 BCE: The Deluge

Approximately 52,000 years ago, Earth experienced the First Cataclysm—an event Draconid archivists refer to as "The Deluge." Geological records show catastrophic global flooding, but the cause remains unknown. However, the Atlanteans know. They remember.

What Caused the Deluge

A rogue leyline surge—similar to what would later happen with the Theslar Event. An unknown event (possibly a meteor impact, supervolcano, or dimensional rupture) destabilized Earth's leyline network. The surge traveled through interconnected leylines like a shockwave through neural pathways.

When the surge reached the Eye of the Sahara, the twelve-fold leyline nexus overloaded. The dimensional bridge holding Atlantis in dual-reality shattered.

The Severance

When the leyline nexus collapsed, Atlantis faced a choice: anchor fully to the Material Plane or phase entirely into the Feywild. The city's architects chose the latter. In the span of three days, Atlantis disappeared from Earth:

Day Event
Day 1 Leyline surge detected. Emergency protocols activated. Citizens recalled to the city.
Day 2 Dimensional Anchor overloads. Reality begins fracturing. Architects choose Feywild refuge over Material destruction.
Day 3 Atlantis phases completely into the Feywild. From Earth's perspective, the city vanishes. Global floods consume coastlines.

To the outside world, Atlantis sank beneath the waves. In truth, it slipped sideways into another dimension—preserved, intact, but utterly cut off from the Material Plane.

50,000 Years in Exile: What Happened to Atlantis

For fifty millennia, Atlantis existed solely in the Feywild—a realm where time flows strangely, where physics are suggestions, where reality itself is negotiable. The Atlanteans survived, but they changed.

The Feywild Mutation

Living in the Feywild for 50,000 years fundamentally altered the Atlanteans:

Aspect Before Exile After 50,000 Years
Lifespan 120-150 years (enhanced human) Functionally immortal (time is non-linear in Feywild)
Physiology Human with minor Fae traits Fae-dominant with vestigial human traits
Reproduction Normal human birth rates Near-sterile (only 1 in 1,000 conceptions succeed)
Psychology Linear thought, human emotions Non-linear perception, emotionally alien
Magic Ability Moderate leyline manipulation Reality-bending Feywild mastery

The Atlanteans who returned in Year 412 are not the same species that left. They are post-human—beings who spent so long in the Feywild that they've become something Other.

The Timeless City

Because time flows non-linearly in the Feywild, many Atlanteans who entered in -50,000 BCE are still alive. From their perspective, only a few centuries passed subjectively. High Architect Thalassar, who led the exodus, is 52,000 years old but subjectively experienced only 800 years. He remembers Earth as it was before humans developed agriculture.

This creates profound existential horror: they remember a world that no longer exists, and everyone they knew outside Atlantis died 50,000 years ago.

The Return: Year 412 Post-Sundering

How the Sundering Brought Them Back

The Theslar Event (Year 0) didn't just tear open the Void—it supercharged Earth's leyline network. The massive influx of extradimensional energy, combined with catastrophic earthquakes, reactivated dormant leyline pathways that had been dead for 50,000 years.

By Year 412, enough leylines had regenerated that the Eye of the Sahara's twelve-fold nexus reignited. From the Feywild side, the Atlanteans detected the sudden reopening of their ancient gateway. After 50,000 years of exile, the door home had opened.

Year 412: The Three-Day Emergence

Day 1 (Reconnaissance):

  • Atlantean scouts phase partially into Material reality
  • They discover Earth is unrecognizable: climate changed, continents shifted, Void corruption everywhere
  • All surface scans detect massive Void saturation and ongoing factional wars

Day 2 (Debate):

  • Atlantean Council debates whether to return: Material Plane is hostile, dangerous, Void-corrupted
  • High Architect Thalassar argues Earth is still home, despite 50,000 years of change
  • Decision: Partial return. Maintain Feywild anchor, but re-establish Material presence

Day 3 (Manifestation):

  • Atlantis phases back into Material reality at the Eye of the Sahara
  • The city appears fully formed: crystalline spires, flowing gardens, glowing leyline channels
  • Population: ~47,000 Atlanteans (down from ~2 million pre-exile due to Feywild-induced sterility)

What Atlantis Looks Like in Year 437

Atlantis in Year 437 is a city caught between dimensions. It maintains simultaneous existence in both Material and Feywild, creating bizarre phenomena:

  • Quantum Architecture: Buildings phase in/out of visibility. Streets exist in one reality but not the other.
  • Temporal Distortion: Time flows 20% slower inside Atlantis. 5 days outside = 4 days inside.
  • Reality Bleeding: Feywild entities (sprites, wisps, minor Fae) randomly manifest in city streets.
  • Leyline Glow: The entire city emits soft teal bioluminescence from leyline-powered infrastructure.

The city is beautiful but deeply unsettling. Visitors report intense feelings of déjà vu, temporal disorientation, and the constant sensation of being watched by something just out of sight.

The Atlanteans: Species Profile (Homo Atlanticus)

Physical Characteristics

Trait Description
Height 6'2" - 6'8" average (taller than baseline humans)
Build Slender, elongated limbs, graceful movement
Skin Iridescent, shifts between teal/blue/silver depending on light. Slightly translucent.
Eyes Solid color, no visible pupils (teal, aquamarine, silver). Faint inner glow.
Hair Ranges from white to deep ocean blue. Flows as if underwater even in still air.
Lifespan Functionally immortal (no observed natural deaths in 50,000 years)
Reproduction Near-sterile (1 in 1,000 conceptions). Population declining slowly.

Unique Abilities

  • Phase Shifting: Can partially phase into the Feywild, becoming translucent/incorporeal for short periods
  • Leyline Sight: See leylines as glowing threads connecting locations. Navigate by leyline topology.
  • Timeless Perception: Experience time non-linearly. Can "remember" events that haven't happened yet (precognition).
  • Reality Editing: Minor Feywild reality-bending (water flows uphill, gravity optional, light bends around them).
  • Dimensional Anchor: Cannot be forcibly banished, teleported, or dimensionally displaced.

Psychological Profile

The Atlanteans are profoundly alien despite their human ancestry. 50,000 years in the Feywild fundamentally changed how they think:

Trait Human Baseline Atlantean
Time Perception Linear (past → present → future) Non-linear (all times simultaneous)
Emotional Range Full spectrum (joy, anger, fear, love) Muted, distant, as if through glass
Urgency Deadlines matter, act quickly Immortal patience, nothing is urgent
Identity Individual self, personal boundaries Collective memory, shared consciousness
Morality Good/evil, right/wrong dichotomy Aesthetic judgment (beautiful/ugly)

Atlanteans don't view themselves as superior to humans. They view themselves as different—a divergent evolutionary branch that took a wrong turn and can never go back. They are profoundly lonely.

Atlantis in Year 437: Political Status

Neutral Isolationists (But Watching)

Since their return in Year 412, the Atlanteans have adopted a policy of strict isolationism:

  • No territorial expansion: Atlantis claims only the Eye of the Sahara and 100km radius around it
  • No alliances: Refuses diplomatic relations with all factions ("You are children playing with matches")
  • No trade: Atlantis is self-sufficient via Feywild conjuration and leyline farming
  • No military intervention: Will not participate in factional wars

However, they are not completely withdrawn. Atlantean scouts monitor leyline activity globally, Feywild rifts, and Void saturation. They are gathering data, studying the post-Sundering world, and waiting.

The Grimdark Tragedy of Atlantis

Atlantis's return is not a triumph—it is a slow-motion funeral. The Atlanteans came home to a world that no longer wants them, piloting bodies that can no longer reproduce, clinging to memories of an Earth that died 50,000 years ago.

Why Atlantis Is Doomed

1. Terminal Sterility

50,000 years in the Feywild rendered Atlanteans nearly sterile. Birth rate: 1 per 1,000 attempts. Current population: ~47,000. At current birth/death rates (accounting for accidents, Void exposure), Atlantis will be extinct in 3,000-5,000 years.

2. Feywild Dependency

Atlanteans can no longer survive purely in Material reality. They require periodic "immersion" in Feywild energy (minimum 6 hours per week). If the leyline nexus fails again, they have weeks before physical degradation begins.

3. Temporal Erosion

Living in non-linear time for 50,000 years damaged Atlantean neurological patterns. They experience "temporal echoes"—memories of futures that never happened, dreams of pasts they didn't live. 8% of Atlanteans suffer permanent temporal psychosis (lost in time, unable to distinguish past/present/future).

4. Cultural Stagnation

Because they're functionally immortal, Atlantean culture has stopped evolving. No generational turnover means no new ideas, no fresh perspectives. They are a civilization frozen in amber, slowly forgetting how to change.

5. Existential Loneliness

The Atlanteans are cosmically alone. They remember an Earth with megafauna, ice age glaciers, and Neanderthals. Everyone they knew outside Atlantis died 50,000 years ago. They returned to a world of strangers who view them as aliens. Many suffer profound depression.

The Atlantean Question

The Draconids ask: "Why did you come back?"

High Architect Thalassar's answer: "Because home is not a place. Home is a promise. And we promised we would return. Even if no one remembers. Even if no one cares. We came home."

GM Secret: The Draconid Warning

The Draconids remember Atlantis from all three Cataclysms. According to their archives:

  • First Cataclysm (~50,000 BCE): Atlantis phases into Feywild to escape global flood
  • Second Cataclysm (~10,000 BCE): Atlantis attempts return, fails due to ice age leyline disruption, phases back to Feywild after 3 years
  • Third Cataclysm (Year 0): Atlantis returns again in Year 412, stable so far

Draconid Elder Vrax's private journal (accessible only to GMs):

"They do not remember the second return. Temporal psychosis erased those 3 years from their collective memory. But I remember. I was there. I watched them try to reclaim Earth in -10,000 BCE, fail, and flee back to the Feywild. They are caught in a cycle: return, fail, forget, return again. This is their third attempt. Will it be different this time? Or will the cycle continue until entropy claims us all?" — Draconid Elder Vrax, Year 425

GM Implications: The Atlanteans are unknowingly trapped in a temporal loop. Every 40,000 years, they try to return. Every time, something goes wrong. The Draconids are watching to see if Year 437 breaks the pattern... or repeats it.
