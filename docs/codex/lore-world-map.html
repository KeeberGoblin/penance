Theslar in Year 437: Political Geography & Territorial Control

"The map is a lie. Borders shift daily. Today's ally controls the valley; tomorrow's enemy claims the ruins. The only certainty is the Void—it expands while we squabble over ash and stone." — Cartographer Lyssa Venn, Exchange Geographical Survey, Year 421

Pre-Sundering maps are relics of a dead world. Coastlines have shifted, mountain ranges have collapsed, and entire regions have been swallowed by Void rifts. The political geography of Year 437 is fluid, contested, and constantly renegotiated through warfare, trade, and survival necessity. This page documents current territorial control, major settlements, travel routes, and resource distribution across Theslar's scarred landscape.

❈

The Siberian Pole: Understanding Theslar's Geography

Critical Note for GMs: Theslar's geography is not Earth-standard. The north pole is located in central Siberia, creating a rotated climate map. This affects where ice forms, which regions are habitable, and how factions distribute themselves across the world.

The Pole Shift: Why Siberia is North

Whether Theslar always had this orientation or the Sundering caused catastrophic axial tilt is debated by scholars. What matters for Year 437:

  • Central Siberia is the North Pole - The coldest point on the planet
  • Ground Zero (Theslar Engine) is in the Siberian Ice Cap - 2,000km from the pole center
  • Ice coverage radiates outward from Siberia, not from the Arctic Ocean
  • Climate zones are rotated - Former "Arctic" regions (Scandinavia, Alaska) are now temperate

Climate Zones (Year 437, Post-Stabilization)

After 437 years, the planet has reached new climate equilibrium following the Ashfall Winter and subsequent greenhouse effects. Global average temperature: 5-7°C cooler than pre-Sundering baseline.

The Siberian Ice Cap (Polar Dead Zone)

The ice cap covering central Siberia is 3-5km thick in places, formed over 437 years of perpetual winter. Key features:

  • Ground Zero (Theslar Engine) - Encased in 4km of ice, but Void pulses melt surrounding area creating unstable glacier caves
  • Void Radiation - Strongest at pole, weakens with distance (exponential decay)
  • Crimson Aurora - Visible year-round from pole, fades beyond 4,000km
  • No Settlements - Closest outpost is Coldharbor (Ossuarium) at 3,200km from pole
  • Exploration Expeditions - Annual suicide missions to Ground Zero; 90% never return

GM Note: The Siberian Ice Cap is a narrative tool for high-level campaigns. "Journey to Ground Zero" is the ultimate endgame mission—shutting down the Engine permanently. Most campaigns never reach this point.

Territorial Overview by Faction

Church of Absolution (Tier 1 Superpower)

Capital: Cathedral Prime (former Paris, France)
Territory: Western Europe (France, Low Countries, Western Germany, Northern Spain)
Climate Zone: Temperate (0-20°C range, ~3,800km from Siberian pole)
Population: 2.4 million
Extent: Approximately 800,000 km²

Major Settlements:

  • Cathedral Prime (Paris) - 600,000 residents, heavily fortified, temperate farmland
  • Penitent's Gate (Brussels) - 150,000 residents, military staging ground
  • Ashborne Shrine (Lyon) - 80,000 residents, pilgrimage site
  • Port Absolution (Bordeaux) - 60,000 residents, Atlantic naval base

Why Here: Western Europe's temperate climate post-Siberian pole shift makes it ideal for agriculture. Mild winters (0°C) and warm summers (20°C) allow year-round farming. Pre-Sundering infrastructure (Paris, Brussels) provided foundation for Cathedral Prime. Distance from pole (3,800km) means low Void radiation but close enough to access Soulstone deposits from eastern expeditions.

Strategic Resources: Temperate farmland (Loire Valley, Flanders), iron deposits (Pyrenees), timber (Ardennes forests), Atlantic fishing waters, salvage from dense pre-Sundering cities

Borders: East: buffer zones with Ossuarium (contested Rhineland). South: Mediterranean trade with Exchange. West: Atlantic Ocean (shipping lanes). North: North Sea (limited naval activity).

The Ossuarium (Tier 1 Superpower)

Capital: The Necropolis (former Moscow, Russia)
Territory: Eastern Europe and Western Russia (subarctic/taiga belt)
Climate Zone: Subarctic (-10°C winter, 15°C summer, ~2,500km from Siberian pole)
Population: 800,000 living + 12,000 Caskets
Extent: Approximately 1.5 million km²

Major Settlements:

  • The Necropolis (Moscow) - 300,000 living, 4,000 Caskets - underground crypt-city
  • Bonefield (St. Petersburg) - 150,000 living, 3,000 Caskets - industrial necromancy center
  • Coldharbor (Murmansk) - 50,000 living, 2,000 Caskets - staging ground for polar expeditions
  • Frostmarrow (Urals foothills) - 80,000 living, 1,500 Caskets - mining outpost

Why Here: Proximity to Siberian Ice Cap (2,500km) provides access to high-concentration Soulstone deposits. The undead labor force thrives in subarctic cold—frozen corpses preserve better, eliminating decay issues. Taiga forests provide timber. Most importantly: they're closest to Ground Zero, allowing Bonelord Karath's expeditions to harvest souls from Void rift zones. The cold is irrelevant to Caskets.

Strategic Resources: Unlimited undead labor (12,000 Caskets work 24/7 without rest), Soulstone refineries (highest purity in world due to proximity to pole), taiga timber, Ural mineral deposits, monopoly on polar expedition supplies

Borders: West: contested buffer with Church (Rhineland, Poland). East: Arctic Tundra (encroaching ice, nomadic Emergent Syndicate scouts). South: trade corridor with Exchange. North: Siberian Ice Cap (uninhabitable beyond Coldharbor).

Dwarven Forge-Guilds (Tier 2 Regional Power)

Capital: Ironhearth (former Alps, Switzerland/Austria)
Territory: Alpine mountain range and surrounding highlands
Population: 3-5 million
Extent: Approximately 400,000 km² (primarily vertical, underground cities)

Major Settlements:

  • Ironhearth (Alps) - 1.2 million residents, 15 vertical levels underground
  • Forgedeep (Carpathian Mountains) - 600,000 residents, primary metallurgy center
  • Runestone Hold (Dolomites) - 400,000 residents, arcane research facility

Strategic Resources: Rich mineral deposits (iron, copper, rare earth elements), underground aquifers, geothermal power, defensible terrain, master runecraft expertise

Borders: Dwarven territory is defined by mountain ranges rather than surface borders. They maintain "vertical sovereignty"—underground tunnels extend far beyond surface-level political boundaries, creating tension with neighbors. Surface borders: West with Church, East with Ossuarium, South with Exchange trade routes.

The Exchange (Tier 2 Regional Power)

Capital: The Vault (former Zurich, Switzerland)
Territory: Central Europe trade corridors (distributed control, non-contiguous)
Population: 2-5 million (difficult to assess, highly mobile mercantile population)
Extent: ~800,000 km² claimed, but actual control is spotty

Major Settlements:

  • The Vault (Zurich) - 800,000 residents, financial center and vault storage
  • Tradehaven (Frankfurt) - 600,000 residents, largest market in Europe
  • Portmarket (Genoa) - 400,000 residents, Mediterranean trade hub
  • Silk's End (Istanbul) - 300,000 residents, gateway to Asian trade

Strategic Resources: Pre-Sundering financial records and wealth caches, extensive trade networks, mercenary forces, information brokerage, neutral ground for diplomatic negotiations

Borders: The Exchange doesn't control continuous territory. Instead, they maintain "trade corridors"—fortified roads connecting their settlements, with watchtowers and mercenary patrols. These corridors pass through Church, Ossuarium, and Dwarven lands via negotiated treaties. The Exchange pays "passage taxes" but maintains autonomy within corridor zones.

Elven Verdant Covenant (Tier 2 Regional Power)

Capital: Roothold (former Amazon Basin, Brazil)
Territory: Amazon rainforest and scattered enclaves globally
Population: 2-4 million
Extent: ~3 million km² (Amazon primary territory) + scattered enclaves totaling ~500,000 km²

Major Settlements:

  • Roothold (Amazon) - 1 million residents, living tree-city
  • Canopy Watch (Congo Basin, Africa) - 400,000 residents, secondary enclave
  • Greenreach (Pacific Northwest, former USA) - 300,000 residents, tertiary enclave
  • Scattered Groves - Dozens of small settlements (500-5,000 residents each) in forested regions globally

Strategic Resources: Timber, medicinal plants, fungal cultivation, ley line access (Amazon node is strongest globally), biological diversity, stealth and guerrilla warfare expertise

Borders: Elven territory is difficult to map. The Amazon "belongs" to the Covenant, but enforcement is minimal beyond 100km from Roothold. They tolerate Nomad passage and small human settlements as long as they don't engage in deforestation. Scattered enclaves have no formal borders—Elves simply claim "the forests" within regions, leading to disputes with local factions.

Nomad Collective (Tier 3 Minor Faction)

Capital: The Wandering (mobile settlement, no fixed location)
Territory: None (nomadic, claims right-of-passage through most regions)
Population: 1-2 million
Range: Global, but concentrates in Eurasian steppe, North African deserts, Australian outback

Major Settlements (Mobile):

  • The Wandering - 300,000 residents, massive caravan of vehicles, animals, and Caskets
  • Dust Convoys - Dozens of smaller caravans (5,000-20,000 each) scattered globally
  • Waystation Camps - Semi-permanent camps at strategic locations (water sources, trade routes), occupied seasonally

Strategic Resources: Mobility, adaptability, extensive trade networks, salvage expertise, Void rift knowledge (they map rift activity as they travel)

Borders: None. Nomads negotiate passage rights with each faction they traverse. Most factions tolerate Nomad caravans because they provide valuable trade, information, and neutral mediation. Conflicts arise when Nomads settle temporarily on contested land or deplete local resources.

Vestige Bloodlines (Tier 3 Minor Faction)

Capital: The Mutation Yards (former Chernobyl Exclusion Zone, Ukraine)
Territory: Void-corrupted wastelands (Chernobyl, Fukushima, scattered zones)
Population: 500,000-1 million
Extent: ~200,000 km² (uninhabitable to non-mutants)

Major Settlements:

  • The Mutation Yards (Chernobyl) - 200,000 residents, mutant sanctuary
  • Glowmarsh (Fukushima, Japan) - 100,000 residents, Pacific mutant enclave
  • Blightwood (Scattered cells in radiation zones) - Total ~200,000 across multiple sites

Strategic Resources: Radiation-tolerant biology, adaptation to Void corruption, access to pre-Sundering facilities in exclusion zones (nuclear reactors, research labs), biological diversity (mutations create unique capabilities)

Borders: Bloodlines don't enforce borders—most factions avoid their territories due to radiation and Void contamination. Unofficial policy: "If you can survive here, you're welcome here." This makes Bloodline territory a refuge for outcasts, criminals, and Void-sensitive individuals.

Emergent Syndicate (Tier 3 Minor Faction)

Capital: The Hive (former Cambodia, Southeast Asia)
Territory: Southeast Asia and expanding into Sibarian Wastes
Population: 1-2 million (hive collective, individuality unclear)
Extent: ~1.5 million km²

Major Settlements:

  • The Hive (Cambodia) - 800,000 residents, bio-organic city
  • Silk Nest (Vietnam) - 400,000 residents, secondary hive cluster
  • Expansion Nodes (Sibarian periphery) - Multiple frontier settlements, total ~300,000

Strategic Resources: Bio-organic architecture (self-repairing, self-sustaining), hive coordination (perfect information sharing), adaptation to Void environments, rapid population expansion

Borders: Emergent borders expand aggressively. The Syndicate claims territory through "conversion"—establishing bio-organic infrastructure that gradually transforms the environment. This makes their borders fluid and alarming to neighbors. Current expansion vector: northeast into Sibarian Wastes, competing with Ossuarium for Void rift control.

Wyrd Conclave (Tier 3 Minor Faction)

Capital: Court of Whimsy (mobile, shifting location)
Territory: "Dimensional overlap zones" (areas where Feywild bleeds into material reality)
Population: ~50,000 Fae (unknown number of humans enthralled or bargained)
Extent: Unmappable (exists partially in material plane, partially in Feywild)

Major Settlements:

  • Court of Whimsy - Location shifts weekly, appears as impossible architecture visible from certain angles
  • Bargain Groves - Dozens of small Fae gathering sites at ley line intersections
  • Dream Markets - Temporary manifestations where Fae trade with mortals

Strategic Resources: Reality manipulation, bargain-based contracts (binding magical agreements), access to Feywild resources, information gathered through dreams and eavesdropping across dimensional boundaries

Borders: The Wyrd Conclave has no physical borders. They claim "dimensional sovereignty" over regions where Feywild and material plane overlap. These zones appear randomly, shift unpredictably, and are marked by strange phenomena (gravity anomalies, time dilation, impossible colors). Most factions avoid these areas entirely.

Crucible Packs (Tier 4 Outlier)

Capital: Ashveil (former Iceland volcanic caldera)
Territory: Ashveil crater and immediate surroundings
Population: ~15,000
Extent: ~5,000 km²

Major Settlements:

  • Ashveil - 12,000 residents, built inside volcanic caldera
  • Blast Temple - 3,000 residents (rotating shifts), explosive manufacturing facility

Strategic Resources: Explosives expertise (monopoly on advanced demolitions), volcanic minerals, geothermal power, strategic location (Iceland controls North Atlantic shipping lanes)

Borders: Crucible territory is limited to Ashveil itself. They have no interest in expansion and maintain strict neutrality. Their explosive manufacturing monopoly gives them diplomatic leverage far beyond their size—every faction buys Crucible munitions, making invasion politically costly.

Draconid Remembrance (Tier 4 Outlier)

Capital: None (47 individuals scattered globally)
Territory: Hidden archives in remote locations
Population: ~50
Extent: N/A

Major Archive Locations (Known):

  • Himalayan Archive (Tibet) - 5 Draconids
  • Antarctic Vault - 3 Draconids
  • Amazon Deep Archive - 4 Draconids (tolerated by Elves)
  • Saharan Observatory - 6 Draconids
  • Unknown/Hidden Archives - Remaining ~30 Draconids

Strategic Resources: Complete historical records spanning 50,000 years, Memory Crystals containing lost knowledge, precognitive warnings about apocalyptic patterns

Borders: Draconids claim no territory. Their archives are hidden, and most factions are unaware of their exact locations. Those who discover a Draconid archive typically negotiate access rather than risk conflict—Draconids are few, but their knowledge is invaluable.

Contested Regions & Neutral Zones

Sibarian Wastes (Major Void Rift)

Control: Contested between Ossuarium and Emergent Syndicate
Status: Active Void rift, reality unstable, rich in Soulstones
Conflict: Low-intensity warfare, neither faction can fully claim the region due to Void hazards

Mediterranean Corridor

Control: Neutral trade zone managed by Exchange
Status: Demilitarized, all factions have port access via treaty
Strategic Importance: Primary shipping route between Europe, Africa, and Asia

Control: Mostly unclaimed, scattered Nomad and Elven settlements

Opportunity: Rich salvage, but dangerous. Expeditions frequently disappear.

Control: Neutral territory surrounding Crucible Packs
Status: 50km buffer zone, enforced by mutual agreement
Reason: Preventing accidental detonation of Crucible munitions stockpiles

Travel Times Between Major Settlements

Travel in Year 437 is slow and dangerous. Pre-Sundering infrastructure (roads, railways, airports) is largely destroyed or unusable. Modern travel relies on:

  • On Foot: 20-30 km/day through safe terrain
  • Mounted/Vehicle: 50-80 km/day (horses, salvaged motorcycles, improvised vehicles)
  • Caravan: 30-40 km/day (Nomad-style large group travel)
  • Casket: 100-150 km/day (fast but draws attention, fuel-intensive)
  • Ship: 150-200 km/day (coastal or river travel, weather-dependent)

Climate & Environmental Hazards

Post-Sundering Climate Zones

Global temperatures dropped 5-10°C after the Sundering due to volcanic ash and Void-induced atmospheric changes. This has created several distinct climate zones:

  • Arctic Expansion: Permafrost extends 500-800km further south than pre-Sundering. Northern Europe experiences subarctic conditions.
  • Temperate Contraction: Former temperate zones (France, Germany) now experience harsher winters and shorter growing seasons.
  • Tropical Resilience: Equatorial regions (Amazon, Congo, Southeast Asia) retain warmth but experience increased rainfall and flooding.
  • Desert Stability: Deserts (Sahara, Gobi) largely unchanged, but sandstorms intensify due to atmospheric instability.

Environmental Hazards

  • Void Storms: Localized reality distortions that manifest as weather phenomena. Can last hours to days, cause hallucinations and spatial warping.
  • Ash Fall: Periodic volcanic eruptions blanket regions in ash, poisoning crops and water supplies.
  • Radiation Zones: Pre-Sundering nuclear disasters (Chernobyl, Fukushima) plus Void-induced radiation create no-go zones.
  • Ley Line Surges: Sudden spikes in magical energy can cause spontaneous fires, mutations, or equipment failures.

GM Notes: Using Geography in Campaigns

Travel as Narrative Tool

Use travel times to create tension. A 60-day journey to The Necropolis is an adventure in itself—encounters with bandits, Void storms, and factional patrols along the way.

Resource Scarcity Drives Conflict

Factions go to war over clean water, farmland, and Soulstone deposits. Players can influence these conflicts by securing or sabotaging resources.

Borders Are Fluid

Don't treat borders as fixed. Factions expand and contract based on military success. A region that's Church-controlled in Session 1 might fall to Ossuarium forces by Session 10.

Exploration Rewards

Unclaimed territories (North America, Australia, interior Africa) offer rich salvage but extreme danger. Use these as high-risk, high-reward expedition zones.
