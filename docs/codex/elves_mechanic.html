The Core Mechanic: Bleed Stacking

Damage Over Time System

Application: Many Elven attacks apply Bleed tokens to enemies. Each Bleed token deals 1 damage at the start of the enemy's turn.

Stacking: Bleed tokens stack infinitely. An enemy with 5 Bleed takes 5 damage per turn automatically.

Duration: Bleed persists until the end of the mission (does not decay or expire)

Execute Synergy: Many Elven cards deal bonus damage to enemies with 3+ Bleed tokens

How It Works

Bleed Stacking is the Elven win condition—apply multiple Bleed tokens, kite enemies, watch them die passively. Your goal is to stack Bleeds, not burst damage. Here's the cycle:

  1. Apply Bleed Tokens - Use cards like "Poisoned Strike" (deals damage + applies 2 Bleed) or "Thornwhip" (applies 1 Bleed, long range)
  2. Kite and Evade - Use high movement (3 hexes) and dodge cards to avoid retaliation
  3. Stack More Bleeds - Each turn, apply 1-2 more Bleed tokens to the same target
    • Turn 1: Enemy has 2 Bleed (takes 2 damage/turn)
    • Turn 2: Add 2 more Bleed (now 4 total, takes 4 damage/turn)
    • Turn 3: Add 1 more Bleed (now 5 total, takes 5 damage/turn)
  4. Execute Low HP Targets - When enemy is below 10 HP with 3+ Bleeds, use "Mercy Strike" (deals double damage to bleeding targets) for finisher

Example Bleed Sequence

Turn 1: Poisoned Strike (3 damage + 2 Bleed) → Enemy at 27 HP, 2 Bleed

Enemy Turn: Takes 2 Bleed damage → Enemy at 25 HP

Turn 2: Thornwhip (2 damage + 1 Bleed) → Enemy at 23 HP, 3 Bleed total

Enemy Turn: Takes 3 Bleed damage → Enemy at 20 HP

Turn 3: Kite away (don't attack) → Bleed does the work

Enemy Turn: Takes 3 Bleed damage → Enemy at 17 HP

Turns 4-10: Enemy continues bleeding 3 damage/turn. You've dealt 18 damage without attacking.

Patience is your weapon. Time is your ally. They die screaming.

Companion Mechanic: Moonlight Blessing

Moonlight Blessing (Passive): When in forest/natural terrain, gain +1 movement and +1 Defense. Elven caskets move gracefully through wilderness.

Moonlight Strike (Active, 3 SP): Teleport up to 5 hexes to any visible natural terrain hex. Gain +2 damage on next attack.

⚖️ Synergy Note: Moonlight Blessing makes Elves incredibly mobile in forest maps. Use terrain to kite while Bleeds do the work.

Strategy Guide: When to Stack Bleeds

✓ Good Times to Stack Bleeds

  • Against Tanks (High HP) - Dwarves, Church, Crucible with 30+ HP. Bleed bypasses armor and chips away at large health pools
  • Early Game (Turns 1-4) - Apply Bleeds early, then kite. By turn 8, they've taken 20+ damage passively
  • When You Can Kite - Bleeds require time to work. If you have mobility advantage, stack and run
  • On Multiple Targets - Apply 2-3 Bleeds to 2-3 enemies. Everyone takes passive damage. Efficient economy
  • Before Executing - Stack 3+ Bleeds, then finish with Mercy Strike (double damage to bleeding targets)

✗ Bad Times to Stack Bleeds

  • Against Low HP Enemies - Don't Bleed a 10 HP Thrall. Just kill it with direct damage
  • When Being Rushed - If enemies close distance fast, you don't have time to kite. Switch to direct damage
  • Late Game (Turn 10+) - Missions end around turn 12-15. Bleeds need time to work. Too late to set up new stacks
  • Against Healing Enemies - Some factions heal (Church, Ossuarium). Bleeds get outhealed. Focus burst instead
  • When You're Cornered - Bleeds require kiting space. If trapped, switch to defensive/burst tactics

Advanced Tactics & Combos

The Poison Cloud

AoE Bleed Combo:

Thornstorm (AoE, 2 damage + 1 Bleed to all enemies in 3-hex radius) → Retreat 4 hexes → Enemies chase → Take Bleed damage every turn

Result: Hit 3-4 enemies with 1 Bleed each. Over 5 turns, that's 15-20 free damage total. Efficient action economy.

The Execute Spike

Finisher Combo:

Stack 3+ Bleeds on enemy (down to 12 HP) → Mercy Strike (4 damage, double damage if target has 3+ Bleeds = 8 damage total) → Enemy dies from 4 damage (doubled to 8) + 3 Bleed = 11 damage

You secure the kill while enemy is still mid-HP. Mercy Strike is your win condition.

The Moonlight Assassin

Mobility Combo:

Moonlight Strike (teleport 5 hexes, +2 damage) → Poisoned Strike (3 damage + 2 Bleed, now 5 damage) → Retreat with high movement (3 base + 1 from Moonlight Blessing = 4 hexes)

You teleport in, apply Bleeds, deal burst damage, teleport out. Enemy can't retaliate. Repeat until dead.

The Patient Hunter

Kiting Strategy:

Apply 2 Bleeds → Kite for 2 turns (don't attack) → Apply 2 more Bleeds → Kite for 2 turns → Apply final Bleed → Execute

You minimize risk by only engaging briefly, maximizing Bleed uptime. Enemy takes 10-15 passive damage while you stay safe.

Common Mistakes

❌ Mistake #1: Trying to Burst Damage

Why it fails: Elves have low base damage (3-5 per attack). You're not a burst faction. Trying to out-damage Church/Dwarves wastes your kit.

Fix: Embrace Bleeds. Apply 3-5 Bleed tokens over 3 turns, then kite while they die passively. Patience wins.

❌ Mistake #2: Standing Still After Applying Bleeds

Why it fails: Bleeds need time to work. If you stand next to the enemy, they hit you back and negate the advantage.

Fix: Apply Bleeds → Retreat 3-4 hexes → Force enemies to chase (wasting their turns) → Bleeds tick while they chase.

❌ Mistake #3: Spreading Bleeds Too Thin

Why it fails: Applying 1 Bleed to 5 enemies means each takes 1 damage/turn. That's inefficient—5 damage total spread across 5 enemies.

Fix: Focus-stack Bleeds. Apply 3-5 Bleeds to 1-2 priority targets. Kill them, then move to next target. Concentration beats diffusion.

❌ Mistake #4: Forgetting to Execute

Why it fails: Bleeds alone don't win. A 10 HP enemy with 3 Bleeds will take 3-4 turns to die. That's 3-4 turns they're attacking you.

Fix: Once enemy is below 12 HP with 3+ Bleeds, use Mercy Strike (double damage) to finish immediately. Don't let wounded enemies linger.

Matchup-Specific Tips

vs. Church of Absolution (Burst Damage)

Church relies on alpha strikes turn 1-2. Apply Bleeds then kite out of range. They burn resources early, you kill them slowly. By turn 6, they're at 10 HP from Bleeds and can't heal.

vs. Dwarven Forge-Guilds (High Armor)

Dwarves have 3-4 Defense. Your direct damage is inefficient. Bleeds bypass armor entirely. Stack 5-6 Bleeds and kite. They take 5-6 damage/turn regardless of armor. Patience beats stone.

vs. The Ossuarium (Resurrection)

Ossuarium resurrects constantly. Bleeds persist through resurrection. A resurrected Bone Thrall still has 3 Bleeds and dies again. Free kills. Farm them for easy victories.

vs. Vestige Bloodlines (Mutation Spam)

Bloodlines snowball if left unchecked. Apply Bleeds early (turn 1-2), then kite. They mutate but still take Bleed damage. Execute at 12 HP before mutations spiral out of control.

vs. Emergent Syndicate (Metamorph Tokens)

Emergent gain power through proximity. Apply Bleeds from long range (Thornwhip, 4 hexes). Kite away. They can't reach you to gain Metamorph tokens. Bleeds kill them while they chase.
