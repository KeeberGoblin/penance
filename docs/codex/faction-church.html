Church of Absolution

CODEX / FACTIONS / Church of Absolution
"We did not die because we were unworthy. We died because the world demanded sacrifice."
❈

Playstyle: Aggressive Martyrdom

Core Mechanic: Blood Offering (self-harm for burst damage, infinite scaling)

Philosophy: Suffering purifies. Pain is prayer. Redemption through violence.

Population: 15-20 million

Territory: Cathedral Prime (capital)

Governance: Theocratic hierarchy led by the Grand Inquisitor

The Confessor (Scout): Fast, aggressive, self-destructive flagellants. 700 lbs, 6 SP, 26-30 cards.

The Crusader (Assault): Balanced faith warrior. 1000 lbs, 7 SP, 28-35 cards.

The Inquisitor (Heavy): Armored interrogator. 1400 lbs, 8 SP, 32-40 cards.

The Cathedral (Fortress): Mobile base. 2000+ lbs, 9 SP, 35-50 cards.

  • Flagellants: Self-harming warriors, gain bonuses from wounds
  • Penitents: Sacrifice HP for powerful effects
  • Relic Bearers: Carry sacred artifacts, healing support
  • Aspirants: Young zealots, high mobility
  • Choir: Buff/healing support unit
  • Absolution Engine: Massive artillery platform

Blood Offering: Mill 2 cards, next attack +2d6 damage, ignore 1 Armor. 0 SP, once per turn.

Righteous Fury: Gain Wrath on kills. Spend Wrath for movement, doubled damage, or Holy Fury mode.

Martyrdom Protocol: Redirect adjacent ally damage to yourself, ally draws card.

Divine Judgment: Execute wounded enemies. All allies remove 1 Taint token on kill.

Consecrated Ground: Place Hallowed tokens on hexes. Allies +1 Defense, remove Taint. Enemies -1 all actions.

The Church of Absolution emerged from pre-Sundering religious institutions, but evolved into something far darker. When the Theslar Engine detonated, killing millions, survivors questioned God's plan.

The Church's answer: suffering is redemption. Pain purifies the soul. Every wound brings humanity closer to salvation.

They were the first to weaponize Caskets, binding captured souls into Soulstone cores and strapping priests into the shells. Penance through steel. Absolution through blood.

The Ossuarium: Cordial (shared understanding of death's power)

The Exchange: Tense (Church distrusts profit motives)

Dwarven Forge-Guilds: Neutral (mutual respect for craftsmanship)

Elven Verdant Covenant: Hostile (competing for territory)

Wyrd Conclave: Suspicious (reality-benders disturb faith)

Early Game: Use Blood Offering to generate burst damage early. Suffer tactical losses to fuel Righteous Fury.

Mid Game: Stack Wrath tokens. Position Martyrs to protect allies. Control terrain with Consecrated Ground.

Late Game: Unleash Holy Fury. Use Divine Judgment to execute enemies and cleanse Taint. Trade resources for momentum.

Key Weakness: Resource-intensive. Runs out of cards quickly. Must finish fights fast or risk exhaustion.
