Faction Overview

The Merchant Guilds control the post-apocalyptic economy. They don't fight wars—they bankroll them, sell weapons to both sides, and profit from the carnage.

"War is profitable. Peace is expensive. Choose accordingly." —Guild Ledger Principle #1

Core Mechanic: Bribery & Resource Control

The Merchant Guilds wage economic warfare—buying enemies, hiring mercenaries, and controlling the battlefield through sheer wealth.

  • Bribery: Pay enemies to switch sides, bribe support units, buy temporary ceasefires
  • Hired Mercenaries: Deploy powerful units mid-battle for Credits
  • Resource Monopoly: Control Scrap nodes, deny enemies equipment, tax the battlefield
  • Economic Warfare: Win without fighting—just buy victory

Playstyle (Conceptual)

Economic control. Resource denial. Hired mercenaries. Bribery and manipulation. High risk, high reward—run out of Credits and you lose everything.

Key Casket Types (Placeholder)

  • Tycoon Frame: Heavy, resource monopoly, controls battlefield economy

Notable Figures

Proctor Ilyara Voss (The Actuary of Ash)

A cold, transactional woman with a mechanical jaw (half her face blown off in a "hostile acquisition"). Responsible for the Ashen Credit System - wartime loans at 40% interest. Can't pay? Your survivors become indentured labor. The most hated woman in three provinces. Also the richest. Pilots "The Ledger" - a mobile vault that can call in mercenaries and artillery.

The Coin-Counter (Anonymous)

No one knows their name. They wear a featureless golden mask and speak through a voice modulator. The Guild's chief accountant - decides which settlements get loans and which get liquidated. Rumor says they're a consciousness uploaded into a Soulstone ledger. Every transaction is recorded forever.

Thane Blackmarket

Runs the Guild's black market: weapons to both sides, Soulstones from "questionable" sources, indentured labor contracts. Everyone wants him dead. He's still alive because he knows where the bodies are buried. Literally. He sells maps.

Lore: The Profiteers of Armageddon

Origins: Disaster is Profitable (Year 0-20)

Before the Sundering, the Merchant Guilds were trade consortiums. When reality broke, most corporations collapsed. A few adapted: disaster is profitable. They sold pre-Sundering goods at 1000% markup. Can't afford it? Take a loan. Can't repay? Become indentured labor. Within 20 years, they controlled half the world's economy.

The Ashen Credit System: Debt as Weapon

Settlements borrow Soulstones at 40% interest. Default? The Guild seizes assets - equipment, buildings, people. Thousands trapped in indentured servitude, working to pay debts their grandparents took. The Guilds call it "fulfilling contracts." Everyone else calls it slavery.

Major Events

Year 34 - The Soulstone Cartel

Five Guilds merged to monopolize 70% of Soulstone extraction. Fixed prices. Settlements revolted. Mercenaries suppressed riots. Prices stayed high.

Year 256 - The Debt Rebellion

Indentured workers rebelled, seizing three mines. Guilds called in ALL debts from rebels' home settlements - bankrupting entire communities. Rebels surrendered. "Rebellion costs" added to debts. They'll never be free.

Current Goals (Year 437)

The Guilds want war to continue indefinitely. Peace crashes profits. However, The Engine's instability threatens supply chains. For the first time, they're considering stability - not for altruism, just risk management. They'll choose whichever option has the best ROI.
