Dwarven Forge-Guilds

CODEX / FACTIONS / Dwarven Forge-Guilds
"Steel doesn't lie. Axes don't break. The mountain doesn't move. We will endure."
❈

Playstyle: Fortress Defender

Core Mechanic: Rune Counters (stack for defense/damage, armor-piercing)

Philosophy: Craftsmanship and tradition. The mountain endures.

Population: 3-5 million

Territory: Ironhearth (underground mountain cities)

Governance: Guild councils ruled by elected Forgemasters

The Scout (Light): Mobile sappers. 600 lbs, 5 SP, 26-30 cards.

The Warrior (Medium): Balanced defender. 1100 lbs, 7 SP, 30-38 cards.

The Sentinel (Heavy): Armored fortress. 1600 lbs, 8 SP, 35-42 cards.

The Colossus (Ultra-Heavy): Walking mountain. 2200+ lbs, 9 SP, 40-50 cards.

  • Shield Wall: Defensive support, creates fortified positions
  • Artillery: Long-range bombardment specialist
  • Rune Sentinel: Applies beneficial rune effects
  • Siege Crawler: Demolition and fortification
  • Ancestor Golem: Ancient construct, powerful defender
  • Dreadnought: Massive walker platform

Dwarven Caskets use Rune Counters that stack over time. Stack defense runes for armor, damage runes for offense. The longer the Dwarf fights, the stronger they become.

Stacking Mechanic: Each turn, add 1 Rune Counter. Spend counters for effects: 1 counter = +1 Defense, 2 counters = +2 damage, 3 counters = Armor-piercing attack.

Counterplay: Enemies must finish Dwarves quickly or face exponentially increasing defenses.

The Dwarves survived the Sundering by retreating underground. Ironhearth, their primary settlement, was carved from solid mountain centuries before the cataclysm. When the world shattered, they sealed the gates and endured.

Master Forgemasters learned to craft Caskets from ancient pre-Sundering schematics. Dwarven steel became legendary. Every weapon, every shield, every casket frame bears the mark of generations of craftsmanship.

The guilds control Soulstone production, making them one of the few factions wealthy enough to maintain standing armies. Trade, tradition, and tenacity sustain them.

The Exchange: Cooperative (profit from Soulstone trade)

Church of Absolution: Neutral (respect for craftsmanship)

The Ossuarium: Suspicious (undead philosophy conflicts)

Emergent Syndicate: Pragmatic (some Dwarves volunteer for metamorphosis)

Nomad Collective: Cautious (territory disputes)

Early Game: Build defensive position. Stack rune counters. Force enemy to attack fortified position.

Mid Game: Convert defense runes into armor. Use heavy weapons sparingly. Maintain high counter stacks.

Late Game: Unleash armor-piercing attacks. Dwarves grow stronger in prolonged battles.

Key Strength: Time is your ally. The longer battle lasts, the stronger you become. Win through attrition.
