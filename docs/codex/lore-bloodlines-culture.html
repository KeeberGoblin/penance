The Vestige Bloodlines: Keepers of the Forbidden

"We are not monsters who became human. We are humans who became something more—and paid the price. Every Bloodline carries the memory of who we were before the Thirst. That's the real curse. Not the hunger. The remembering." -Margrave Elise Corvain, Matriarch of House Corvain

The Vestige Bloodlines are ~250,000 individuals organized into seven Great Houses and dozens of minor lineages, bound by shared affliction: vampirism, necromantic mutation, or symbiotic parasitism. Pre-Sundering occultists and bio-hackers who survived by embracing forbidden transformations, they now exist on society's margins—powerful, feared, and eternally othered.

They are not a unified faction. They are scattered enclaves clinging to bloodline identity while navigating a world that would eradicate them if given the chance.

✿

Origin: The Sanguine Compact (Years -2 to 0)

Before the Sundering: Occult Underground

Pre-Sundering, ~80,000 individuals globally practiced "fringe metaphysics"—occultism, experimental bioalchemy, transhumanist body modification, and death magic research. Operating in secret (illegal in most nations), they formed networks of:

  • Sanguine Societies: Researchers pursuing immortality via blood-borne parasites (proto-vampirism)
  • Necrocartels: Biotech firms illegally experimenting with reanimation and consciousness transfer
  • Shadow Lodges: Aristocratic occult clubs practicing ritual magic derived from pre-modern grimoires
  • Chimeric Communes: Transhumanist collectives undergoing genetic/cybernetic augmentation

Year -2: The Warning Signs

Two years before the Sundering, occult practitioners worldwide experienced prophetic visions—nightmares of burning skies, collapsing reality, mass death. Dismissing these as shared hysteria was impossible when 27 unconnected seers across 14 countries reported identical imagery.

The Sanguine Compact (Year -1): Leaders of major occult factions convened in secret (location: abandoned monastery, Romanian Carpathians). Agreement reached:

  • "An apocalypse is imminent. Conventional survival methods will fail."
  • "We pursue extreme measures: blood parasitism, necromantic transformation, symbiotic fusion."
  • "If successful, we become immortal. If we fail, we die anyway when the world ends."

Year 0: Transformation and Survival

When the Sundering occurred, ~80,000 occultists had undergone various "ascension rituals":

Transformation Type Process Survival Rate
Sanguine Parasitism Infection with engineered blood parasite; grants immortality + requires blood consumption 40% (48,000 attempted, 19,200 survived)
Necromantic Rebirth Ritual death + reanimation via Soulstone binding; undead but conscious 25% (20,000 attempted, 5,000 survived)
Chimeric Fusion Genetic splicing with apex predator DNA (bat, serpent, wolf) 15% (12,000 attempted, 1,800 survived)

Of ~80,000 who attempted transformation, 26,000 survived to see Day 7 of the Sundering. These became the founding Bloodlines.

Years 1-50: Hunted and Scattered

Early post-Sundering, Bloodlines faced:

  • Church Persecution: Declared "abominations"; hunted by Inquisition (12,000 killed in Years 10-30)
  • Resource Competition: Blood scarcity (few humans left); Soulstone hoarding (necromancers need continuous supply)
  • Internal Fragmentation: Without pre-Sundering infrastructure, occult networks collapsed into isolated lineages
  • Adaptive Evolution: Surviving Bloodlines learned to hide, blend with human populations, or retreat to remote enclaves

By Year 50, population stabilized at ~22,000. By Year 437 (current), grown to ~250,000 through:

  • Natural reproduction (affliction hereditary in 60% of offspring)
  • Voluntary "Turning" (surface dwellers offered immortality in exchange for servitude)
  • Accidental infection (blood contamination, necromantic exposure)

The Seven Great Houses

The Bloodlines are organized into aristocratic lineages, each descended from a pre-Sundering founder:

House Corvain (Sanguine Purists)

  • Population: ~42,000
  • Affliction: Classic vampirism (blood parasitism; nocturnal; photosensitive)
  • Territory: Scattered estates across former Eastern Europe, South America
  • Philosophy: "We are apex predators. Humanity is prey, but sustainable prey. We farm, not hunt to extinction."
  • Culture: Aristocratic; maintain pre-Sundering etiquette, fashion, art
  • Matriarch: Margrave Elise Corvain (age 462; turned Year -1)

House Malachar (Necrarch)

  • Population: ~38,000
  • Affliction: Necromantic undeath (reanimated corpses; souls bound via Soulstone magic)
  • Territory: Crypts beneath former Paris, Prague, Istanbul
  • Philosophy: "Death is transition, not end. We master it. We transcend it."
  • Culture: Scholarly; obsessed with death magic research, mausoleum architecture
  • Patriarch: Lichmaster Valdos Malachar (age 478; lich since Year 0)

House Dracul (Chimeric)

  • Population: ~31,000
  • Affliction: Bat-human hybrid (wings, echolocation, extreme agility)
  • Territory: Mountain cave networks (Carpathians, Andes, Himalayas)
  • Philosophy: "Humanity's form was cage. We broke free. We are evolution."
  • Culture: Martial; aerial combat specialists; honor duels
  • Lord Commander: Skytalon Viktor Dracul (age 401; third-generation chimera)

House Seraph (Blood Mystics)

  • Population: ~29,000
  • Affliction: Blood magic practitioners (consume blood to fuel spellcasting; not undead)
  • Territory: Hidden monasteries (Tibet, Mongolia, Andes)
  • Philosophy: "Blood is life. Life is power. Power is enlightenment."
  • Culture: Monastic; meditative practices; blood alchemy
  • High Priestess: Crimson Sage Amara Seraph (age 412; ascetic leader)

House Vargr (Lycanthropes)

  • Population: ~35,000
  • Affliction: Wolf-human shapeshifting (controlled transformation; enhanced senses)
  • Territory: Wilderness packs (Canadian tundra, Siberian taiga, Scandinavian forests)
  • Philosophy: "Civilization is weakness. The pack is strength. The hunt is truth."
  • Culture: Tribal; oral tradition; ritual hunts
  • Alpha Prime: Fenrir Vargr (age 387; largest recorded lycanthrope—9ft tall in hybrid form)

House Thanis (Shadow Weavers)

  • Population: ~27,000
  • Affliction: Shadow symbiosis (bonded to Void shadow-entities; can phase through darkness)
  • Territory: Urban undergrounds (subway tunnels, catacombs, sewer networks)
  • Philosophy: "Light reveals. Shadow conceals. We thrive in the spaces between."
  • Culture: Espionage-focused; information brokers; assassins
  • Shadowlord: Umbral Kael Thanis (age 392; rarely seen in physical form)

House Mortis (Death Eaters)

  • Population: ~21,000
  • Affliction: Consume death energy (feed on dying breaths, corpse emanations; grotesque appearance)
  • Territory: Plague zones, battlefields, disaster sites
  • Philosophy: "Death is harvest. We are reapers. Suffering sustains us."
  • Culture: Grim pragmatists; follow wars/plagues like scavengers
  • Reaper-King: Carrion Lord Mortis (age 423; skeletal, decayed form)

Minor Bloodlines

Beyond the Great Houses, ~27,000 belong to dozens of minor lineages (serpent-blooded, insect-fused, elemental-touched, etc.). These lack political power but maintain independent enclaves.

Social Structure and Culture

Hierarchy Within Houses

Rank Role Selection Method
Patriarch/Matriarch House leader; final authority on policy, alliances, justice Hereditary or Trial by Blood (ritual combat for succession)
Elders Advisory council (3-12 members); age 200+ years required Appointed by Patriarch based on wisdom/power
Trueborn Born into Bloodline (not turned); higher status Hereditary (60% chance of inheriting affliction)
Turned Converted from baseline humanity; loyal but lower caste Selected by Elders for specific skills/potential
Thralls Bound servants (blood-bonded to specific master); not fully afflicted Voluntary servitude or debt repayment

Inter-House Relations

Great Houses maintain fragile alliances through:

  • Blood Marriages: Cross-House unions to cement treaties
  • The Conclave: Annual gathering of House leaders (neutral ground; location rotates); discusses collective threats
  • Resource Sharing: Blood tithes, Soulstone exchanges, territorial agreements
  • Vendetta Protocol: Formal blood feuds (regulated to prevent faction-wide war)

Philosophy: The Dual Nature

Vestige Bloodlines grapple with existential tension:

"We Are Not Human, But We Were"

Every Bloodline member remembers baseline humanity. This creates internal conflict: Are they evolved or cursed? Predators or protectors? Superior or damned?

"The Thirst Defines Us"

Blood hunger (or death energy, or Soulstone dependency) is constant. It's not addiction—it's biological necessity. Resisting means death.

"Immortality Is Loneliness"

Bloodlines watch mortal loved ones age and die. Relationships with baseline humans are inherently temporary. This breeds isolation and melancholy.

"We Chose This"

Unlike Void-mutated (Syndicate, Wyrd), Bloodlines actively chose transformation. This carries moral weight—they cannot claim victimhood.

Daily Life and Practices

Feeding Strategies

Sustaining 250,000 immortal predators requires careful resource management:

  • Blood Farms (House Corvain): Voluntary donors paid in protection/resources (ethical but controversial)
  • Soulstone Harvesting (House Malachar): Trade with Exchange for condemned criminals' souls
  • Battlefield Scavenging (House Mortis): Follow wars, consume death energy from dying soldiers
  • Wildlife Predation (House Vargr): Hunt mutated fauna in wasteland (less sustaining than human blood)
  • Thrall System: Permanent blood-bonded servants who donate regularly in exchange for immortality (eventually)

Architecture and Settlements

Bloodline enclaves reflect their dual nature—clinging to pre-Sundering aesthetics while adapting to post-apocalyptic reality:

  • Gothic Revival: Castles, manors, crypts restored from ruins (House Corvain, Malachar)
  • Cave Complexes: Natural formations expanded into vertical cities (House Dracul)
  • Underground Monasteries: Hidden sanctuaries with blood-powered runecraft lighting (House Seraph)
  • Nomadic Camps: Temporary structures following prey/war zones (House Mortis, Vargr)

Art and Cultural Preservation

Bloodlines see themselves as keepers of pre-Sundering high culture:

  • Music: Classical orchestras (some members 400+ years old, remembering Baroque era)
  • Literature: Extensive libraries salvaged from ruins; literacy rate 95%
  • Visual Arts: Portrait painting tradition (immortal subjects painted every 50 years to track subtle aging)
  • Blood Art: Paintings created with enchanted blood (changes color with viewer's emotions)

Education and Knowledge

  • Apprenticeship Model: Elders teach Trueborn in one-on-one mentorship (decades-long training)
  • Occult Libraries: Collective knowledge of pre-Sundering death magic, blood alchemy, necromancy
  • Cross-House Exchanges: Promising students sent to allied Houses to learn specialized techniques
  • Forbidden Lore: Restricted texts on Void manipulation, forced Turning, soul consumption (access by Elders only)

GM Guidance: Using Bloodlines in Campaigns

Role in the World

  • Tragic Antiheroes: Not evil, but othered; driven by biological necessity
  • Keepers of Lost Knowledge: Pre-Sundering occult secrets, historical records
  • Political Wild Cards: Hunted by Church, tolerated by others; alliances shift
  • Moral Complexity: Players must decide—are Bloodlines monsters or victims of circumstance?

Plot Hooks

  • "The Blood Pact": Bloodline offers party immortality in exchange for dangerous service. Accept or refuse?
  • "Inquisition Hunt": Church hires party to track Bloodline enclave. Do they complete mission or warn targets?
  • "House War": Two Great Houses go to vendetta; party caught in crossfire. Pick side or broker peace?
  • "The Turning": NPC ally bitten/infected; slowly transforming. Cure them or help them adapt?
  • "Elder's Request": 450-year-old vampire seeks true death (soul exhaustion). Party must grant mercy killing.

Playing a Bloodline Character

Roleplaying Tips:

  • Struggle with Thirst (constant low-level hunger; intense after combat or stress)
  • Melancholy worldview (immortality = watching everyone you love die repeatedly)
  • Formal speech patterns (aristocratic mannerisms from pre-Sundering era)
  • Discomfort with sunlight/holy symbols (varies by affliction type)
  • Sense of superiority mixed with isolation ("We are better, but utterly alone")
  • Strong House loyalty (chosen family when biological family is centuries dead)
