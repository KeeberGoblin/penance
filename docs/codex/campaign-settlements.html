Settlement Mechanics

Brutal campaign-inspired Settlement Phase • Version 3.0 • Campaign Progression

"Your settlement is a promise. Will you keep it, or watch it burn?"
✿

Design Philosophy

This settlement system focuses on meaningful scarcity, interconnected upgrades, narrative consequences, asymmetric disasters, and impossible choices. Resources are always limited, forcing strategic priorities.

  • Meaningful scarcity - Resources are always limited, forcing hard choices
  • Interconnected upgrades - Buildings unlock other buildings, creating strategic paths
  • Narrative consequences - Settlements tell stories through their architecture
  • Asymmetric disasters - Settlements can be destroyed, and recovery is brutal
  • No pure optimization - Every choice has a cost
✿

Core Resources

Resource Description How to Gain
Credits Primary currency (max bank: 500) 100 per primary objective, 50 per secondary
Scrap Salvage material for construction 1d3 per enemy Casket destroyed
Population Settlement size (unlocks building slots) +1 per successful mission, starts at 10
Morale Settlement spirit (0-10 scale, affects events) +1 per mission success, -2 per failure

Building Slots

  • Tier 1: Start with 3 building slots
  • Tier 2: Unlocked at Population 20 (adds 2 slots)
  • Tier 3: Unlocked at Population 35 (adds 2 slots)
  • Maximum: 7 building slots total

Tier 1 Buildings (Starting Options)

The Forge [Infrastructure]

Cost: 3 Scrap, 100 Credits
Build Time: Immediate

Effect:

  • Unlocks equipment crafting
  • Repair costs reduced by 25%
  • Special: Scrap Salvage - Convert 1 destroyed equipment into 1 Scrap (once per phase)

A crude workshop with a bellows-forge and anvil. Sparks fly as blacksmiths hammer out repairs.

The Barracks [Military]

Cost: 2 Scrap, 50 Credits
Build Time: Immediate

Effect:

  • Increases pilot roster capacity from 4 to 6
  • Pilots heal 1 additional injury per phase
  • Special: Drill Training - Spend 50 Credits to grant 1 pilot +1 Initiative permanently (max +2)

Rows of cots and training dummies. The smell of sweat and oil.

The Apothecary [Infrastructure]

Cost: 2 Scrap, 75 Credits
Build Time: Immediate

Effect:

  • Reduces injury recovery time by 1 phase
  • Unlocks advanced medical treatments
  • Special: Emergency Stabilization - Prevent 1 pilot death per mission (declare immediately)

Shelves lined with bloodied bandages and herbal tinctures. The screams of the wounded echo.

The Market [Economic]

Cost: 1 Scrap, 50 Credits
Build Time: Immediate

Effect:

  • Generates +25 Credits per settlement phase (passive income)
  • Unlocks Trade Actions
  • Special: Black Market Contact - Purchase 1 equipment at 50% discount (random, once per phase)

A bustling bazaar where refugees trade scavenged goods. Prices are brutal.

The Shrine [Infrastructure]

Cost: 2 Scrap, 50 Credits
Build Time: Immediate

Effect:

  • +1 Morale (passive)
  • Reduces Taint accumulation by 1 per phase (all pilots)
  • Special: Ritual of Cleansing - Spend 100 Credits to remove 2 Taint from 1 pilot

Incense burns before an altar draped in prayer chains. Penitents kneel in silent devotion.

Tier 2 Buildings (Population 20+)

The Arsenal [Military]

Cost: 5 Scrap, 200 Credits
Build Time: 1 phase
Requires: The Forge

Effect:

  • Unlocks Tier 2 equipment crafting (Relic-grade weapons)
  • +1 Relic slot for all Caskets
  • Special: Weapon Enchantment - Spend 150 Credits to upgrade 1 weapon permanently

A vault filled with Soulstone-powered weaponry. The air hums with latent energy.

The Workshop [Infrastructure]

Cost: 4 Scrap, 150 Credits
Build Time: 1 phase
Requires: The Forge

Effect:

  • Unlocks Casket modifications (weight class changes, component swaps)
  • Combine 2 broken equipment into 1 functional item
  • Special: Emergency Retrofit - Rebuild 1 destroyed Casket (200 Credits + 3 Scrap)

A cavernous hangar where Caskets hang on chains like carcasses in a butcher's shop.

Soulstone Refinery [Economic]

Cost: 6 Scrap, 250 Credits
Build Time: 2 phases
Requires: The Forge

Effect:

  • Generates 1 Soulstone Fragment per phase (worth 75 Credits OR special crafting)
  • Unlocks Soulstone-powered equipment
  • Danger: Roll 1d6 each phase. On 1, suffer Void Leak (see Disasters)

A pulsing crystal chamber surrounded by arcane wards. Workers wear lead-lined suits.

The Archive [Special]

Cost: 3 Scrap, 100 Credits
Build Time: 1 phase

Effect:

  • Unlocks Research Actions
  • Ask GM 1 lore question per phase (receive truthful answer)
  • Special: Ancient Schematic - Roll on Lost Technology table for forgotten equipment

A dusty library filled with water-damaged pre-Cataclysm tomes. Knowledge is hoarded here.

The Watchtower [Military]

Cost: 4 Scrap, 100 Credits
Build Time: 1 phase

Effect:

  • Reduces surprise attack chance by 50%
  • May scout next mission before committing (reveal objectives + 1 enemy type)
  • Special: Early Warning System - Avoid 1 settlement disaster entirely (once per campaign)

A crude tower built from scrap metal and salvaged wood. Sentries scan the horizon.

Build Time: 3 phases

Settlement Events Table

Roll 1d20 at the end of each Settlement Phase:

Morale Modifiers: +1 to roll per 2 Morale above 5, -1 per 2 Morale below 5

Roll Event
1-2 Void Rift Opens - Settlement attacked by 1d3 Abominations. Defend or lose 1d6 Population + 1 random building.
3-4 Plague Outbreak - Lose 1d3 Population. If you have The Apothecary, reduce loss by half.
5-6 Raider Attack - Bandits demand 100 Credits. Pay or fight (roll opposed d6).
7-8 Supply Shortage - All repairs cost +50% this phase.
9-10 Refugee Caravan - Gain 1d3 Population. They bring rumors of Soulstone deposits.
11-12 Uneventful - Nothing happens. A rare moment of peace.
13-14 Salvage Discovery - Gain 1d3 Scrap for free.
15-16 Talented Recruit - Gain 1 new pilot with random positive trait.
17-18 Trade Windfall - Merchants offer rare goods. Access to 1 random Tier 2 equipment (discounted 100 Credits).
19-20 Morale Surge - +2 Morale. Gain 50 Credits from donations.

Settlement Disasters

Some events or building failures trigger catastrophic Disasters:

Void Leak (Soulstone Refinery failure)

  • 1d3 Population dies from Taint exposure
  • All pilots gain +1 Taint
  • Refinery disabled until repaired (100 Credits + 2 Scrap)

Fire (Random chance, increases with wooden buildings)

  • Roll 1d6: Lose that number of Scrap
  • 1 random building takes 1 damage (3 damage = destroyed)

Betrayal (Low Morale event, Morale <3)

  • 1 pilot deserts, taking 1 piece of equipment
  • Lose 1d6 Population
  • -2 Morale

Inquisition Raid (High Taint event, any pilot Taint 8+)

  • Church sends Penitent Knights to "cleanse" settlement
  • Fight them off or surrender corrupted pilot
  • If you fight and lose: Lose 2d6 Population + 1 building

Settlement Growth Path (Example Campaign)

Phase 1 (Missions 1-5): Survival

  • Build: Forge, Barracks, Shrine
  • Focus: Repair infrastructure, stockpile Credits
  • Population: 10 → 18

Phase 2 (Missions 6-10): Expansion

  • Build: Workshop, Market, Apothecary
  • Focus: Unlock better equipment, stabilize Morale
  • Population: 18 → 27

Phase 3 (Missions 11-15): Specialization

  • Build: Arsenal OR Soulstone Refinery (choose your path)
  • Focus: Push toward Tier 3 buildings or faction alignment
  • Population: 27 → 40

Phase 4 (Endgame): Powerhouse or Ruin

  • Build: Reliquary, Foundry, or faction-specific building
  • Outcome: Either dominate the campaign or suffer spectacular collapse
