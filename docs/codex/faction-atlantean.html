The Between People: Homo Atlanticus

Atlanteans are not mythological demigods. They are proto-human evolutionary divergents who spent 50,000 years in the Feywild and emerged as something profoundly Other. They are Earth's first great civilization, transformed by dimensional exile into post-human beings who exist between realities.

"Imagine encountering a 6'6" humanoid with iridescent teal skin that shifts like oil on water, eyes of solid aquamarine with no pupils that seem to look through time itself, and hair that flows as if underwater even in still air. When they move, reality seems to hesitate around them. When they speak, you hear echoes of words they haven't said yet. They are beautiful. They are wrong. They are home after 50,000 years, and the world doesn't recognize them." — Visual Identity of Atlanteans

The Lost History: Earth's First Civilization

The Founding (~62,000 BCE)

Atlantis was founded at the Eye of the Sahara (Richat Structure), the most powerful twelve-fold leyline nexus on Earth. Early proto-humans discovered the site's unique properties and built a civilization that existed simultaneously in two realities:

  • Material Anchor: Physical bodies, agriculture, industry, architecture existed on Earth
  • Feywild Resonance: Consciousness, magic, art, dreams extended into the Feywild
  • Leyline Bridge: The twelve-fold nexus maintained a permanent dimensional gateway between both planes

The Dimensional Engine Architecture

The Eye of the Sahara wasn't just a city location; it was a machine. The Atlanteans built their civilization into the geological formation itself:

  • Outer Ring (40km diameter): Leyline capacitors; massive stone circles storing dimensional energy
  • Middle Ring (25km diameter): Distribution network; channels routing power throughout the city
  • Inner Ring (10km diameter): The City of Atlantis; crystalline spires, bio-luminescent gardens, archives, temples
  • Central Nexus (2km diameter): The Dimensional Anchor; a 500-meter crystalline obelisk maintaining the Feywild bridge

This wasn't mere architecture. It was dimensional stabilization technology using Earth's natural leylines to anchor a city in dual reality.

The First Cataclysm: The Deluge (Year -50,000 BCE)

A catastrophic leyline surge, similar to the later Theslar Event, destabilized Earth's entire leyline network. When the surge reached the Eye of the Sahara, the twelve-fold nexus overloaded. The dimensional bridge holding Atlantis in dual-reality shattered.

The Atlantean architects faced an impossible choice:

Option Consequence
Anchor fully to Material Plane City survives physically, but millions die in global floods. Lose dimensional abilities
Phase entirely into Feywild City and population survive intact, but severed from Earth. Exile in alien dimension

They chose exile. Over three days, Atlantis phased completely into the Feywild. From Earth's perspective, the city vanished. Myths called it "sinking beneath the waves." The truth: it slipped sideways into another dimension.

The Phasing Cycle: Atlantis Across History

Atlantis hasn't been exiled once. It has been phasing between Material Plane and Feywild for 62,000 years, pulled back and forth by leyline surges. Each return lasts centuries or mere decades before another surge forces them back. They are perpetual wanderers, cursed to return to an Earth that never stays the same.

Return Era Duration Human Record
First Founding ~62,000-50,000 BCE 12,000 years None (pre-history)
Second Return ~10,000-8,000 BCE 2,000 years "Great Flood" myths

Draconid Secret: This is the seventh return, not the fourth. Three earlier cycles were lost to history. The Draconids warn: "The pattern holds. They will fail again."

How the Cycles Changed Them

Extended exposure to the Feywild (totaling ~50,000 years across all cycles) fundamentally transformed the Atlanteans. They evolved from Homo sapiens atlanticus (enhanced humans) into Homo Atlanticus (post-human Fae-touched):

The Return (Year 412): When the Lost Came Home

How the Sundering Brought Them Back

The Theslar Event (Year 0) didn't just tear open the Void; it supercharged Earth's dormant leyline network. By Year 412, enough leylines had regenerated that the Eye of the Sahara's twelve-fold nexus reignited.

From the Feywild, the Atlanteans detected the gateway reopening. After 50,000 years of exile, the door home had opened. Over three days, they debated whether to return to a transformed, hostile Earth... or remain safe in the Feywild forever.

They chose home. Even if home no longer recognized them.

Atlantis in Year 437: The Dual-Reality City

Modern Atlantis exists simultaneously in Material Plane and Feywild, creating bizarre phenomena:

  • Quantum Architecture: Buildings phase in/out of visibility. Streets exist in one reality but not the other. Rooms are bigger inside than outside
  • Temporal Distortion: Time flows 20% slower inside city borders. 5 days outside = 4 days inside. Clocks behave erratically
  • Reality Bleeding: Feywild entities (sprites, wisps, minor Fae) randomly manifest in city streets, then vanish
  • Leyline Glow: Entire city emits soft teal bioluminescence from leyline-powered infrastructure. Visible from 200+ miles away on clear nights (Nomad caravans use it for navigation)
  • Perception Distortion: Visitors report intense déjà vu, temporal disorientation, feeling watched by something just out of sight

The Three Iconic Atlanteans

1. High Architect Thalassar, The First Exile

Age: 52,000 years old (subjectively experienced 800 years) | Role: Political leader, quest-giver, tragic figure

Physical Description

  • Height: 6'8", impossibly slender
  • Skin: Deep ocean blue with silver iridescence. Leyline veins glow bright teal across entire body
  • Eyes: Solid aquamarine, no pupils. Appear to focus on things not present
  • Hair: White with bioluminescent blue streaks. Flows constantly as if underwater
  • Presence: When he speaks, nearby water levitates. Small objects orbit him slowly

Personality & Tragedy

Thalassar is the only Atlantean who has endured every phase cycle since the First Founding in -62,000 BCE. He has watched Atlantis return to Material reality at least seven times, witnessed the rise and fall of countless civilizations, and forgotten more history than humanity has recorded.

Chronologically 62,000 years old, subjectively ~1,200 years of conscious experience. He remembers when humans were still hunter-gatherers. He remembers Plato documenting Atlantis's Third Return. He remembers every time the leylines failed and pulled them back into the Feywild.

He maintains strict isolationism because he knows how this ends. Every return follows the same pattern: brief hope, conflict with surface dwellers, inevitable phasing back to Feywild. He's done this seven times. He expects an eighth. The tragedy: he can't stop it, and his people won't remember when it happens again.

Story Hooks

  • Quest: "Retrieve artifacts from pre-Sundering civilizations. I must understand what happened to Earth while we were gone."
  • Leyline Expertise: Can identify leyline disruptions, predict leyline surges, teach players leyline navigation
  • Diplomatic Mediator: Atlantis is neutral ground. Thalassar can broker truces between hostile factions
  • Temporal Knowledge: Occasionally experiences precognitive visions. Can warn players of future dangers (cryptic, 50% accurate)

Atlantean Technology & Lore

Leyline Engineering

Atlanteans are masters of leyline manipulation: technology 50,000 years more advanced than any post-Sundering faction.

  • Memory Crystals: Store knowledge, memories, skills. Players can learn abilities by interfacing with crystals
  • Dimensional Anchors: Portable devices stabilizing reality rifts. Essential for Void exploration
  • Leyline Capacitors: Store leyline energy for later use. Superior to Soulstones (rechargeable, non-corruptive)
  • Phase Cloth: Fabric existing in Material + Feywild. Provides protection against Void corruption
  • Temporal Beacons: Mark locations in spacetime. Allow recall to previous positions (limited teleportation)

Atlantean Trade Goods

Atlanteans rarely trade, but when they do, they offer items impossible to replicate:

What They Want:

  • Pre-Sundering artifacts (historical documentation)
  • Samples of Void-corrupted materials (research purposes)

  • Rare leyline-attuned minerals

What They Offer:

  • Memory Crystal (Common): Contains skill training (grants +1 to specific stat after study). Cost: 800 Credits or equivalent favor
  • Phase Cloth Cloak (Rare): +2 Defense, immune to Void spatial distortions. Cost: 2,000 Credits
  • Dimensional Anchor (Rare): Stabilizes reality rifts within 50 feet. Prevents Void incursions. Cost: 3,500 Credits
  • Leyline Capacitor (Legendary): Stores 20 leyline energy. Rechargeable. Superior Soulstone alternative. Cost: 5,000 Credits or major favor
  • Temporal Recall Beacon (Legendary): Set waypoint, teleport back once. Single use. Cost: 7,000 Credits

The Grimdark Tragedy of Atlantis

Atlantis's return is not a triumph; it is a slow-motion extinction event. The Atlanteans came home to a world that no longer wants them, piloting bodies that can no longer reproduce, clinging to memories of an Earth that died 50,000 years ago.

Why Atlantis Is Doomed

1. Terminal Sterility

50,000 years in the Feywild rendered Atlanteans nearly sterile. Birth rate: 1 per 1,000 attempts. Current population: ~47,000. At current rates, Atlantis will be extinct in 3,000-5,000 years. They returned home only to watch their species die.

2. Feywild Dependency

Atlanteans can no longer survive purely in Material reality. They require periodic Feywild immersion (minimum 6 hours per week). If the leyline nexus fails again, they have weeks before physical degradation begins. Their immortality is conditional.

3. Temporal Erosion

Living in non-linear time for 50,000 years damaged Atlantean neurology. They experience "temporal echoes": memories of futures that never happened, dreams of pasts they didn't live. 8% suffer permanent temporal psychosis (lost in time, unable to distinguish past/present/future).

4. Cultural Stagnation

Because they're functionally immortal, Atlantean culture has stopped evolving. No generational turnover means no new ideas, no fresh perspectives. They are a civilization frozen in amber, slowly forgetting how to change.

5. Existential Loneliness

The Atlanteans are cosmically alone. They remember an Earth with megafauna, ice age glaciers, Neanderthals. Everyone they knew outside Atlantis died 50,000 years ago. They returned to a world of strangers who view them as aliens. Many suffer profound depression.

"Why did you come back?" the Draconids ask.

"Because home is not a place," Thalassar answers. "Home is a promise. And we promised we would return. Even if no one remembers. Even if no one cares. We came home." — High Architect Thalassar, Year 425

Campaign Integration: Using Atlanteans

Early Game (Missions 1-3)

Rumor Hooks: Tales of "ghost city in the Sahara" where reality breaks. First Encounter: Players detect leyline anomaly leading to Atlantis border. Thalassar grants audience. Reward: Basic leyline navigation training, Memory Crystal with Void entity weaknesses.

Mid Game (Missions 4-6)

Quest Chain: Thessia requests pre-Sundering artifacts for Archive. Kael'thas Support: Can hire as ally for anti-Void missions. Trade Access: Unlock ability to purchase Atlantean technology. Choice Point: Ally with Atlanteans (lose Church favor, gain dimensional knowledge) OR reject them.

Late Game (Missions 7+)

Leyline Crisis: Eye of the Sahara nexus destabilizing. Atlantis may phase back to Feywild permanently. Final Quest: Help stabilize nexus (keeps Atlantis on Earth) OR sabotage it (forces them back to exile). Consequence: Decision affects ending. Atlantis becomes permanent ally OR vanishes forever, leaving only Memory Crystals behind.

Secret Endgame Hook

The Draconid Warning: Draconid archives reveal Atlantis has returned three times across history. Second return (~10,000 BCE) failed; Atlanteans forgot due to temporal damage. They're trapped in a loop: Return → Fail → Forget → Return. Will Year 437 break the cycle, or repeat it?

Design Philosophy (GM Notes)

Why NPC-Only?

  • Atlanteans are too powerful as playable (phase shifting, precognition, dimensional manipulation)
  • Their isolationist philosophy conflicts with player-driven adventure ("Why fight? We've seen civilizations rise and fall a hundred times.")
  • Serve better as quest-givers and lore dispensers than protagonists
  • Rarity preserves mystique and tragedy; every Atlantean encounter is meaningful

Thematic Role: The Ghosts of the Past

  • Living History: Atlanteans are witnesses to 62,000 years of Earth's history. They know secrets buried before writing was invented
  • Tragic Mirrors: Their exile mirrors the post-Sundering world; both are broken, changed, struggling to survive in transformed realities
  • Existential Horror: They're functionally immortal but doomed to extinction. They survived 50,000 years only to die in the next 5,000
  • The Unanswered Question: Why did they return? Nostalgia? Hope? Stubbornness? Players may never know
"We are ghosts haunting our own graves. We returned to a world that buried us 50,000 years ago and moved on. But we remember. And memory, for us, is both prison and sanctuary." — Archivist Thessia, Year 437
