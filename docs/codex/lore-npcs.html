Iconic Pilots: Legends of Iron and Blood

"Every pilot is a tragedy in motion. The only question is: how long until they break?" —Bonelord Thresh, The Ledger of Souls

These are the living legends of Year 437—pilots whose names are whispered in taverns and scrawled in combat logs. Each has paid a terrible price for their power. Each carries scars that tell stories of survival, mutation, and the cost of binding one's fate to a Casket.

✿

Church of Absolution

1. Sister Vex - "The Hollow Penitent"

Portrait Description:
Sister Vex's most striking feature is her complete lack of facial features—a smooth expanse of ritually burned scar tissue where eyes, nose, and mouth once were. Communion-stones pulse with dull red light beneath the scarred skin, forming a constellation pattern across her faceless head. Prayer-chains are welded directly to her exposed ribs, visible through torn High Penitent robes of faded crimson. Patches of crystalline Soulstone growth protrude from her shoulders and collarbone like grotesque tumors, glowing faintly with inner light. Her hands are wrapped in blood-stained cloth, and her posture suggests both exhaustion and unyielding determination. The overall impression is of a martyr who has transcended humanity through suffering.

Detail Information
Casket Heavy - "The Confessional" (1,600 lbs)
Taint Level 7 (Advanced Corruption)
Key Trait Martyrdom Protocol - Cannot die, sacrifices permanent injuries to survive

Once the Inquisition's most feared prosecutor, Vex attempted to save her Taint-corrupted daughter through a forbidden blood ritual. The child died, but the Taint took root in Vex's soul. Rather than face execution, she mutilated herself—removing her face so she could no longer weep. The Church declared her a saint. She seeks death in every battle, but death refuses to claim her.

"Pain is proof of devotion. I will bear your sins."

2. Inquisitor Grayle - "The Absolver"

Portrait Description:
Grayle is a towering figure, 6'7" with the build of an executioner. His head is shaved clean and tattooed with scripture in microscopic text, covering every inch of scalp and extending down his neck. His right eye is a milky white cataract, the result of staring into a Void rift during an exorcism; his left eye burns with fanatical intensity. He wears heavy crimson armor plates over black leather, each plate engraved with the names of heretics he's "absolved" (executed). His jaw is clenched perpetually, teeth filed to points in an act of self-mortification. Burn scars cover his hands from handling blessed fire without gloves. He carries himself with the rigid posture of absolute conviction—a man who has never doubted, never questioned, never shown mercy.

Detail Information
Casket Assault - "The Pyre" (1,800 lbs)
Taint Level 0 (Obsessively purified)
Key Trait Righteous Immolation - Sets self on fire to deal bonus damage

Grayle has personally executed 412 "heretics" and "Taint-bearers" in his 20-year career. He believes suffering purifies the soul, and fire is the ultimate sacrament. His Casket, The Pyre, is equipped with promethium flamethrowers and blessed incendiary rounds. He underwent voluntary blinding in one eye to "see only truth" and files his teeth to remind himself he is a weapon, not a man. He hunts Void-corrupted with relentless zeal, viewing mercy as weakness and compromise as heresy.

"The fire does not judge. It only cleanses. Submit to absolution."

Dwarven Forge-Guilds

3. Forgemaster Durr - "The Calculator"

Portrait Description:
Durr is more machine than dwarf—60% of his body replaced with clockwork prosthetics. His left arm is a multi-tool apparatus with rotating drill bits and precision instruments. His right leg is a piston-driven hydraulic limb that hisses with each step. One eye is a magnifying lens array that clicks and rotates mechanically when he focuses. A glass window in his sternum reveals a pressure gauge tracking his blood pressure in real-time. His beard is patchy and singed from forge accidents, and his remaining flesh is covered in self-inflicted measurement scars. He carries calipers and rulers at all times, obsessively measuring everything. His expression is emotionless, analytical—the face of a man who has replaced feeling with calculation.

Detail Information
Casket Support - "The Abacus" (1,100 lbs)
Taint Level 3 (Controlled/Measured)
Key Trait Probability Matrix - Knows exact card counts, manipulates deck order

Lead engineer on the catastrophic Echo Project—a failed attempt to replicate the Theslar Engine. A rounding error in the 47th decimal place killed 200 dwarves. Durr survived buried under rubble for three days, calculating the exact engineering failure. The realization broke him: the universe is a poorly designed machine running on faulty math. He rebuilt himself with prosthetics to "eliminate biological inefficiencies" and now treats everything—emotions, combat, morality—as engineering problems to be solved through calculation.

"Randomness is just insufficient data. Acceptable losses for optimal outcome."

4. Ironwright Karsa - "Rust-Eater"

Portrait Description:
Karsa is a stocky dwarf woman with iron-gray hair braided with copper wire and rivets. Her skin has a metallic sheen—the result of decades of Soulstone exposure causing mineral deposits in her flesh. Her teeth are bronze replacements, filed sharp, and her tongue is stained black from "tasting" metal alloys (she can identify ore composition by licking it). Her hands are perpetually stained with rust and oil, nails cracked and blackened. She wears a heavy leather apron over runic armor, the apron pockets bulging with scrap metal, bolts, and improvised tools. Acid burn scars cover her forearms from experimental metalwork. Her eyes are the color of molten iron, and her expression is one of fierce, stubborn pride—a master craftsman who refuses to acknowledge limits.

Detail Information
Casket Heavy - "The Crucible" (2,000 lbs)
Taint Level 5 (Metal Corruption)
Key Trait Salvage Master - Repairs mid-combat using enemy wreckage

Karsa earned her title by surviving the Ashveil Collapse—a mine disaster where she was trapped for 11 days with no food. She survived by eating rust scraped from mine walls, her body adapting to metabolize metal. Now she's obsessed with perfecting "living metal"—Casket components that repair themselves. The Crucible is her workshop and weapon, equipped with mobile forge and welding arms. She views scrapping enemy Caskets as "recycling" and has filed her teeth to bite through steel cable (she once chewed through her own restraints during capture).

"Waste nothing. Break nothing you can't rebuild. And if you can't rebuild it, melt it down."

The Ossuarium

5. Bonelord Thresh - "The Debt Collector"

Portrait Description:
Thresh is a skeletal lich wrapped in tattered nobleman's robes—moth-eaten velvet and silk from the Old World. His skull is pristine ivory, polished to a mirror sheen, with rubies socketed in his eye sockets that glow with cold, calculating light. Every bone in his skeleton is inscribed with microscopic contract text—thousands of names covering every surface. He has too many bones—extra ribs, redundant finger bones, spare vertebrae grafted from previous owners like grotesque trophies. His jaw moves with unsettling precision when he speaks, and the names etched on his bones whisper in chorus. He wears jeweled rings on skeletal fingers and carries himself with the posture of a corrupt aristocrat—polite, refined, and utterly remorseless.

Detail Information
Casket Assault - "The Creditor" (2,200 lbs)
Taint Level N/A (Undead - immune via technicality)
Key Trait Predatory Pacts - Forces enemies to accept cursed bargains

Before the Cataclysm, Thresh was a loan shark and contract lawyer. When the Sundering occurred, he made necromantic pacts with dying survivors, promising "eternal life" in exchange for "small favors after death." Hundreds signed. They didn't read the fine print. Thresh kept his promises—he granted eternal life. But the contracts stipulated souls remained his property, to be called upon for labor, combat, or experimentation. He's built an undead empire on predatory lending.

"All debts come due. Your death is merely a change of employer."

6. Mortis the Silencer - "The Quiet Reaper"

Portrait Description:
Mortis is a haunting figure—gaunt, pale, with translucent skin that reveals the shadow of bones beneath. His eyes are completely black, no whites, like staring into empty sockets. His mouth is sewn shut with silver thread, rendering him completely mute; he communicates through a bone-inscribed sign language only the Ossuarium understands. His hair is white as bleached bone and hangs lank around a hollow-cheeked face. He wears a plague doctor's mask over his sewn mouth when in public, adding to his unsettling appearance. His hands are skeletal thin, with abnormally long fingers that move with spider-like precision. He's covered in preservation runes carved into his flesh—rituals to prevent his body from fully dying or fully living. His presence drains sound; rooms grow quieter when he enters, voices become whispers.

Detail Information
Casket Scout - "The Mausoleum" (700 lbs)
Taint Level 8 (Sustained Death State)
Key Trait Silence Field - Dampens all sound, prevents enemy communication

Mortis was an Ossuarium assassin who botched a soul-binding ritual, trapping himself in a liminal state between life and death. He cannot speak, cannot sleep, cannot feel warmth. His body temperature is permanently 50°F. He's become the Ossuarium's most effective infiltrator, using his Silence Field to muffle sound within 30 feet—footsteps, weapon fire, even screams vanish into eerie quiet. He hunts with surgical precision, binding souls before victims realize they're dead. Some say his sewn mouth hides a scream that's been building for 15 years.

Elven Verdant Covenant

7. Rootwarden Kess - "The Pruner"

Portrait Description:
Kess is no longer entirely elven—her lower body has fused with her Casket, roots and vines growing through legs, spine, and pelvis. Her skin has a greenish tint, with bark-like growths covering shoulders and forearms. Her hands have split into twig-like appendages, allowing direct interface with living wood machinery. Most disturbing are her eyes: solid amber with no pupils or whites, and sap occasionally leaks from the corners like tears. Thorny vines grow from her scalp instead of hair, constantly writhing. Her expression is eerily calm, even serene, as if she's achieved some plant-like enlightenment beyond mortal concerns. Lichen patches grow on her exposed skin, and small flowers bloom from cracks in her bark-flesh.

Detail Information
Casket Scout - "The Briar-Thorn" (650 lbs)
Taint Level 0 (Immune - Casket filters corruption)
Key Trait Rootbound - Cannot leave Casket, immune to Taint

Kess was a Rootwarden responsible for "pruning" corrupted ley lines. When a Void rift opened in her grove, she grafted herself to a World-Tree sapling to filter the Taint. It worked—the ley line was saved. But Kess and the sapling merged irreversibly, becoming something neither plant nor elf. She now "prunes" in a different way—hunting corruption sources and cutting them away with surgical precision. She believes the entire world is a garden, and she is the gardener. Everything that doesn't belong must be removed.

"Cut away the diseased limb. The world heals where I walk."

8. Thornwalker Sylris - "Blood-Bloom"

Portrait Description:
Sylris is beautiful in a disturbing way—too perfect, like a porcelain doll. Her skin is pale as birch bark, and her hair is living crimson vines that flow like blood-red water. Her eyes are deep forest green with vertical slit pupils like a predator. Most striking are the thorns growing from her body—emerging from joints, shoulders, and along her spine like a natural weapon. When she bleeds (which is often, from self-inflicted ritual cuts), flowers bloom from the blood. She wears living armor—bark plates that have grown onto her skin, impossible to remove without flaying her alive. Crimson sap weeps from cracks in the bark. She moves with unnatural grace, every gesture deliberate and ritualistic, as if she's performing a sacred dance even in combat.

Detail Information
Casket Assault - "The Bleeding Garden" (1,300 lbs)
Taint Level 6 (Nature Corruption)
Key Trait Blood Bloom - Converts damage taken into area-of-effect thorn attacks

Sylris performed the Crimson Rite—a forbidden ritual where elves merge with carnivorous plants to gain predatory power. She bonded with a Void-touched Blood Vine, and now she feeds on violence. Her Casket, The Bleeding Garden, sprouts thorny vines that impale enemies and drain their fluids. She views combat as sacred—an offering to the "Old Growth," a deity she claims whispered to her during the ritual. Other elves fear her as a heretic, but she's undeniably effective. She cuts herself before battle as a prayer, letting flowers bloom from her wounds.

"The forest drinks deep. Blood is the best fertilizer. Let me show you."

The Wyrd Conclave (Fae)

9. Mockingbird - "The Many-Masked Bargainer"

Portrait Description:
Mockingbird doesn't have a face—or rather, she has too many. She wears constantly shifting porcelain masks, each representing a stolen identity, phasing in and out like reflections in broken mirrors. Beneath the masks is nothing but void filled with distant stars and impossible geometries. Her body is similarly unstable: sometimes two arms, sometimes six, sometimes none. Her height varies by several feet moment to moment. Her voice is a chorus of stolen identities speaking in overlapping echoes. She wears layered robes of shimmering fabric that looks like captured moonlight, and her presence distorts space slightly—doorways become oval, shadows point the wrong direction. Staring at her for too long causes vertigo and migraines.

Detail Information
Casket Aberrant - "The Mirror-Dance" (800 lbs - variable)
Taint Level ??? (Unmeasurable - Fae physiology)
Key Trait Stolen Reflection - Copies enemy abilities mid-combat

Mockingbird is ancient—old enough she's forgotten her true name. She collects possibilities—alternate versions of events that never happened. When the Sundering opened rifts, she walked through, fascinated by this broken world. She doesn't fight for territory or resources, but for stories—collecting "what-ifs" of every battle. What if the hero turned coward? What if the dead lived? She steals alternate outcomes and wears them like masks, becoming a walking archive of roads not taken.

"I'll borrow that, thank you. Reality is negotiable."

10. The Veil-Walker - "Sorrow's Echo"

Portrait Description:
The Veil-Walker appears as a slender androgynous figure draped in translucent silk that seems to exist in multiple dimensions simultaneously. Their skin is opalescent, shifting between pale white, deep violet, and chromatic reflections depending on the angle of light. They have no face—instead, a smooth mirror surface reflects the viewer's greatest regret. Their hands end in impossibly long fingers (seven per hand, not five) that trail wisps of silver mist. They wear a crown of moth wings and shattered glass that hovers above their head without touching. Their movements are wrong—they walk forward but cast shadows that move backward. Reality distorts around them: colors invert, time stutters, gravity weakens. Those who meet their gaze see glimpses of their alternate selves in parallel worlds.

Detail Information
Casket Scout - "The Folded Path" (500 lbs - unstable)
Taint Level N/A (Exists partially outside reality)
Key Trait Phase-Walk - Moves through solid matter, immune to physical damage

The Veil-Walker claims to be "a sorrow that learned to move." They are Fae who became obsessed with human grief and began collecting it, wearing regrets like jewelry. They can phase between dimensions, existing in multiple realities simultaneously. Their Casket, The Folded Path, operates on non-Euclidean principles—it can be in two places at once or nowhere at all. They offer impossible bargains: "Give me your saddest memory, and I'll make you forget what made you sad." Those who accept find themselves hollow, emotions muted, unable to feel joy or pain.

"I am the space between heartbeats. The pause before tears. Give me your grief—you won't need it where I'm taking you."

Nomad Collective

11. "Three-Card" Morrow - "The Drifter King"

Portrait Description:
Morrow has weathered leather skin crisscrossed with radiation scars and frostbite damage from decades in the Wastes. Most memorable are the three playing cards permanently embedded in his left eye socket—Ace of Spades, Nine of Hearts, and Joker—fused there by a Soulstone explosion. His right arm is entirely prosthetic scavenged junk held together with wire, covered in welded-on trinkets: rabbit feet, dice, compass needles. He wears a tattered duster coat with patched holes, each patch from a different convoy he's guided. His remaining eye is gray and tired, and his face carries the look of a man who's gambled with death and keeps winning despite wanting to lose. Dust and ash are permanently ground into his skin and clothes.

Detail Information
Casket Scout - "The Tumbledown" (720 lbs)
Taint Level 4 (Fluctuating)
Key Trait Lucky Draw - Flip coin to negate damage or take double

Morrow lost an entire convoy—forty souls—to an unmarked Void rift. Only he survived. The guilt broke him, and he became a drifter, gambling with death. When a Soulstone engine exploded two feet from his face, cards embedded in his eye socket. Should've killed him. Didn't. Now he "reads" fortunes from the embedded cards, and traders say if Morrow warns you to turn back, you turn back. He carries three cards as a reminder: "You're dealt three chances. I used mine."

"Three-Card Morrow doesn't dodge. Fate does it for him. You either win big or lose everything."

12. Wraith-Caller Yenna - "The Dust Prophet"

Portrait Description:
Yenna is draped in layers of scavenged cloth—tribal wrappings, torn military fatigues, ancient religious vestments—all stained with rust and Wasteland dust. Her face is wrapped in gauze bandages, only her eyes visible—milky white with cataracts caused by staring into dust storms for vision quests. Tribal tattoos cover every inch of exposed skin in geometric patterns representing "the paths reality can take." Her hands are gnarled and scarred from handling probability charms made of scavenged tech, bone, and Void-touched crystals. She carries a staff hung with dozens of small mirrors and glass shards that tinkle eerily in the wind. Her posture is that of a crone, hunched and ancient despite being only 40 years old—the Wasteland ages people fast. Dust perpetually swirls around her as if drawn to her presence.

Detail Information
Casket Support - "The Augury" (900 lbs)
Taint Level 7 (Void-Touched Clairvoyance)
Key Trait Probability Sight - Sees 3 turns into future, warns allies

Yenna is a Nomad prophet who survived a Void storm by allowing it to "rewrite her eyes." She can now see probability patterns—the branching paths of possible futures. This gift drove her half-mad, as she experiences all potential outcomes simultaneously. Her Casket, The Augury, is covered in probability charms and scrying equipment. She guides Nomad convoys through the Wastes by reading dust patterns and Void currents. Her predictions are 80% accurate, but she cannot see her own death—a blind spot that terrifies her. She speaks in cryptic warnings and riddles.

"I see the paths you might walk. Three lead to ash. One leads to survival. But which will you choose?"

The Exchange

13. Proctor Ilyara Voss - "The Actuary of Ash"

Portrait Description:
Voss appears as if she stepped from a corporate boardroom into Hell. She wears a pristine business suit under her pilot harness—gold cufflinks, silk tie, hair pulled into a severe bun graying at the temples. Her defining feature is the mechanical jaw—half her face blown off in a "hostile acquisition," replaced with hinged steel prosthetic that clicks audibly when she speaks, giving her voice a metallic rasp. Her left hand is covered in contract scars—ritual cuts where blood oaths were sealed, each representing deals worth 100,000+ Soulstone shards. She has 14 scars. Her remaining eye is cold gray, calculating, devoid of empathy. Her posture is rigid, professional, and her expression suggests she's perpetually evaluating your worth.

Detail Information
Casket Heavy - "The Ledger" (1,400 lbs)
Taint Level 2 (Controlled via quarterly cleansing fees)
Key Trait Hostile Buyout - Bribes enemies to stand down mid-combat

Voss rose to Proctor rank by exposing a bidding war scheme during the Scorched Sky Auction, bankrupting rivals and claiming Soulstone deposits. She created the Ashen Credit System—wartime loans at 40% interest. Can't pay? Your survivors become indentured labor. Her Casket, The Ledger, is a mobile vault equipped with communication suite to call in mercenaries, artillery, and legal injunctions. She's the most hated and richest woman in three provinces. Every mission is a business transaction; every casualty is a line item.

"Everyone has a price. Even you. I sell you hope. You buy it. We both profit."

14. Broker Tannis - "The Coin-Counter"

Portrait Description:
Tannis is a wiry man with thinning hair slicked back with expensive oils, wearing spectacles with multiple magnifying lenses for examining Soulstone quality. His fingers are permanently stained black from handling soul currency, and he obsessively counts invisible coins—a nervous tic from decades of accounting. He dresses in fine merchant robes embroidered with gold thread, but beneath the finery he wears a weighted vest filled with emergency Soulstone shards (he trusts no vault). His eyes are sharp, darting, constantly assessing value. He has a merchant's smile—too wide, too practiced, never reaching his eyes. Acid scars mar his forearms from soul-acid burns during a vault robbery he barely survived. He carries multiple ledgers chained to his belt.

Detail Information
Casket Scout - "The Coffer" (800 lbs)
Taint Level 1 (Minimal—pays for premium cleansing)
Key Trait Soul Insurance - Spends Soulstones to negate damage

Tannis is a field broker—The Exchange's frontline negotiator who purchases salvage and souls directly from battlefields. His Casket, The Coffer, is a mobile vault and treasury on legs. He's mastered "soul insurance"—spending Soulstone shards to literally buy his way out of death, converting currency into healing energy. He's survived 47 combat missions through cowardice, bribery, and calculated retreats. He views honor as "an expense with no return on investment" and has no shame about fleeing when odds turn unfavorable. His motto: "Dead men sign no contracts."

"Calculate the risk. Hedge your bets. Diversify your survival portfolio. And when in doubt, run."

Vestige Bloodlines

15. "Woundwright" Tallis - "The Stitcher"

Portrait Description:
Tallis is a patchwork of grafted animal parts—wolf forelimb for left arm, eagle's golden eye in right socket, deer legs allowing digitigrade locomotion. His torso is sewn shut with thick black thread as if his entire body is a quilt of stolen flesh. When he moves, stitches creak and sometimes tear, leaking black ichor. His face is mostly human but his mouth is split at the corners like a Glasgow smile, revealing too many teeth. He wears a blood-stained leather apron covered in surgical tools and vials. His remaining human skin is covered in fur, scales, chitin, and feathers—all stitched together with surgical precision. He moves with uncanny grace, animal instincts merged with human intelligence.

Detail Information
Casket Assault - "The Abattoir" (1,200 lbs)
Taint Level 12 (Permanent Mutation)
Key Trait Battlefield Surgery - Grafts mutations mid-combat

Tallis was a Church field medic when a Void rift exposed his garrison to mutation energy. As his body transformed, he realized he could control the process—directing where mutations took hold. He became the first Woundwright—a surgeon who grafts Blighted mutations onto others. His philosophy: "The body is just meat. Meat can be rewritten." He's created abominations and saved lives by grafting resistant tissue onto radiation victims. His Casket, The Abattoir, is a living workshop that grafts mutations onto itself, adapting to threats.

"Hold still. This won't hurt. Much. Waste not. Let me show you what you could become."

16. Apex-Strain Vor - "The Devourer"

Portrait Description:
Vor is barely humanoid—a hulking mass of predatory mutations standing 7'4" tall. His head is wolf-like with too many eyes (six total) scattered asymmetrically across his skull. His jaw unhinges like a snake's, and his teeth are replaced with jagged obsidian shards he's jammed into his gums. His arms are asymmetrical: left is a mantis-claw scythe, right is a bear's limb with retractable talons. His torso is covered in chitinous plates growing from beneath the skin, pushing outward in grotesque ridges. His spine has extra vertebrae, giving him serpentine flexibility. Most horrifying is his regeneration—wounds visibly heal in real-time, flesh knitting together with wet, organic sounds. He's covered in ritual scars from "feeding ceremonies" where he consumed enemy pilots mid-battle.

Detail Information
Casket Heavy - "The Maw" (1,900 lbs)
Taint Level 15 (Beyond Standard Scale)
Key Trait Cannibalize - Consumes destroyed enemies to regenerate HP

Vor is an Apex-Strain Vestige—a mutation so advanced he's become a new apex predator. He embraces the Bloodlines' philosophy of "evolution through consumption," literally eating defeated enemies to absorb their genetic traits. His Casket, The Maw, has a functioning digestive system and can consume scrap metal to repair itself. Vor speaks in guttural, broken sentences, his intelligence degrading as his predatory instincts dominate. He's worshipped by some Vestiges as "the future of humanity" and hunted by others as a cautionary tale. He grows stronger with every kill.

"Hungry. Always hungry. You meat. Good meat. Strong meat. I eat. I become."

Emergent Syndicate

Their Casket, The Metamorph, is bio-organic and grows with them, capable of reconfiguring for flight, burrowing, or combat. Zephyr speaks with unsettling calm, referring to themselves in plural ("We believe," "Our transformation"). They're evangelical about the "Chosen Evolution," trying to convert others.

Grikka is a Boom-Shaman—spiritual leader of the Crucible Packs, who ritually blinded herself by staring into a volcanic vent during a vision quest. She claims the First Forge deity speaks to her through the flames, teaching explosive recipes and predicting where the earth will crack. Her Casket, The Ash-Prophet, is a mobile shrine covered in totems of ash and bone. She performs pre-battle rites, blessing crude bombs with prayers and sprinkling them with volcanic ash. Her prophecies are disturbingly accurate—she once predicted an earthquake 30 seconds before it hit. Goblins view her as touched by fire, simultaneously revered and feared.

"The Forge whispers in cracks and booms. It says: burn bright, burn fast, burn everything. Ashes return to ashes."

Using Iconic Pilots in Play

As Antagonists

Each pilot can serve as a Raid Boss with their own AI behavior deck, or as recurring campaign villains with shifting motivations. Their unique abilities make them memorable encounters that force players to adapt tactics.

As Allies

Players might temporarily ally with these pilots during crisis events (Abomination raids, faction wars), creating uncomfortable partnerships with deeply flawed individuals.

As Faction Representatives

Use these pilots during Faction Negotiation scenes—they embody their faction's philosophy in twisted, extreme ways, showing what happens when ideology is taken to its logical conclusion.

As Cautionary Tales

These pilots demonstrate what happens when you lean too hard into Casket piloting:

  • Vex & Grayle (Church) - Martyrdom becomes addiction; righteousness becomes cruelty

  • Thresh & Mortis (Ossuarium) - You exploit others until you're hollow; death becomes routine
  • Kess & Sylris (Elves) - You merge with nature until you're no longer yourself
  • Mockingbird & Veil-Walker (Fae) - You forget what's real; identity dissolves
  • Morrow & Yenna (Nomads) - Survival becomes obsession; prophecy drives madness
  • Voss & Tannis (Exchange) - Everything becomes transactional; empathy dies
  • Tallis & Vor (Vestiges) - Evolution becomes horror; humanity is sacrificed
  • Zephyr & Krix (Syndicate) - Individuality vanishes; the self becomes collective
  • Skragg & Grikka (Crucible) - Destruction becomes worship; chaos is faith
