Current Mechanics (v5.29-FINAL)

Dice System

  • Attack Dice: 2d6 custom [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 0/JAM]
  • Base Target: 5+ (58% hit rate) - CRITICAL: Was bugged at 4+ causing 24% power inflation
  • Defense Dice: 1d6 per damage (33% block rate)
  • Catastrophic Failure: 2 JAMs = weapon jam, +2 Heat, -2 damage next attack
  • Execution: Double 5s (total 10) = instant component kill, bypass all defense

Deck Composition

  • 6 Faction Cards - Randomly chosen from 21 available per faction
  • 10 Universal Cards - All included in every deck
  • 13-19 Equipment Cards - 2 equipment items chosen, each with 5-8 cards

  • Card-as-HP System: Deck size = health, damage removes cards to damage_pile permanently

Meta Ecology Insights

The balance journey revealed complex predator-prey relationships between factions:

Key Patterns Discovered

  1. Predator-Prey Dynamics: Nerfing Ossuarium (75.6% → 48.9%) caused Bloodlines to rise (57.8% → 73.3%) because Ossuarium was their main predator
  2. Inverse Correlations: Church ↑ = Wyrd ↓ (r = -0.82 correlation). When Church rose from 22.2% → 51.1%, Wyrd fell
  3. Meta Speed Cascades: Damage nerfs → slower battles → helps economy factions paradoxically
  4. Non-Linear Scaling: +1 damage can cause 15-25% WR swings depending on faction archetype
  5. Cascade Triggers: Changing 3+ factions simultaneously causes unpredictable meta collapses

Cascade Failures Documented

Version Changes Made Result
v5.22 5 factions changed 5/10 → 1/10 balanced (cascade)

v5.28 5 factions changed 5/10 → 1/10 balanced (overcorrection)

Why v5.29 is Final

The Limits of Balance

After 10 iterations and 4 cascade failures, v5.29 represents optimal achievable balance given the meta's inherent instability.

Failed Attempts to Improve Beyond v5.29

v5.30 attempted to fix the 3 outlier factions with minimal changes:

  • Elves: -1 equipment damage → 26.6% WR collapse (62.2% → 35.6%)
  • Crucible: +1 equipment damage → 17.8% WR spike (42.2% → 60.0%)
  • Emergent: +1 equipment damage → Worked, but cascades affected other factions

Conclusion: The meta exhibits too much sensitivity for further damage-based tuning. Even ±1 damage to 3 factions causes unpredictable cascades that destroy the 7 currently balanced factions.

Alternative Approaches for Future Work

  • Economy Rebalancing: Adjust economy card counts instead of damage (47pp WR gap correlated with economy size)
  • Matchup-Specific Balancing: Add faction-specific counters (e.g., Elves take +1 dmg from Church)
  • Mechanic Diversification: Add non-damage mechanics (armor penetration, DOT, etc.)
